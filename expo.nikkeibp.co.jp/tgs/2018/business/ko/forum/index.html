    September 20th (Thu)

    Keynote Speech

    Interpretation Available

    Location: Makuhari Messe Hall 1 Event Stage

    10:30～12:00

    Road map for e-sports to spread out as “sports”
    〜 From creative to making fans, challenges and visions the game industry should address

    e-Sports is now initiating worldwide movements. A variety of medias cover those enthusiasm and we see the increased momentum of popularization in Japan too. At the same time, since the e-Sports game tournaments are held as exhibitions and cultural programs in the large-scale sports events such as Olympics, Asian Games and National Sports Festival, potentials in e-Sports becoming popular as “sports” are receiving attention.

    In this environment, what does the game industry should work on? Hearing opinions by the people involved in real sports, we discuss challenges and visions from creatives to making fans of games.

    ［Panelists］

    Kenneth Fok　Asian Electronic Sports Federation(AESF), President

    Global Game Business Summit 2018

    Interpretation Available

    Location: Makuhari Messe Hall 1 Event Stage

    13:00～14:30

    How to win in the world for domestic games: Learn from global hit titles made-in-Japan
    --What are the latest strategies for marketing, development and promotion for overseas markets?--

    Over the recent years, Japanese games have been said to face a bigger hurdle to compete against Western games in global markets, due to the scale of development costs and the styles of games. In the past couple of years, however, game titles made in Japan are starting to show their presence, such as some games being recognized for overseas awards and proving popular among global gamers. Depending on how to compete in the global market, Japanese games still have odds to win.
    Amid the progress of multi-platforms and the growth of new entertainment such as e-Sports, what are the tactics to compete in the global game market? What will be the most important factor to win--the initial plan, the development process or the marketing strategy? The Forum will provide an opportunity to learn from the success of Japanese hit makers and explore the directions for Japanese games to move forward.

    [Panelist]

    Yosuke Saito　SQUARE ENIX “NieR:Automata” Producer

    Ryozo Tsujimoto　CAPCOM “MONSTER HUNTER: WORLD ”Producer

    Fumihiko Yasuda　Koei Tecmo Games “Nioh” Director
