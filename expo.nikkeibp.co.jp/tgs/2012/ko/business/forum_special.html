Social Game Session

Admission fee (tax included) ¥8,000 Advance
¥10,000 Day of the event
*A “Business Day Registration Ticket” will be provided

Venue:
International Conference Hall 2F,
Room 201

September 21 (Friday)1:00 P.M. – 3:00 P.M.

“Social Games, Act Two: The Outlook for the New Era”

The social game market is facing various challenges. With the start of self-regulation, companies are formulating a variety of measures and moving toward their next step. What developments will we see in social games in the future? Representatives from leading social application providers (SAP) that develop social games will discuss the world that they see in the future, and each company’s vision and strategy, in the context of the paths they have taken up to this point.

Relay Talk& Panel Discussion
Shinsuke Kawakata
President and Representative, gloops, Inc.

CEO, gumi Inc.

President, Ateam Inc.

Admission fee (tax included) ¥8,000 Advance
¥10,000 Day of the event
*A “Business Day Registration Ticket” will be provided

Venue:
International Conference Hall 3F,
Room 301

September 21 (Friday)3:15 P.M. – 5:15 P.M.

“What is the New Form of Games? New Circumstances for Game Businesses in the Network Era”

The development of networks has resulted in an increase in the number of games delivered digitally to home game consoles, personal computers, smartphones, tablets, and other devices. At the same time, a variety of business models are being developed, from sale (packaged-sale) type games to pay-per-use, item-based billing, and stock-type billing. What will the “next-generation game business,” with its close connection to content, be like? Participants will analyze the possibilities, focusing on examples of current approaches to that difficult issue.

Gamification Session

Admission fee (tax included) ¥8,000 Advance
¥10,000 Day of the event
*A “Business Day Registration Ticket” will be provided

Venue:
International Conference Hall 3F,
Room 302

September 21 (Friday)3:15 P.M. – 5:15 P.M.

“New Game Possibilities Pioneered through Gamification”

One of the keywords attracting attention recently is “gamification.” Companies are breaking down the various functions and other elements of games and working to utilize them in corporate activities, marketing, product development, etc. There has also been an increase in the need for human resources and companies that can make that concept a reality. Participants will introduce the most recent examples to explain the entire process to members of the game industry, from the fundamentals of gamification to application, focusing on what elements should receive the focus, and how they can be of use.

Relay Talk & Panel Discussion
Toru Fujimoto
Project Assistant Professor, Interfaculty Initiative in Information Studies, University of Tokyo
