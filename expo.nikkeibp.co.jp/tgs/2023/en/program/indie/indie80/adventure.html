Scene Investigators is a first-person, single-player game for fans of detective and true crime genres. Players are given several questions about each case file and required to solve them all in order to become licensed investigators. The process involves searching for clues, observing the narrative presented in the crime scenes, uncovering the perpetrators’ hidden motives, and resolving the tragedies that befell the victims.

The game's most unique feature is the requirement of both logic and intuition on the player’s part to move forward with the investigation. This is referred to as the "calculated assumption" or the “70/30 principle”, meaning that in some cases, 70% of the evidence can be found but the remaining 30% has to be assumed by the player. The intuitive ability to sense what is missing now becomes a necessity that allows the player to connect the dots and get to the bottom of it.

Can you solve it all?
