TOKYO GAME SHOW and Japan Game Awards 20th Anniversary BoothHall 3

To commemorate the 20th anniversary of the Tokyo Game Show and Japan Game Awards, we set up the “Tokyo Game Show and Japan Game Awards 20th Anniversary Booth” in Hall 3 where you can experience the past and future of games.

“Look back on 20 Years History” Corner

In the Japan Game Awards Corner, the progression of the TOKYO GAME SHOW and Japan Game Awards will be presented with all past posters. Together with that, you can look back on the 20 year history of games, one panel for a year(in cooperation with: Famitsu editors).
There is even a space below the panels for visitors to post a message. We hope that all of your memories will connect to spread the fun and appeal of games even further. Furthermore, we have provided a station for you to try out nostalgic titles that have won a Japan Game Award. We are also displaying paper boards with congratulatory messages sent from top creators for the 20th anniversary.

“The Future of Entertainment” Corner

Cooperation: JST ACCEL Embodied Media Project, Sony Computer Science Laboratories, Inc./Yamaguchi Center for Arts and Media, NIKKEI ELECTRONICS/Nikkei Technology Online

The technological evolution of games and entertainment shows no signs of stopping. With virtual reality at the center of attention at this year’s TOKYO GAME SHOW, in what form might we enjoy entertainment in five or ten years? We will introduce advanced technology current in research, and present one future vision of games and entertainment. You can actually experience a variety of technologies in research such as devices that reproduce the feeling of hard objects or liquids, 3D images you can touch, and goggles that incorporate the sights of multiple people into your own vision.

What is CEDEC?
CEDEC (Computer Entertainment Developers Conference) is the largest conference for computer entertainment developers in Japan organized by CESA (Computer Entertainment Supplier's Association), which also organizes the TOKYO GAME SHOW. The Interactive Session presents technologies related to games in a display and demonstration format rather than lecture format.
