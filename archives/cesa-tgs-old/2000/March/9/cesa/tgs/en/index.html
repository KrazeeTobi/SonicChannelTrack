The Computer Entertainment Software Association (abbreviation: CESA, Chairman: Kagemasa Kozuki) has finalized the schedule of "Tokyo Game Show 2000 Spring" which it is organizing for Mar. 31 (Fri.) (trade day), Apr. 1 (Sat.) and Apr. 2 (Sun.) as days when the show will be open to the public. The show will be open on all days from 10 AM to 5 PM.

The venue for the show will be Exhibition Halls 1 to 8 at "Makuhari Messe" (Nippon Convention Center, Mihama-ku, Chiba City).

"Tokyo Game Show" was first held in Aug. 1996, with Spring 2000 eighth time the event will be held. The show is the focus of attention as Japan's first comprehensive computer entertainment software show, and the number of people in the industry from overseas and from overseas press organizations is increasing each time the show is held. This has made it grow into a show that attracts global attention.

In the preceding "Tokyo Game Show '99 Autumn," more than 400 new titles of software were released simultaneously, attracting the attention of industry and general public alike, and helping to establish a new record of 163,866 visitors.

"Tokyo Game Show 2000 Spring," to be held in the year that marks the start of a new millennium, will be even more spectacular than previous years, and preparations are underway to ensure all visitors from throughout the world are able to fully enjoy the attractions of computer entertainment software.
