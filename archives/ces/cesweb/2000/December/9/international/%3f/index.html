International Visitors
If technology is important in your world, you need to be at CES.

2001 International CES - January 6-9, 2001, Las Vegas, Nevada, USA

We invite you to visit a consumer technology show unlike any other in the world-an international trade fair with more excitement, more innovative products and more business opportunities than any other.

Endorsed by the U.S. Department of Commerce's International Buyer Program, the 2001 International CES has been selected as the industry trade event with the most export potential and networking opportunities.

CES is the largest trade fair of consumer technology products worldwide. Come to see and touch the most amazing communication, entertainment, computer and networking products the digital world has to offer. Learn about incredible advances in technology that will lead to the next generation of products. Meet industry leaders and innovators - and perhaps your next global business partners.

Meet the people who make deals happen.

Join nearly 12,000 international visitors from more than 130 countries at CES, with a total attendance of more than 100,000 professional attendees. CES is a trade fair, a business forum, a conference with more than 100 sessions, and a place to meet the people who can make a difference in your company's future.

Whether you wish to purchase product, arrange a joint venture or licensing agreement or become a foreign agent or distributor, CES is where you'll meet all the right people. Connect with the most influential decision-makers in the consumer technology industry. More than 1,500 exhibitors are at CES.

Don't miss the most essential technology event of the year.

Visas and Invitation Letters

Visitors from qualified countries, coming to the U.S. for business or tourism for 90 days or less, may be eligible to enter the U.S. without a visa. Currently, 29 countries participate in the Visa Waiver Pilot Program: Andorra, Argentina, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brunei, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Japan, Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Monaco, The Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Portugal, San Marino, Singapore, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, the United Kingdom and Uruguay.

Find out more about the U.S. State Department's Visa Waiver Pilot Program.

We want you to have sufficient time to obtain a visa to enter the U.S. You have three simple ways to request a visa invitation letter:

  1. Online Visa Invitation Request Form
    • Attendee Form
    • Exhibitor Form

  2. Download the Visa Invitation Request Form
    • Attendee Form (PDF format)
    • Exhibitor Form (PDF format)

International Buyer Program

Due to the high export potential of the technology displayed at CES, the 2001 International CES has been selected to participate in the U.S. Department of Commerce's International Buyer Program (IBP). U.S. Foreign Commercial Service Offices work with CES to promote and coordinate delegations of buyers to CES. If you would like to join a delegation, please contact the American Embassy or Consulate General in your country.

Learn more about the International Buyer Program and get a list of U.S. Commercial Service Offices around the world.

The Commerce Department hosts the International Commerce Center (ICCs) during CES to assist you in finding U.S. companies interested in exporting to your country.

Accomplish More at CES

CES welcomes visitors from around the world. We appreciate that you travel a great distance to attend CES, and your time is extremely valuable. Many services are provided at CES to make your experience as profitable as possible.

International Commerce Center (ICC)

To ensure your CES experience is productive, CES and the U.S. Department of Commerce host the International Commerce Center, located in the Las Vegas Convention Center. The helpful, multilingual staff answers questions and directs you to complimentary refreshments, meeting rooms, workstations and global business matchmaking services. Fax machines and copiers also are available.

Interpreters at CES

Language interpreters are available in the International Commerce Center for anyone needing assistance in Chinese, French, German, Japanese or Spanish. If you wish to hire a full-time interpreter to assist you during the show, please contact CES prior to the event to ensure that arrangements can be made for your interpretation needs.

International Reception
Sponsored by
Proxima/Infocus
You are cordially invited to attend the CES International Reception on Saturday, January 6, 2001 (6:00-7:30 pm, Room N-250 of the Las Vegas Convention Center). The reception is your best opportunity to meet and network with exporting exhibitors and other international visitors. Invitations will be air mailed to attendees who register by November 3, 2000. You also may pick-up an invitation at the International Commerce Center during the show.

Free Transportation at CES

Complimentary shuttle bus service takes you from official hotels to exhibit facilities all four days of the show. Service also is available between the exhibit facilities.
