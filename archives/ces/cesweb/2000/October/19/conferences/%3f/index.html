Craig Barrett of Intel to Present Kick-Off Keynote
Craig R. Barrett, president and CEO of Intel Corporation, will deliver the Kick-Off Keynote address at the 2001 International CES. The keynote will be on Friday, January 5, 2001, the night before CES officially opens, from 7:30 - 8:30 p.m. in the Las Vegas Hilton Theater.

Barrett joined Intel in 1974 as a technology development manager and later became president in 1997 and CEO in 1998. After receiving his Bachelor of Science, Master of Science and Ph.D. degrees in Materials Science from Stanford University in Palo Alto, Calif., he joined the faculty of Stanford University and remained there until he joined Intel.

Bill Gates of Microsoft to Present Opening Keynote at the 2001 International CES

Bill Gates, chairman and chief software architect of Microsoft Corp., will deliver the Opening Keynote address at the 2001 International CES. Gates' foresight and vision regarding personal computing have been central to the success of Microsoft and the software industry. Gates is actively involved in key management and strategic decisions at Microsoft, and plays an important role in the technical development of new products. A significant portion of his time is devoted to meeting with customers and staying in contact with Microsoft employees around the world through e-mail.
