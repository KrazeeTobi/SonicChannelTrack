BILL GATES WOWS AUDIENCE AT
1998 INTERNATIONAL CES

Speaking to a jammed-pack crowd live and at several remote satellite locations at the 1998 International CES, Microsoft Chairman and CEO delivered a visionary look at the future of the consumer electronics industry. Offering CES his Executive Perspective Address, Gates' remarks focused on Microsoft's Windows CE operating system, and the exploding number of consumer products this platform supports.

"The PC is not the only tool that consumers have," Gates said. "There are many products that consumers use in their everyday lives, from TV's to pagers, from cellular phones to new devices such as our just announced PC Companion products, including the Auto PC and Palm PC." Gates also talked about Microsoft's partnership with manufacturers including Casio, Everex, LG Electronics, Palmax Technology, Philips, Samsung, and Uniden, and Clarion and Microsoft's strategic agreement with Motorola for wireless communications.

Gates' presentation wowed attendees with a humorous and entertaining look at real-life applications of future technologies, including on-stage demonstrations of state-of- the-art technology and video clips of customer testimonials.

At the conclusion of the Keynote Address, Bill Gates announced Microsoft's agreement with Tele-Communications, Inc. (TCI) under which TCI will license a version of the Microsoft Windows CE operating system for digital set-top boxes. Microsoft and TCI expect that Windows CE will be available for the advanced digital set-top devices that TCI will begin to deploy in late 1998 or early 1999.

In introducing Bill Gates, the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA) president, Gary Shapiro, said, "Our nation's continued world leadership, our phenomenal economic growth, and our rapid move to a computer literate society can be traced directly to the vision and genius of Bill Gates, the technological leadership of Microsoft partners like Andy Grove of Intel, and the creative efforts of hundreds of their hardware and software partners… Microsoft competes ferociously, but it thrives on competition and it forces its competitors to invent, innovate and compete just as ferociously. This competition moves our nation forward."
