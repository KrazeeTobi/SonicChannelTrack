"Networks are slowly losing their audiences as more channels are added to cable and satellite services and we have to assume the current trends will continue. But to me, high-definition with sports and movies and major entertainment is a way to arrest that decline…. Only the major players are going to do it. And, it's going to help us; it gives us an advantage."
- Michael H. Jordan, Chairman of Westinghouse Electric, parent company of CBS, as quoted in the New York Times

"We are absolutely committed to broadcasting HDTV."
- ABC Television Network President Preston Paddon to Senate Commerce Committee, September 17, 1997

"LIN Television station's towers have already been rebuilt or reinforced in anticipation of the high definition transition. Our transmission buildings have been readied for the high definition transmitters, and we are planning the first local transmission in Dallas/Fort Worth on March 31, 1998, for the opening game of the Texas Rangers."
- Gary Chapman, President, LIN TV (owner of network affiliated TV stations), December 15, 1997

"HDTV will be embraced by the television industry because it is the way that we can compete successfully in the new video marketplace. Most importantly, American viewers will be its greatest beneficiaries."
- Robert Dechard, A.H. Belo Corporation (owner of 16 network-affiliated TV stations), testifying on behalf of the National Association of Broadcasters before the Senate Commerce Committee, September 17, 1997

"HDTV will enhance our viewers' enjoyment of many of PBS's most popular programs…. Imagine exploring the majesty of the Grand Canyon in high resolution, crystal clear pictures, displayed on wide screen format. Or, being able to listen to the on-stage musical performance of Les Miserables in CD quality, surround sound."
- Excerpt from "PBS and DTV," on PBS website

"Customers have come to expect us to be first when new technologies come along, and we think high-definition is one of those things."
- Jeff Bewkes, Chairman of HBO, in announcing plans to begin HDTV programming in the Summer of 1998

 Broadcasters are required by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) to transition to digital in the top ten markets (reaching 30% of US households) by May of 1999, and in the top 30 markets (reaching 50% of US households) by November 1999. But, the industry has promised to have at least one digital station in each of the top ten markets by November of 1998, and it is likely that digital stations will be launched even earlier.

Network executives at ABC, NBC and CBS, and many local broadcasters have committed to provide high-definition programming when they launch the digital channels this year. Initial HDTV broadcasts on the networks will likely include prime-time programming, and local broadcasts of sporting events in HDTV are scheduled during the first quarter of 1998. PBS and its member stations are expected to make the switch to digital in 1998 with a significant amount of HDTV programming. In addition, cable operators like HBO, Discovery Channel and Turner Broadcasting have announced their commitment to provide HDTV programming in 1998, perhaps as early as this summer. It is also likely that HDTV programming will be provided by digital satellite systems in 1998.
