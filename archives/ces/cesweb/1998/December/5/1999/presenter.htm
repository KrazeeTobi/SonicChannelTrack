INTRODUCTION

The 1999 International Consumer Electronics Show® (CES) is the world's largest annual event bringing together the industry leaders with the products and technologies that will impact the consumer electronics retail market in 1999. The 1999 International CES conference program provides show attendees with the latest information about cutting-edge products, technologies, market trends, and instructional information on installation and retail management.

International CES attracts more than 90,000 attendees from all 50 states and over 100 countries. More than 76.5% of these attendees are final decision makers or have significant influence over buying decisions. CES draws over 2,900 editorial press.

 

SEMINAR FORMAT

  • Seminars are approximately one hour in length and are designed to promote the exchange of ideas among the presenters and the participants.
  • Except for training sessions, conference sessions are conducted in a moderator/panelist format. Audience participation and dynamic audio-visual support are both strongly encouraged in each seminar.

 

SEMINAR TOPICS

The 1999 International CES will provide over 80 sessions which will focus on product and technology development, economic and retailing trends and issues in consumer electronics. Sessions are moderated by members of the media, analysts or industry consultants. Panel members are selected from manufacturers, retailers or other industry members.

The Habitech Institute
Sessions will focus on home and systems integration, networking and controls. The majority of these sessions will be "training" programs and business management courses that will last from one to three hours.

  • Home Entertainment
  • Networking and Infrastructure
  • Home Controls and Systems Integration
  • Business of Systems Integration

Consumer Technology
These seminar sessions will focus on the latest developments occurring in the categories listed below. Panel members should be able to give perspective on:

  • Convergent products that are being developed in the category
  • Digital advances made in the product category
  • Delivery methods being used in the product area
  • Impact of the product or technology on the consumer market

 Web Content/Programming  Internet Boxes
 Smart Cards  Specialty Audio
 Set Top Boxes/Cable Modems

SUBMISSION PROCESS

Please have your proposal to CES by Tuesday, June 30, 1998.

  • Please include in your proposal a suggested seminar title, a 75 word description of the seminar and the names of proposed panelists (if applicable) biographies and references of previous speaking engagements. (We will not contact the proposed panelists without your knowledge.)
  • Incomplete proposals will not be accepted.

PROPOSAL EVALUATION

  • All proposals will be considered based on originality, relevance to CES and the industry, definition of focus, and the expertise of the speaker(s). You will receive notification as to
    the status of your proposal from CES by September 1.

GENERAL INFORMATION

  • All related hotel and travel expenses will be the responsibility of the presenter.
  • Upon acceptance of your proposal, the CES Industry Relations and Education Department will work with you on all logistical, operational, and promotional aspects of the seminar.
  • Speakers will be required to prepare and duplicate presentation materials (e.g., slides, handouts and topic outlines).
