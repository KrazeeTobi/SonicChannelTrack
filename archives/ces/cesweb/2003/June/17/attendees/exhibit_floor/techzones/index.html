Accessibility TechZone
LVCC, South Hall, 3-4

Without accessibility, consumer electronics—in all markets—become constrained and limited. More and more, today's technology products and services address universal design and accessibility for people with special needs, including those with visual, hearing, physical, and cognitive disabilities. At the Accessibility TechZone, see how dynamic companies conform to various legislation that works to keep access to information a fundamental human right.

What to expect
Manufacturers or suppliers of products such as:
  • supply accessible hardware
  • software
  • accessories and services
  • including digital captioning
  • telecommunications
  • wireless devices
  • audio and visual indicators
  • speech recognition technology
  • easy-to-use controls
  • TTY or hearing aid access
  • wheelchair mounts

Energy Efficiency and Electronics Recycling
LVCC, South Hall, 3-4

The past decade's explosion of consumer technology prevalence has created a whole new issue for high-tech companies and consumers—and a burgeoning market opportunity. Products that save energy, protect the environment and address the productive use of used electronics equipment come together at the ENERGY EFFICIENCY AND ELECTRONICS RECYCLING TECHZONE at the 2004 International CES. This TechZone promotes energy-efficient products and their manufacturers.

The International CES dedicates Saturday, January 10, 2004 to ENVIRONMENTAL AWARENESS DAY. Look for special conference sessions that address energy issues and the future of recycling electronics products. Help us advance the CE industry—and the future of recycling electronics products.

What to expect
  • manufacturers that produce or sell energy efficient audio/video products, computers, chips, power management devices, home monitoring devices and more
  • companies that recycle products such as computers, televisions and mobile phones
  • electronics collections agencies
  • energy and recycling industry groups
