The 2003 International CES: Setting the Standard of Success

The 2003 International CES truly lived up to the label of world's largest consumer technology tradeshow with:

116,687 attendees from 128 countries
1.25 million net square feet of exhibit space
2,283 exhibitors
Read CEA President and CEO Gary Shapiro's thoughts on the largest International CES to date.

View photos from the 2003 International CES.

Techies En Masse
Whether they came to see the latest in wireless technology, HDTV, digital audio/video, home networking and entertainment or mobile technology, thousands of people traveled to Las Vegas to witness the largest International CES in the history of the show. Read highlights from the first day of the 2003 International CES.

The Promise Of Broadband
FCC Chairman Michael Powell and CEA President Gary Shapiro engage in a candid one-on-one discussion Friday. Said Powell, "I view broadband as a national priority that holds amazing promise to stimulate the economy...."
Read more about this session and other Day 2 highlights.

Progress On All Fronts
As technology becomes better, faster, more powerful and more affordable, it also makes great strides in efficiency and recycling ability. Read about Green Saturday at CES and other newsworthy events that happened during the third day of the world's largest consumer technology show.

Show Me The Product
A few announcements from the show:
Hewlett-Packard introduced its HP Digital Media Receiver with built-in Wi-Fi wireless networking.
Sirius Satellite Radio showed off a new Kenwood car stereo that doubles as a mobile video system.
Sanyo showcased the next generation of digital displays, organic light emitting diode (OLED).
Zenith announced more than 40 digital devices, including a digital cable-ready high-definition television.
Archos introduced its modular Video A140, a 40-GB hard drive media player.
