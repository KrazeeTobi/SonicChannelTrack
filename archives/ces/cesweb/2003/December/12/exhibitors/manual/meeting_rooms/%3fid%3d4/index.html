Las Vegas Convention Center Meeting Rooms
To allow ample time for the planning of your display, and to support the Las Vegas Convention Center with the maintenance of their upgrades, we ask that you review the fire and safety guidelines below. All exhibitors, occupying LVCC meeting rooms C101 - C206, N101 - N264, S101-S118 and S201-S218 must abide by these guidelines.
All LVCC meeting room exhibit displays must have a floor plan that has been approved by the LVCC Customer Account Manager. Floor plans must be submitted 60 days in advance of set up to:
Kelly Butler Holtz
4723 SW Humphrey Court
Portland, OR 97221
Plans will then be submitted to the LVCC's Customer Account Manager for review and approval. Earlier submissions are appreciated.

LVCC Meeting Room Guidelines
Freight / Crates:
When moving freight or equipment in these areas, the existing carpet must be protected by the use of approved runners or visqueen (lightweight, plastic covering). No crates will be allowed on the upper level of the North or Central meeting rooms, nor in any South meeting rooms.
Exhibitors may ship crates to the convention center, but the shipment must be broken down and brought upstairs by either using a pushcart or by hand carrying the items. No forklifts will be allowed.
For second level South Meeting Rooms, all materials must be transported via the South Hall ramps and not via the lobby passenger elevators. Custom exhibits are not permitted in any of the South Meeting Rooms.
Prior to the show, Liz Burns with GES Exposition Services will be contacting all meeting room exhibitors to discuss this procedure so as to ensure a smooth transition on-site.

Electrical:
Electrical service is limited to installed plugs/Crouse-HInds show power receptacles in North, Central and South Meeting Rooms (60 amps per room). Existing electrical service is limited to installed outlets in each room.
To utilize these outlets, exhibitors should order service and labor from TSE Electrical. TSE Electrical order forms and pricing are available from the section labeled Electrical.
Questions can be directed to Kelly Butler Holtz at (503) 296-9321 or via e-mail at kbholtz@juno.com; or Barbara Alcaraz with the LVCC at (702) 892-2924 or balcaraz@lvcva.com. Exhibitors will be responsible for ordering electrical service and labor, if necessary.

Floor Plan Approval:
All persons who are in the process of designing exhibits or displays for these meeting rooms are encouraged to contact the LVCC's Customer Account Manager for clarification of all rules and regulations. They may contact Barbara Alcaraz, LVCC Customer Service Manager, at (702) 892-2941 or via e-mail at balcaraz@lvcva.com.

Fire Prevention Equipment:
Fire extinguishers, fire hose cabinets, fire alarm pull stations, and exit signage must not be blocked and remain visible and identified. Smoke detectors/alarms must be connected, tested and audible outside the room. Sufficient walk space must be provided within the display. Exits must not be blocked.

Wall Units:
When constructing any wall units, care must be used not to obstruct any of the HVAC (Heating/Ventilation/Air Conditioning) controls, light controls, electrical outlets, cable TV outlets or sound outlets. Walls must be 18-inches below Automatic Fire Sprinkler Heads.

Ceilings:
No structures erected in these rooms may have any type of ceiling. All walls must be 18-inches below Automatic Fire Sprinkler Heads.

Flooring:
No carpet or flooring may be installed on top of the existing carpet.

Signage:
No signs, banners, trussing, lights, etc. may be hung from the ceilings or the support structures above the ceilings. All truss and support must be floor standing.

Exterior Room Signage:
Identity signs and/or exhibit overlays set flush to the wall directly outside the room must have prior written approval from Show Management. Approved signage and overlays may not extend more than two feet into the corridor, and must be parallel to the room's permanent outside walls. Displays of any kind may not be set up in corridors outside meeting rooms. Any exterior signage must be incorporated into your design.

Rigging:
Rigging, hanging signs and affixing signs to the existing permanent walls is not permitted.

Property Damage:
At all times due care must be used to protect the property of the LVCVA from any damage. Nothing may be adhered to or affixed to the interior/exterior walls, doors or floors. Any damages are the sole responsibility of the exhibiting company.

Storage Closets:
Existing storage closets and utility rooms are not included for exhibitor use or access with any meeting room.

Room Partitions:
No meeting room wall partitions will be moved without LVCC personnel present.

Keys:
Keys to each LVCC meeting room will be held in the International CES security office in rooms C-105. There is a $20.00 deposit per key. The deposit will be refunded at the end of the show when the keys are returned. All keys must be signed in and out through the security office. The keys are the sole responsibility of the exhibiting company. Although exhibitors maintain responsibility of the keys, exhibitors should still plan to secure valuables, as LVCC in-house services may also have access to these rooms

Las Vegas Hilton Meeting Rooms
Exhibitors in permanent meeting rooms in the Las Vegas Hilton must note the following:
  • Fire extinguishers, fire hose cabinets, fire alarm pull stations, and exit signage must not be blocked and remain visible and identified.
  • Smoke detectors/alarms must be connected, tested and audible outside the room.
  • Sufficient walk space must be provided within the display.
  • Exits must not be blocked.
  • Nothing may be adhered to or affixed to the interior/exterior walls or floors. Any damages are the sole responsibility of the exhibiting company.
  • Additional electrical is prohibited. Exhibitors must use existing electrical only.
  • Displays of any kind may not be set up in corridors outside meeting rooms.
  • Identity signs and/or exhibit overlays set flush to the wall directly outside the room must have prior written approval from Show Management. Approved signage and overlays may not extend more than two feet into the corridor, and must be parallel to the room's permanent outside walls.
Please refer to the Rules and Regulations section for additional information regarding Hilton meeting rooms.

Demonstration Rooms (D-Rooms)
The purpose of a D-Room or acoustic sound room is to allow audio and home theater exhibitors the opportunity to rent a prefabricated package for demonstration of their products. Each D-Room comes pre-packaged with a set amount of electrical, lighting, and ventilation for the exhibitor's convenience.
Packages offered are as follows:
  • 3.5m x 5m = 11.48' x 16.40'
    Includes: 1 door, 1 fan, 1 electrical outlet (1500 watts), 4 lights on 2 ceiling tracks
  • 5m x 7m = 16.40' x 22.97'
    Includes: 1 door, 2 fans, 2 electrical outlets (1500 watts each), 8 lights on 4 ceiling tracks
  • 7m x 10m = 22.97' x 32.81'
    Includes up to: 2 doors, 4 fans, 4 electrical outlets (1500 watts each), 16 lights on 8 ceiling tracks

Plus:
  • One (1) smoke detector
  • One (1) fire detector
  • One (1) door with lock
  • One (1) 2000 watt duplex electrical outlet
  • NOTE: All interior and exterior walls are a neutral color. Graphics may be used on the interior and exterior walls. Carpet is not included, but may be ordered at a special price to include installation and removal.
IMPORTANT: Project management fees apply to all structural modifications. Exhibitors will not receive credit for items included in their package(s), but not utilized within that package.
Each exhibitor is responsible for approving their D-Room layout (i.e.: interior walls, removal of walls, and plexiglass panel placement).
Your completed order and final floor plans are due to GES Exposition Services by November 14, 2003. Orders submitted or altered after this deadline will be subject to additional charges.
A GES Representative will be contacting each D-Room exhibitor to discuss material handling arrangements.
Electrical order forms including placement for outlet(s), fan(s), lights, and door(s) included in the D-Room package can be found in the Show Utilities section. Exhibitors may also use this form to order additional equipment and/or make changes to the structural layout of the room within the guidelines below.

D-Room Guidelines
  • Exhibitors may not make any modifications to the structure of the 3.5m x 5m D-Rooms.
  • Any D-Rooms larger than 1000 square feet must have a fire sprinkler system and a 10' aisle drawn into the D-Room design.
  • Using plexiglass alters the sound absorbing qualities of the demonstration rooms. D-Rooms using plexiglass must adhere to the 85 dB sound rules. NOTE: When demonstrating audio equipment within an enclosed demonstration room, position subwoofers away from walls that abut neighboring exhibitors. Sonic vibration nuisance complaints will be acted upon by Show Management; if situations are not rectified by the offending party, Show Management reserves the right to shut down exhibits.
  • Signage may be placed on the exterior walls of the D-Rooms to a maximum height of 8 feet. Signage is not permitted on top of any D-Rooms smaller than 7m x 10m. Any requests for hanging signs over D-Rooms must adhere to hanging sign regulations in the International CES Exhibitor manual, and must be coordinated through GES.

Exhibitor One-Day Meeting Rooms
One-Day Meeting Rooms are only available at the Las Vegas Hilton or Rivera Hotel. Meeting space is extremely limited and is only available to official CES exhibitors looking for one-day functions and/or meetings.
Download the One-Day Exhibitor Meeting Room Application.
  • Meeting space will be assigned on a first-come, first-serve basis and will be charged $200 per individual room used (under 4 hours) or $400 for the day (over 4 hours). This is an application fee only and does not cover any applicable charges for room rental, room set, audio/visual, or catering services.
  • After submitting your Application, a confirmation and invoice outlining your space assignment will be sent to your attention. PAYMENT MUST BE REMITTED BY THE DECEMBER 5 DEADLINE OR YOUR MEETING ROOM WILL BE RELEASED AND CANCELLED.
  • Friday, December 5, 2003 is the last day you may cancel your meeting space and receive a full refund. Cancellations made after this date will not be refunded. All cancellations must be submitted in writing to the International CES Operations Department.
  • Once your space is assigned, a hotel representative will contact you to discuss your specific room set requirements.
  • If we do not have space to accommodate your request, our meetings manager will be in touch with you to offer assistance in finding function space at other official International CES hotel properties.
  • If you are interested in function space at another hotel, we can either 1) assist you in making contact with the sales department at the properties you are interested in, or 2) forward you a list of official International CES hotels and contact names.
