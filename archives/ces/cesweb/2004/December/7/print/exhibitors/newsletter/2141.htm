    Intellectual Property Reminder for the 2005 International CES
    We realize most consumer technology professionals can recite the dos and don’ts of copyright, patents and trademarking in their sleep, but we’d be remiss if we didn’t just give you a few brief reminders.

    We’ll save the legal speak for your International CES Space Contract, but here’s a recap:

    1. If you are using anything that is copyrighted, patented or trademarked in your exhibit,
      please make sure you’re free and clear to use it (by way of royalties, fees or consent). As an exhibitor, you are responsible for taking care of these royalties and fees, and you would also be responsible for the fines that may be incurred if you don’t.
    2. If you own the intellectual property you’re promoting at CES, you have nothing to worry about. If you don’t, we might be put in a position where we could be forced to make you remove displays, shut down your booth or even cancel your space. We’d like to ask you a huge favor: Please don’t make us do that.
    3. If your products use technology from another company (as many tech products do), please get your licensing ducks in a row to avoid legal action by that company.

    We will monitor the show floor, along with companies looking for misuse of their intellectual property, and we will require exhibitors not complying with the rules to remove the unlicensed products and problematic materials. Failure to comply with CES staff direction in this regard may result in sanctions up to and including your booth being closed down.

    Additionally, CES will compile a list of companies displaying unlicensed products at the 2005 International CES; repeat violations in future years may result in further sanctions against your company.

    The continued success of CES as the premier showcase for consumer electronics innovation carries with it an obligation to respect and protect the intellectual property rights of technology owners. We take this obligation very seriously, and we look forward to working with you to ensure the integrity of our event. Thanks everyone.

    Questions? Please contact bellis@CE.org.

    A Message From Dan Cole


    Dan Cole
    Vice President, Exhibitor Services, Sales and Business Development, International CES

    Three Tips To Make Your Four Days a Five-star Experience
    by Dan Cole

    There are a few things at this point in time I realize: One, you’re busier prepping for CES this month than you’ve likely been all year. And two: A few of the no-bones-about-‘em reminders I’m about to give you also may appear in some shape or form in this newsletter.

    But we’re down to the count, and of all the things in the world I want for each exhibitor, it’s for you to have a five-star experience this January in Las Vegas. With a month to go, I’ll save the analogies and allegories and cut to three critical things you need to do or know right now:

    1. Register your staff now. I just took a look at exhibitor registration numbers, and it seems this is a chore many of you are leaving to the last minute. Take my sage advice: Reserving a few minutes to get this off of your to-do list will undoubtedly save valuable time on-site. We’re trying to make it as easy as possible for you; this year, we’re offering group registration. You can upload a spreadsheet of names rather than registering each person one-by-one. If you register by early December, you still might receive the badges in the mail. Otherwise, you can pick them up on-site.
    2. Next up is another marketing must. Now’s the time that the other 120,000 CES folks are starting to pick their short-list of must-see exhibitors. And it should be you. We have pre-show registration lists available for rent for your pre-show marketing efforts. This database can be tailored to fit your specific target market. E-mail Wendy Hudak at byb@CE.org to learn about this.
    3. And although your head is spinning with 2005 CES details, there’s a very important packet coming your way that details all you need to know about choosing your 2006 exhibit space. Don’t let this slip to the bottom of your stack; you need this information in January. Look at it now, and pack it with you when you hop your flight to Vegas. If you have any questions about 2006 space selection before January 6, e-mail your account representative.

    Okay—You’ve got three great tasks—and great people to assist you. You should be on your way to a five-star experience. See you soon—during what I hope will be the four best days of your business year.


    Dan Cole
