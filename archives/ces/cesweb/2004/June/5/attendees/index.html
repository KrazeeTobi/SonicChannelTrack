INTERNATIONAL CES BREAKS ALL RECORDS
The International CES spans 1.3 million square feet and brings together:
  • 130,000+ industry professionals
  • 2,400+ exhibiting companies
  • 18,000 international travelers from 110 countries


2004 International CES highlights
The numbers are in, and the 2004 International CES continues to establish itself as a driving force for the consumer technology industry. Read the details in the official press releases, including:
  • Sprint CEO Gary Forsee says anywhere-anytime access to voice and data will drive consumer technology in '04. Forsee joined other keynoters such as HP's Carly Fiorina at this year's show. [Read transcripts.]
  • CEA President Shapiro says U.S. consumer technology sales should top $100 billion this year. [More CES news.]
  • Home networking technology takes center stage as more homeowners demand access to content and home control features anywhere, anytime.
  • Samsung touts the world's largest LCD HDTV offering full 1920 by 1080 progressive scan resolution. [More product announcements.]
  • Industry Insiders Series draws big names such as Dell—and calls tough issues to attention. [Read transcripts.]
  • FCC Chairman Michael Powell talks about regulatory issues related to unlicensed spectrum and VoIP and says, "Government has to prove that regulation is necessary rather than making innovators prove that it isn't."
  • Display technology mantra: thin, flat, big and plasma. IT companies such as Dell and Epson round out manufacturers.
  • Digital imaging market enjoys largest presence on the floor in history, including Flash Forward TechZone. [See other show floor details.]
  • Two common CES threads: portability and wireless technology. Seamless connectivity reigns supreme.
  • TechTV's Best of CES top ranking goes to Denon's NS-S100 Network Multimedia Server. [See other awards.]
