International CES Highlights Digital Technology
World's Largest Technology Event Showcases the Products that Facilitate Consumers' Active Lives

More than 122,000 industry professionals from 120 countries came to Las Vegas this week to see the cutting edge in digital technology at the 2001 International CES. The world's largest annual technology tradeshow, featuring 1.2 million net square feet of exhibit space, wrapped up today after four days of conference sessions, keynote speeches and exhibits of the latest developments in consumer electronics technology.

Technology Spotlight: In-Car Electronics

 The mobile electronics industry continues to attract consumers with innovative entertainment and information products for the car.

Digital audio, video and communications products allow consumers to travel with all of the lifestyle and workstyle conveniences they have at home. DVD mobile theater systems for rear seat passengers, MP3 players and satellite radio systems provide quality entertainment options for the mobile consumer. Hands free phones, PDA docking stations and wireless networks of your personal devices keep you productive during your commute while voice recognition products that obey spoken commands, telematics systems that automatically summon help in an emergency and navigation systems that provide directions while you drive, protect your safety on the road.
