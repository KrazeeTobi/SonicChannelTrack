EXHIBITOR AREA

General Information
The 2001 International CES delivered technology-savvy attendees, and from more than 125 countries, and markets than any other consumer technology event in the world. Exhibitors estimated an average of $1.5 million each in sales revenue generated from leads at the 2000 International CES, where attendees represented $72.1 billion overall buying power, 89 percent of all retail purchase decision-making. With more than 1,800 exhibitors filling in more than 1.2 million net-feet of exhibit space, the 2001 International CES was a global media mecca.

Maximum Press Coverage

2001 International CES: Mega Media Event of the New Millenium

CES Exhibitors get lots of coverage and publicity! More media attended the 2000 International CES than the Super Bowl, and we expect the final count for the 2001 show to be another year of fabulous press coverage.

More than 3,600 journalists came to the 2000 International CES! The audited media attendance for the 2000 show increased by 13 percent from 1999, and editorial press attendance grew by 35 percent. Prepare your press kits and materials for electronic and video format! Nearly half of the journalists that attend CES are with broadcasting or online media outlets. Reach out to journalists beyond the print world.

Press attendance counts for the 2001 International CES are being finalized and will be made public soon.

Here's the press breakdown from the 2000 show:

Media Outlet % of Total Media Attendance
Print Media 51%
Online/Wire Media 27%
TV/Cable/Radio/Broadcast Media 22%

Source: 2000 ABC Expomark Audit

Eighty percent of the media stories about the 2000 CES covered products that were displayed at the show, listing most products and manufacturers by name. More than 60 percent of the articles were devoted to exhibited products. See your efforts pay off in more ways than you imagined!

Record Attendance and Key Audiences

2000 International CES Hit The Mark With Record Attendance Numbers In Key Categories

Pre-registration statistics two weeks before the 2001 show were up by 14%, compared to last year's numbers.

The International CES is one of the few large-scale trade shows that offers an independent, third-party verification of trade show attendees and attendee demographic information. We appreciate your patience while we wait for the Audit Bureau of Circulations (ABC) Expomark to count and verify the 2001 International CES counts.

The 2000 show had more than 110,000 attendees, more than 27 percent of which represented presidents/CEOs/owners. CES attendees' buying power has grown in step with the consumer electronics industry. The 2000 show attendees represented $72.1 billion overall buying power, which is 89 percent of all retail purchasing decision-making.

The number of financial/market analysts at the 2000 International CES grew by 58% over 1999 audited figures, with more than 1,100 in attendance.

Benefits and Services

CES provides our exhibitors with the best benefits and services:

  • Customer tickets (buyer invitations)
  • Exhibitor newsletters and fax updates
  • Directory listing in the official Show Directory, in paper and online versions
  • Pre-Show Planner, with potential editorial coverage
  • Shuttle bus service to/from hotels and between facilities
  • Nightly booth vacuuming
  • Sign-hanging assistance (Booths 400 nsf or larger receive one hour of sign hanging labor.)
  • Electrical assistance (Booths under 400 nsf receive one hour of electrical labor.)
  • Booth ID, including signs on all aisles surrounding booth, adhered to carpet
  • Exhibitor boothmanship seminar (day before show opens)
  • Press room (Editorial press receive exhibitor press kits and may utilize work stations.)
  • Press conference rooms
  • VIP luncheon (by invitation only, principally for CEA members and VIPs)
  • CEA member lounge/business office
  • International lounge, registration and translation services
  • International reception and networking for international attendees and exhibitors
  • International dinner, by invitation only
  • Keynote session passes
  • Innovations, the prestigious CES awards program
  • Internet stations
