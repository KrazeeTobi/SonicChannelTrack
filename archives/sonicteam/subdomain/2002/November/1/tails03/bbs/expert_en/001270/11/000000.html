It pains me so to have to write these words, to tell my tale. Yet, I must find a way to get the thoughts, the memories of my encounters with my double, out of my head. I can only hope with all my soul that telling someone will stop the horrible dreams and reminiscing. Within these pages I fill with the memories of my encounters with my doppelganger. After my first encounter with this evil demon, I began carrying a handgun with me, just to feel safe. Of course, I knew that this would never help me if I ran into it again, but it did give me a sense of security. My double haunted my dreams, thoughts, and speech. I was denied the privilege of a dreamless sleep many times, so eventually this forced me back to the mansion where I discovered my demon. As I neared the gates, I paused and looked up at the mansion. It was fairly large, with a large tower jutting out from the middle of the building. I often longed to enter the mansion, and on this day I succumbed to this urge. Boldly I thrust open the main doors, staring down the dusty hallway. I paused, thinking about how this reminded me of a dream I had. Disregarding this, I moved further in, the doors slamming behind me. This was definitely not going to turn out well...

Sighing, I began to walk further down the hall, my eyes passing across the many, many paintings that hung upon the wall. Paintings of people, of landscapes, of houses. I stopped to admire many a painting, until I found that I had walked quite far into the mansion. I shrugged it off, stopping to admire another painting. I felt something pass behind me, and I whirled around, drawing my gun and firing a shot at what I saw. Unfortunatly for me, I had just shot at a mirror. Not a smart thing to do. The sound of shattering glass filled the hall and I barely managed to shield my eyes in time as I was bombarded with broken glass. I winced as some of it pierced the skin on my arms. I spent the next painful half hour digging shards of glass out of my arms, and another half hour trying to figure out why I had been so stupid and what I felt pass behind me. Once I finished this, I continued on my way, knowing that I was beind watched and followed but not really caring.

What caused my unnecassary braveness, I doubt I'll ever know. With that braveness came great stupidity. Such stupidity that at one point I turned around and actually confronted the doppelganger, who had indeed been following me. Of course, had I know that I would have been knocked unconscious and chained to a wall again, I wouldn't have done that. In fact, I wouldn't have gone in the mansion at all. I sighed as I awoke, hanging on the wall again, I noticed my gun was gone. Wow, not only is it a violent evil demon, it's a SMART evil demon. How lucky am I? Now the only thing for me to do was watch and wait. Hanging in the darkness, I began to hear something. Something that sounded like dripping. The kind of annoying dripping you hear when you've got a leaky faucet. As my eyes began to adjust to the darkness, the room began to get brighter, and also I began hearing a small splashing sound accompanying the dripping. Eventually the room was bright enough for me to see normally, and I scanned the room. I froze when I found what had been causing the dripping noise, and screamed at the top of my lungs soon after. For across from me in the room hung a body, drenched in it's own blood, hideous wounds covering it, blood dripping off it and down into a little puddle on the floor. If I thought about anything at this point, it was if I was going to suffer the same fate as my cellmate.

I spent the next few hours crying out of fear, sometimes screaming in frustration. My wrists ached from the continuous strain put on them by the chains, and I was getting sick from looking at, or rather, not looking at, the corpse than hung across from me.
