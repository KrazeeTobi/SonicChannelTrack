If you think that insult was devastating you really haven't got a clue. No one insult is so genius, so perfect, that it can't easily be diffused or even reflected with a witty retort. If there's one thing you learn about insults when you start to understand the workings of the human mind is that they aren't universal. Insults need to be tailor made for the person you want to offend, because they're only effective if the person lets them be. To really be effective you must know your opponents weaknesses and insecurities, for it's only when you find those vital cracks in their armor that those insults do anything whatsoever. In cyberspace this is much harder, because you can't see what they look like and therefore can't tell, for example, whether fat jokes are likely to embarass them, because you can't tell whether they have extra girth to be embarassed about. You either need to flail blindly or be very clever in finding some other, less obvious clue to pick at.
