Mountain rise and ocean tide, Forest grow and winged glide;

Windy blow and prairies spread, River run and shadow fled.

Rise, rise, you warming sun,

To spread your light to days begun.

Thunder roar and rain will fall, Nightly storm come, freezing all;

Moonshine glow and fissures show, Magma singe and shadow grow.

Rise, rise, you stormy sun,

To help the changes yet undone.

Sentience breeds and now takes root, Covers nature in his suit;

Creatures creep and work concerted, Towns appear, shadow asserted.

Rise, rise, you elder sun,

To watch how this new world is run.

Race divides and gripped by strife, Bringing hate and end of life;

People lead themselves to death, Shadow takes his first real breath.

Rise, rise, you blood red sun,

To see the gore of battles won.
