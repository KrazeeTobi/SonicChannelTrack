It was morning now. I slowly opened my eyes, and saw that only Dante, Gonu, Atri, Aniyu, and Kaya were awake. Dante was talking with Aniyu. "I heard you say something about a pasture?" He said. "It is the most beautiful place there is, Dante." Replied Aniyu. "It's actually more of a valley, but there's so much green there that we call it a pasture. But the hard job now is just getting there." "And," Said Kaya. "We're being driven unmercifally." "By who?" Asked Gonu. "Kerchak," Replied Aniyu. "The herd's head honcho." "We can hardly keep up." Whimpered Kaya. "For an older dragon like myself, well, it's just indecent." "Well, tell him." Said Dante. "What's the worst he can do?"

No sooner had he said it, three dragons pushed passed us. I had to get up to avoid being stepped on. The dragons were the black one we had seen before, the green one that was always walking with him, and a dark pink one that we had never seen before. "Hey!" Said Gonu as they pushed pass. "What's their problem?!" "That's him." Said Kaya. "Kerchak." "Excuse me, Kerchak!" Said Dante, running up to Kerchak. "Got a second?" Kerchak and the green dragon whirled around. "Get lost, kid." said the green one. "Relax, Zimbarii." Said Kerchak. "Who are you?" He asked Gonu. "Doragonu, but I prefer to be called Gonu." Replied Doragonu. "I was back here, talking to these guys." He pointed to Aniyu and Kaya. "They're having a bit of trouble keeping up. so maybe you could slow it down a bit?" Kerchak laughed a bit. "Let the weak set the pace? Now there's an idea. Better let me do the thinking from now on, Gonu." He turned and walked away, Zimbarii and the dark pink dragon following him. The pink one turned around and said, "Don't worry. That's how my brother treats newcomers... No matter how charming they are." Then she turned and followed Kerchak and Zimbarii.

Atriiyu walked up to Gonu. "Gonu sure knows how to catch a girl's eye!" She laughed. "I wouldn't be catching NOBODY's eye if I were you." Said Aniyu. "Especially not Vixanie's. You just keep your head down and mind what Kerchak tells you." "Since when do we take orders from him?!" Cried Dante. "I could hold that jerk's brain in the palm of my hand!" "No, Dante, she's right." I said, noticing the small dull red dragons following us. "Better to keep our heads down with this bunch then get them bitten off by... those things." "Well," Said Kaya. "Just consider yourselves lucky that's ALL that's following us."

Elsewhere, two giant dark purple dragons with sharp teeth caught the scent of the herd. they saw our footprints, and began tracking us down.
