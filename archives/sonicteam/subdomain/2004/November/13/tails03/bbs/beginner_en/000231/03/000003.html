"Listen, I only said that to get your attention. Now then, about your dislike of the giraffes..."
"Oh, all right." they said simultaneously, resuming their activity.
"Get off!"
"Looking for the perfect gift? Try looking in a dumpster."
"YOUR COUSIN WATCHES EDUCATIONAL TELEVISION!!!" Swazi shouted in revived desperation. They receased their activities and restared at him rereproachfully.
"Were you shouting that to get our attention as well?" the escapee retorted flatly.
"Rebellion! Rebellion! Rebellion! Giraffes! Mr. Cheese! Trash can! Three years!" He shouted insanely, flailing his arms about uselessly. The escapee looked up at Quack, still steadfastly anchoring him down by the ankle.
"Did you get any of that?" he asked him doubtfully.
"No." was his reply.
"Neither did I. It just sort of went underneath the neck, if you know what I'm saying."
"Will you idiots form a rebellion with me? Please? I've been needing one for the past three years, in which I have spent the time gnawing on old tires in a trash can." The two of them considered this thoughtfully, or at least, one of them did. Indeed, they were idiots, and indeed, they could tell that he had been in a trash can gnawing on tires for three years. What the heck?
"Certainly, lad. I'd be glad to rebel. What exactly are we rebelling against?"
"Mr. Cheese and his dominion of giraffes." More thoughtful/less consideration.
"Certainly, lad. I'd be glad to rebel. What exactly are we rebelling against?" Quack repeated, not entirely certain he had heard the reply correctly. Swazi shook his head.
"Who are you?"
"Allow me to introduce myself. I am Quarkle Urkin Arklin Carmen Kaiser." he said suavely, extending his hand. Swazi stared benightedly at the hand.
"Right, but who are you?"
"Ahem, well. Yes. Just... Just call me Quack, all right?"
"Most people don't realize something is missing, or rather, being ignored, until it is brought to their attention." stated the escapee matter-of-factly.
"I guess you can come, too," Swazi sighed, staring down at Quack's ankle, "I see you've already made a friend."
"I believe he is amorously infatuated with my ankle." said Quack.
"And what shall I address you by, O one who cuts off the circulation to ankles?"
"Thank you kindly, Gifford, but I'm allergic to shellfish."
"Er, right. We'll call you Rocklobster.
"My name's Swazi. Swazi Land."
"What shall we call our little force?" Quack inquired, "We need a hearty name. One that can be remembered. One that will state our cause, whatever that may be... One that says 'Hey! We're rebels! Get me a sandwich!'"
"How about 'Fred'?" suggested Rocklobster.
"No. Not that sort of name." replied Quack.
"Harold?"
"Uhh... I meant more along the lines of a concept or organization, not a person."
"Wal-mart?"
"That's already been taken. We need to compose one."
"McFredward's House of European Tacos?"
"All right, now you're just being stupid. Think more about concepts and stop, well, being stupid."
"How about 'Tax'? Taxes are concepts."
"If I may interject... SwaziLand." interjected Swazi.
"Do what now?" do-whated Quack.
"SwaziLand!"
"Swaziland doesn't exist anymore. It was dissolved with all the other countries."
"No. No. I meant that as a name. SwaziLand. It's a combination of my first and last name. How's that? Eh? Eh? I think it fits, seeing as how I organized this rebellion."
"Hmmm..." hmmm-ed Quack, "You have a point there, Mr. Land. I was sort of leaning towards Tax, but SwaziLand works, too."
"And what about you?" asked Swazi, looking down at Rocklobster, who was now sucking on Quack's ankle, "What do you think of the name SwaziLand?"
"I don't think, Gifford. I don't think."
"Then it's settled. We are now the SwaziLand Anti-Cheese 'n Giraffe Battalion Legion Force Resistance Movement. SwaziLand, for short."
