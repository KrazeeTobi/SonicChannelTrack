And so the beginning of the rebellion was formed. In celebration, the three ran to the nearest roadside, hijacked a jeep as it was driving by, tossed the driver into a lake, spray-painted the phrase "Giraffes have long necks" on the exterior wall of a footwear store and later threw large rocks at that same store's windows.

Swazi spent hours upon hours during the night erecting a three-second, crudely crafted, yet still legible, incomprehensibly unambiguous and attention drawing sign that read "SWAZILAND HEADQUARTERS" in the park. He had chosen the location of their resistance movement's headquarters and, ironically, it was centered around the trash can he loathed so much.
"All right. Our first tactical meeting," stated Swazi, as he headed towards the podium, which was actually the trash can, "All in favor, say 'aye'." Quack stared benightedly at him.
"Er...?" began Quack.
"No, 'aye'." corrected Swazi.
"Uhh..."
"'Aye'"
"Wha?"
"'AYE!!'"
"What about my eye?"
"It's about to be gouged out, if you don't get it right!"
"Get what right?"
"Aye."
"Yes. I have two of those."
"Aye. Not eye."
"You're speaking in riddles."
"ARGHH!!!"
"You haven't proposed anything yet, Gifford." honked Rocklobster. Swazi's face reddened.
"Oh, uh, right. I say, we rebel against the giraffes! All in favor, say 'aye'!"
"We already decided on that, Gifford. We already decided on that."
"All in favor of saying aye, say 'aye'."
No response.
"All in favor of saying aye, say 'aye'!"
Still no response.
"Say aye, you miserable misbegotten insects! Say aye!!"
"We're not in favor, Gifford." Rocklobster pointed out.
"Well, I want a joyous chorus of 'ayes' to burst forth and toss me backward, so, all- regardless of whether or not you're in favor- say 'aye'!" Rocklobster yelled 'toothpick' and Quack, still benighted, produced a pathetic excuse of an attempt to say 'aye.'
"Ah... Oh.. Hrrm..." mumbled Swazi, stunned by what he just realized, "I'm stunned. You know what I just realized? It hit me like a ton of potato shavings." He sighed and turned his back to his rather confused and dwindling audience. "There's only three members of SwaziLand. Only three members. It couldn't be four, it couldn't be two. Three. I hate the number three! It's so... Three-like!"
"Not to mention that we're far outnumbered by the giraffes." stated Quack, as his mind dipped itself in apprehension and snapped his being back to reality. "To rectify this dilemma, I propose we gather more members."
"Wonderful! Brilliant! Suave! Why didn't I think of that?" He shot them a dark look. "Don't answer that.
"All in favor, say 'aye'."
"Aye."
"Ham sandwich."
"Close enough. So, we're off to gather more members, are we? This'll prove interesting. And non-three-like."
And so they left their base of operations completely obvious and open to attack to gather more members.

"What's the problem? What's the problem!? Sir, I suggest you take a good look at that receipt." barked Llama. The cashier studied it with such ferocity that his eyes nearly caused the receipt to burst into flames.
"All right, now, what's the problem?" he asked again. Llama banged his hands down upon the countertop.
"You can't even see it? And it's your job to do stuff like this? How much do they pay you here?"
