"This is McLardy's. Frothy Octopi 'n Stuff is across the street." Llama glanced around the facility, taking special note of all the enormous signs, banners, plaques, torches, laser shows, and stock market ratings that spelled out "Welcome to McLardy's" in ridiculously bold print.
"Oh. Erm, well, uh..." rationalized Llama.
"Fries or tater tots?"
Suddenly, Rocklobster flung himself through a giant pane of glass next to the entrance door, which just happened to be wide open.
"I didn't know there was a 'Q' in 'McLardy's.'" he proclaimed, holding up one of the letters that had previously been encased in the window. Swazi shattered through the other window next to the wide-open entrance door.
"A lot of people don't know that," he replied, "McLardy's is actually spelled M-C-L-Q-R-D-Y-'-S. I don't know. It's French, or something. Anyway, MCLQRDY'S is a bit hard to pronounce, so they renamed it McLardy's."
"Gifford... You know things.... Are you a duck?" inquired Rocklobster.
"Wh-what?"
"A duck."
"N-no... What sort of vile demon possessed you and made you ask that?"
"Ducks work for the government. And the government knows things."
"Hello? What's this? Have you not noticed that the world is an anarchy!? WE HAVE NO GOVERNMENT!! JUST A BUNCH OF HONKING GIRAFFES AND SOME SUPREME DAIRY PRODUCT!!!"
"Tune in next week to hear a snail slowly devour itself in someone's wet slipper." broadcasted Rocklobster.
"Have you people ever heard of a convenient little invention called a 'door?'" inquired Quack as he walked towards the wide-open entrance door, "It's really quite handy."
"NOOOOO!!! QUACK!!! GET BACK!!!" shouted Swazi, his eyes nearly leaping from their sockets and his arms moving so fast that the word "washer" became visible in their wake. Quack ceased going towards the door, glanced about nervously and with a bit of ignorance, then slowly backed up. Swazi collapsed with relief, then cursed, because he fell in glass.
"Why shouldn't I use the door?" Quack asked, his voice slightly trembling. As if to conveniently demonstrate the answer to his question, two random salespersons shoved Quack aside and headed for the entrance. One was carrying a burnt orange briefcase, which is pertinent to the storyline, I assure you. They walked through the door. Upon reaching the interior of McLardy's, they immediately noticed that something had gone terribly wrong.
"Fred..." whimpered the suitcase-less salesperson, "You're turning into a penguin. Stop it."
"I'm afraid I can't honor your request, Nigel," stated Fred, the salesperson with the burnt orange suitcase, "as your head seems to be receding into your chest."
"Ack! I can't control my arms anymore!" exclaimed Nigel, "And I'm pretty certain that they aren't supposed to bend and stretch that way, either." Nigel's arms flailed about like a flimsy tape ruler and turned purple in coloration. Unexpectedly, they shot outward in an extending fashion, broke out an unbroken window near the entrance, deliberately avoiding the broken ones, uprooted a lamppost thirty feet away and lambasted it upon an ice cream truck that just happened to be driving by. By now, his head was fully inside of his chest, and a foot was growing out of his stomach.
"Howl howl howl grunt howl howl grunt" explained Fred, who was now a full-fledged penguin with a minor throat mutation, "grunt howl gurgle howl howl crack snap whistle howl grunt grunt howl howl grunt." A frying pan materialized over penguin Fred's head and made good use of the law of gravity.
"Fred," shouted Nigel's second head, "Fred. Am I supposed to be this yellow? How many of me do you see?" There were about forty-eight of him prancing about the place, doing a wide variety of assorted fruitless tasks.
"Make it stop! Make it stop!" pleaded Quack, tearing his clothes, "I can't bear to stomach any more of this traumatizing display of horrific transmogrified salesmen!! Make it stop!"
