Kinugasa Research Organization, Ritsumeikan University

Jh Shin
Kinugasa Research Organization, Ritsumeikan University

Jh Shin

Shin is a postdoctoral researcher at Ritsumeikan University, Kyoto, Japan. She obtained her Ph.D. in 2020 at the same institution. She specializes in serious games and serious gaming approaches from a socio-cultural perspective, focusing on Japan and South Korea. Shin published "Questioning the Social Acceptance of Serious Games" (Fukumura Shuppan) in February 2024. Her work explores how games can make our world a better place. Shin also participates in projects at the Ritsumeikan Center for Game Studies (RCGS).
NPO OcuFes

Takeshi TAKAHASHI
NPO OcuFes

Takeshi TAKAHASHI

TAKAHASHI has joined Koei Co., Ltd. in 1998. Engaged in "Shin Sangoku Musou 1-4". In 2008, transferred to Creatures Co., Ltd. and worked as a director of "Pokepark". In 2013, we acquired Oculus Rift DK1 with a kick starter and started the VR software development and VR software presentation OcuFes (now Japan VR Fest). In 2014, became independent as a VR specialist. In the same year, OcuFes became an NPO corporation. Since then, he has been actively working for the spread of VR in Japan and VR developers in the world.
Chairman of Fukuoka Indie Game Association

Koji MURAKAMI
Chairman of Fukuoka Indie Game Association

Koji MURAKAMI

Koji Murakami first encountered indie games at the age of 39, when he was an architect, and began creating games in earnest. So far, he has created games such as "Dark Blood" and "Keribato!" and other games with beautiful dot-picture designs that are conscious of the good old days of the 90s. In 2022, he will launch the Fukuoka Indie Game Association to support indie game creators through game events and study groups.
In 2020, he will establish Asobuild.LLC. With the philosophy of "creating a fun world through play," he develops games and operates Metaverse. His hobbies are bonfires and cooking.

Juan Gril

Juan Gril

For the past 25 years, Juan Gril has worked in Games, Interactive Media, and Augmented Reality. Juan has served different roles: programmer, game designer, producer. He founded his own game studio (Joju Games), and ran it successfully for 12 years.
Over the course of Juan's career, he has been part of teams that launched various AR applications, websites, and he has produced and designed more than 50 games. Among others, he has designed games for the following companies: Dreamworks, Disney, Jam City, Nickelodeon, Universal, 505 Games, Atari, GSN, MTV, and Comedy Central.
Videogame Ninja: Trusiga

Ramon Nafria
Videogame Ninja: Trusiga

Ramon Nafria

In 1992, Ramon realized at the age of 12 when he started playing videogames that it was the best medium to express himself. Since 1999, he has contributed to Spanish media such as Onez, Ociojoven, Anaitgames, Eurogamer, Videoshock and Vandal and produced games since 2003 in companies like Nerlaska, Gameloft, Digital Legends, Abylight, U Play, Blit Software and A Crowd of Monsters. He has been giving lectures about games at multiple universities, organizes Valencia Indie Summit, and the last game he has worked in is Alex Kidd in Miracle World Dx, the remake of the classical game from SEGA.
