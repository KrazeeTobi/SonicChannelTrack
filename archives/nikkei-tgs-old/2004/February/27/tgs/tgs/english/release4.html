TOKYO GAME SHOW 2003 REPORT
This year’s theme-- “A Playful Spirit Can Change the World”
A big success with about 150,000 visitors over 3 days--exceeding last year!

TOKYO GAME SHOW 2003 was held from September 26 (Fri) through 28 (Sun) under the theme “A Playful Spirit Can Change the World.” The organizer was Computer Entertainment Supplier’s Association <CESA>, and the co-organizer was Nikkei Business Publications, Inc. The show was very well attended with a total 150,089 visitors over 3 days.

A total 111 exhibitors--the highest number ever--joined this 13th Tokyo Game Show. Game software companies, game software development tool companies, and game colleges from Korea, Taiwan, the U.S. and Japan exhibited more than 500 new video game software and game-related peripherals.

Online games and mobile phone game, which was well over last year’s show, drew a lot of attention in the venue as well as a wide variety of new game software including Family, Action, and Role-Playing games. Expanded “Kids’ Area” and “Table Game Stadium” also enjoyed big popularity. Kids Play Area where kids can play analog games was created in the Kids’ Area. At the Table Game Stadium, a corner of digital board games was newly established.

The next Tokyo Game Show is scheduled to be held from September 24 (Fri) through 26 (Sun), 2004.
