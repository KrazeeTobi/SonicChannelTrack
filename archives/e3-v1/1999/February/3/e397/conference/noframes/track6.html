Track 6: Financing New Media Track
developed in association with: Coopers & Lybrand

This track takes you through the crucial steps of funding a new media company. Industry experts teach you how to survive in the competitive search for funding as well as how to find and evaluate the best deals for your company.

Thursday, June 19 10:30-11:45 am
Seeking Early Stage Financing: How to Build a Business Model that Will Attract Investors
This panel of financing gurus will detail the components of a solid business plan that will generate interest from the investor community. They'll address topics such as business plan do's and don'ts and the alternative financing options available to new media companies. They'll also answer questions such as, how much money does your company really need?, when should I raise money? and which financing source makes the most sense for me?
Kim Davis, Associate, BankAmerica Ventures; Jon Goodman, Director, Distance Learning and Director of EC2, The Annenberg Incubator Project USC MM Incubators; Phillip Merrick, President and CEO, webMethods; David Zhu, CEO, Eurythmica; Jeff Weiss, Picture Network International
Moderator: Robert Stavers, Partner, Internet Services Group, Coopers & Lybrand,
Thursday, June 19 1:00-2:15 pm
The Who, What, When and Why of the Venture Capital Stage
What are venture capitalists investing in? How can you get their attention? What do venture capitalists look for in a company and what should you look for in a potential investor? How much equity should you give up for how much money? This panel will answer these questions and give insider information on negotiation tactics and strategies for dealing with venture capitalists.
Tod Francis, General Partner, Trinity Ventures; Alex Gove, Senior Editor, Red Herring; Warren Martin; Tom Vertin, Senior Vice President, Northern California Technology Division, Silicon Valley Bank; Paul Matteucci, Mpath Interactive
Friday, June 20 10:30-11:45 am
Partnering in the New Media Industry
Today's success formula often involves a strategic partner or a merger and acquisition. This panel will discuss partnering pros and cons, deal negotiation tactics, buyer and seller positions in M&A deals, and management integration. Come and listen to the panelists' advice and experiences and evaluate whether your company can benefit from the current trends in partnering strategies.
Michael Hudes, President, Organic Online Inc.; Tom Hyland, Felice Kincannon, Communicade; Stephen J. O'Leary, Managing Director, Broadview Associates; Isabel Hoffmann, President and CEO, I. Hoffmann & Associates; Eric Bidell, Parable Corp.
Friday, June 20 1:00-2:15 pm
Exit Stage: New Media Companies Going Public - A Whole New World
Experts from companies who have recently gone public are joined by savvy professionals to discuss their experience and offer advice. What are the pros and cons of going public? What are the steps to going public? What is life like after going public? Don't miss this opportunity to hear from those who've been there.
Massoud Entekhabi, Marketing Managing Partner, Coopers & Lybrand L.L.P.; Brian Farrell, CEO, THQ, Inc.; Ariella Lehler, President/CEO, Legacy Software J. Neil Weintraut, Partner, Twenty-First Century Internet Venture Partners; John Wilczak, Founder and CEO, MetaTools;
Friday, June 20 2:45-4:00 pm
Intellectual Capital: Compensation Strategies Through All Stages of A New Media Company's Life Cycle
Digital media companies, whether emerging or mature, are faced with the dual forces of cash flow pressures and the increasingly competitive wages of key technical talent. Attracting and retaining vital knowledge workers can pose many challenges at each stage of a company's development. Learn the compensation strategies of successful new media companies as they move through their life cycles.
Bashker Biswas, Director, Human Resource Advisory, Coopers & Lybrand; Irene Wong, Vice President, Human Resources, Seer Technologies, Inc.; Ken Bauer, Xircom; Dan Eastman, Innovative HR; Joe Heery, Cyborg Technologies
