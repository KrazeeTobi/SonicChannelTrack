About The Interactive Digital Software Association

Charter:
Formed in April 1994, the Interactive Digital Software Association (IDSA) is the only trade association which exclusively serves the business and public affairs needs of interactive entertainment software publishers in the $4.5 billion U.S. computer and video game industry. IDSA members include 43 of the world's leading publishers of interactive entertainment software, representing 75% of the total U.S. entertainment software market.

Current Membership:
Acclaim Entertainment, Accolade, Berkeley Systems, Bethesda Softworks, BMG Interactive Entertainment, Broderbund Software, Capcom, Capitol Multimedia, Crystal Dynamics, Discovery Channel Multimedia, Disney Interactive, Electronic Arts, Fox Interactive, GT Interactive Software, The IBM Multimedia Studio, Interactive Magic, Interplay Productions, JVC Musical Industries, Konami, LucasArts Entertainment Company, Mattel Media, Maxis, MGM Interactive, Microsoft, Midway Home Entertainment, Mindscape, Namco Hometek, Nintendo of America, Ocean of America, Panasonic Interactive Media, Philips Media, Sega of America, Sony Computer Entertainment, SouthPeak Interactive, Spectrum Holobyte, TerraGlyph Interactive Studios, THQ, Total Entertainment Network (TEN), The 3DO Company, Ubi Soft, Universal Interactive Studios, Virgin Interactive, and Wanderlust Interactive.

Services Include:

  • Presenting E3, the World's Only Trade Show Focused on Interactive Content Every year, the IDSA presents the highly acclaimed Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3), the world's largest trade show dedicated exclusively to the interactive content industry. Wholly owned by the IDSA, E3 attracts tens of thousands of industry professionals from around the world to see the latest in interactive entertainment software and related products, and to participate in three days of thought-provoking sessions and seminars. This year, E3/Atlanta will be held from June 19 to 21, 1997 at the Georgia World Congress Center & Georgia Dome.

  • Serving as a Resource for Key Industry Information and Commentary

    The IDSA provides:

    • Timely information on issues shaping the computer game, video game, and interactive entertainment software industry, as well as background about related market trends, including where the industry is headed, current trends, key public policy issues, copyright and piracy issues, etc.;
    • Current facts and figures on the PC and video game industry, e.g. size of industry, key players, growth rate, consumer usage information;
    • Expert commentary on interactive entertainment industry events as they occur; and
    • Information about what's new and hot in the industry.

  • Providing the Nation's Pre-eminent Entertainment Software Rating System
    The Entertainment Software Ratings Board (ESRB), created by the IDSA, is the nation's pre-eminent entertainment software rating system for all platforms. As of December 1996, the ESRB has rated over 2,500 titles submitted by more than 160 of the industry's leading publishers.

  • Conducting a Worldwide Anti-Piracy Program
    The IDSA's Anti-Piracy Program is designed to combat entertainment software piracy both in the U.S. and around the world. Worldwide piracy is estimated to have cost the U.S. entertainment software industry $2.5 billion in 1995. IDSA anti-piracy efforts include working closely with government agencies such as United States Trade Representative, the United States Customs Service, the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), and foreign government officials. The IDSA also conducts direct anti-piracy investigative and enforcement activities.

  • Working with Government on Policy Issues
    The IDSA works with government at all levels to make the voice of its members heard on a wide range of crucial legislative and public policy issues ranging from copyright to First Amendment protection and Internet regulation.

Location:
The IDSA is based in Washington, D.C.
