Track 4: Trends In On-Line Entertainment
developed in association with: ISA

Many say the future of on-line games depends on the development of a community between game players and companies. With this new opportunity and challenge, both developers and publishers are struggling to find out how to make on-line games successful and profitable.

Thursday, June 19 1:00-2:15 pm
On-line Distribution Strategies
This panel explores the various distribution systems for multi-player games that operate through the Internet and on-line services. We'll discuss the differences in game design and technology, business models for on-line services Internet game platforms, and go-it-alone strategies.
Greg Harper, Vice President, Business Development, Total Entertainment Network (TEN); Lawrence Schick, General Manager, Games, America Online; Brad Sobel, Director of Online Games, EarthLink Network; Erick Hachenburg, Vice President, Business and Legal Affairs, TEN; Mike Moniz, President, VR·1; Adam Waalkes, Lead Product Planner, Microsoft Internet Gaming Zone
Moderator: Mike Yocco, Associate Analyst, Paul Kagan Associates
Thursday, June 19 2:45-4:00 pm
On-line Marketing
This session explores what needs to go into building a positive image for your site: selecting public relations and marketing firms, developing channels, producing promotions, cultivating relationships, branding, developing advertising content and more.
Mike Hanley, Director of Audience Development, SpotMedia Communications; Erik Lundberg, Director of Sales and Interactive Marketing, TEN; Holly Newman, Vice President, Marketing, GT Interactive; Don Keene, Director of Mplayer Marketing, Mpath Interactive
Moderator: Julie Schwerin, President, InfoTech, Inc.
Friday, June 20 10:30-11:45 am
Designing On-line Games
What's important in creating multi-player games? Is design basically the same for LAN and stand-alone titles or are there fundamental differences?
Eric Goldberg, President, Crossover Technologies; Dani Bunten Berry, President, Ozark Softscape, Inc.; Chris Roberts, CEO, Digital Anvil; Gordon Walton, General Manager, Kesmai Studios, Inc.
Moderator: Ben Calica, Director, Creative Development and Marketing, ThinkFish Productions, Inc.
Friday, June 20 1:00-2:15 pm
On-line Platforms and Development Tools
So, you want to make an on-line game. Should you make a stand-alone game and provide Internet hooks? Build from the ground up for Net-only play? And what are the protocols and APIs that are most profitable to support? What are the mistakes to avoid, and the must-have technologies? In this moderated Q&A session, top executives from leading multi-player services, technology companies and developers debate the technical strategies for producing the best on-line gaming experiences.
Brian Apgar, COO, Mpath Interactive; Jay Cotton, President, Kali, Inc.; Robert Huntley, President and CEO, IVS Corp.; John Taylor, President, ARIES Games; Dean A. DeBiase, President and CEO, The Imagination Network
Moderator: Jon Epstein, President, SpotMedia Communications
Friday, June 20 2:45-4:00 pm
Analysts Predict the Future of On-line Gaming
Where is the on-line game market going? Will it kill off stand-alone titles? A group of top industry analysts give their opinions and predictions.
David Cole, President, DFC Intelligence; Mark Mooradian, Group Director, Consumer Content, Jupiter Communications;
Moderator: Alice Hill, Editor, Gamecenter
