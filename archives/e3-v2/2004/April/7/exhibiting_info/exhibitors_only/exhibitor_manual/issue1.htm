ESA (Entertainment Software Association)
The Interactive Digital Software Association (IDSA) has changed its name to the Entertainment Software Association (ESA). “More than half of Americans derive hundreds of hours of entertainment from the software they play on their PCs, their video game consoles, their cell phones, and on the Internet”, said ESA President Douglas Lowenstein. “The members of the ESA are the companies creating and publishing this entertainment software and our new name is a more clear and direct statement defining the exciting industry we represent.” The association website, can now be found at www.theESA.com

    CALLING INTERESTED SPEAKERS 

The E3 Conference Program has always provided attendees with a unique perspective on the interactive entertainment industry. From focusing on trends taking place in Asia and Europe, to presenting developers and publishers on the front lines of persistent world gaming, to encouraging discussion and debate among individuals creating entertainment applications for mobile and handheld game platforms, the conference program is designed to showcase new business models and give the industry’s most creative thinkers a forum to discuss their goals and methods. The 2004 program will build on that tradition. This year’s program will celebrate the industry’s transition from fringe player to core member of the entertainment community, explore the expanding alliance between the game industry and the film and music industries, and look at new trends in marketing and cross-promotion.

    SOUND POLICY 

A maximum level of 85dbs will be permitted on the exhibit floor. This policy has been ratified by the E3 Trade Show Committee and will be strictly enforced. Any exhibitor found in violation of this ordinance will receive ONE verbal warning. If a second violation occurs, the exhibitor will receive ONE written warning. If a third violation occurs, power will be disconnected for the remainder of the show and 50% of the exhibitor’s priority points will be deducted. To avoid any on - site issues, all speaker/sound systems must be directed towards the center of the booth. There will be a sound check conducted by show management on Tuesday May 11 from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM to ensure sound level compliance.

    CHARACTER OF EXHIBITS 

Products or services to be exhibited are limited to software/content for computer and gaming consoles and accessories; virtual reality products; online gaming; multimedia products; associated publications and trade associations. Material, including live models, and/or conduct that is sexually explicit, including nudity, are prohibited on the Show floor and at any access points to the Show. Nudity, partial nudity and sexually explicit materials are prohibited on the Show floor and all common areas. ESA, in its sole discretion, will determine whether material is acceptable.
