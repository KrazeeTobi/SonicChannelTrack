    showNets, LLC TO OFFER WIRELESS INTERNET ACCESS AT E3 THIS YEAR.

Internet access at E3 will be brought to you by showNets, LLC again this year. Not only will you have the high-speed Internet options that you have had in previous years, but you will also have a wireless option this year.

There will be two different wireless networks for the show. The first network is a "Free Hot spot" in one or two locations (to be determined) outside of the exhibit area. These hot spots will be limited to 75 users at one time in designated areas. Remember: The Free Hot Spots are for attendee and exhibitor access outside the exhibit halls only.

The other wireless network will be accessible on a Cost Basis in the Halls for exhibitors with either a wireless laptop or a PDA. The billing for this service will be done automatically when you log on with your browser in the exhibit hall. Please keep in mind that because of the size and height of the booths and the elaborate light rigging in the rafters, the quality of the signal may be affected in certain areas of the hall. This service is meant for web surfing and e-mail. If you are going to be demonstrating Internet gaming, we recommend the "cabled drop" on the show network or an individual dedicated circuit.

An announcement from showNets with pricing options will be sent via e-mail in February. For more information or to order Internet services on-line, go to: www.shownets.net and select Order.

    BIOTERRORISM ACT OF 2002 

The Congress of the United States passed the Bioterrorism Act of 2002 to secure and protect the food and beverage supply of the United States. Certain provisions apply to all parties to the processing, manufacturing, distribution, storing and sale of food and beverage products for human or animal consumption within the United States regardless of their location.

Here is some information relative to import of food and beverage products for use at tradeshows and exhibitions in the United States.

  1. Shipments sent to tradeshows or exhibitions are not exempt from the provisions of this Act.
  2. Food and beverages are defined as anything to be consumed by humans or animals. This does include alcoholic beverages.
  3. The provisions of this law apply even for shipments that contain small amounts of food or beverage products no matter how insignificant including candy, mints, coffee, tea, sugar bags, etc. that are typically packed by exhibitors for use on their booth or to give away.
  4. Unless the product an exhibitor intends to show at a tradefair or exhibition in the U.S. is a food or beverage product, Rogers Worldwide advises exhibitor to avoid shipping any sort of food or beverage product.
  5. Individual tradeshows or exhibitions must register with the FDA if it’s a food and/or beverage show.
  6. Exhibitors are responsible for any fines, penalties, liquidated damages and other costs associated with this law and related regulations.

This information was provided to us by Rogers Worldwide, the official freight forwarder of E3 2004 and is just a summary; complete information will be sent to you in a separate cover.
