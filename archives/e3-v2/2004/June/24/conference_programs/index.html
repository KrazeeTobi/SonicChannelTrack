The E3 Conference Program has always provided attendees with a unique perspective on the interactive entertainment industry. From focusing on trends taking place in Asia and Europe, to presenting developers and publishers on the front lines of persistent world gaming, to encouraging discussion and debate among individuals creating entertainment applications for mobile and handheld game platforms, the conference program is designed to showcase new business models and give the industry's most creative thinkers a forum to discuss their goals and methods.

The 2004 program will build on that tradition. This year's program will celebrate the industry's increasingly important role in the entertainment industry, explore the expanding alliance between the game industry and the film and music industries, and look at new trends in marketing and cross-promotion.

If you are in the business of funding, creating, packaging, or promoting interactive entertainment, the E3 Conference Program will start you on a path toward reaching, and exceeding, your goals.

And this year's program will offer attendees more value than ever. The program will increase the number of lunches during the opening day of workshops from one to three, and will also increase the number of opportunities to attend sessions by two.

We look forward to seeing you in Los Angeles next May!



E3 is a trade event and is not open to the general public. No one under the age of 18 will be admitted, including infants.
