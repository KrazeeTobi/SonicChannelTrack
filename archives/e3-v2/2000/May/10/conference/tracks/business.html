Business Trends:
The Future, 2.0

Each day seems to brings convergence cloaser as we approach an all-entertainment-all-the-time wired world. How do you decide where to go next? Will your licenses take you to the big dance with the Media Giants…and endless revenue streams? Maybe, but all the deals in the world won't matter if your products aren't on shelves. Hear first-hand how titles are selected and stocked by the biggest distributers and Retailers. We'll show success stories that answer all that and more. Plus: how to make yourself a Media Giant.

Sessions:

5.1 - Expanding and Leveraging Licenses
10:00 a.m. - 11:30 a.m.
Thursday, May 11

Now, you've built a character that will live forever -- how do you leverage this asset to the greater glory of your bottom line? Learn how to get the most from your character without saturating the market.

Moderator: James Mammarella, License Magazine
Panelists: Lisa Paulson, Electronic Arts; Rob Felton, Wizard Entertainment; Christine Finch, Todd McFarlane Productions, Inc.; Pam Newton, Viacom Consumer Products

5.2 - Ready. Get Set. Launch!
12:30 p.m. - 2:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

With sky-high development and marketing costs and downward pressure on price points, "also ran" games can put a company out of business. Learn what it takes to bring idea, design, production, marketing, and distribution all together to make a game into a huge hit. A panel of senior industry executives explores issues such as market timing, product quality, features, budgeting and more.

Moderator: Gil Winters, Entertaining Ideas
Panelists: Holly Newman, Crave Entertainment; Steve Crane, Activision; Mark Hood, Sierra; Trip Hawkins, 3DO; Amy Boylan, Mattel Media

5.3 - What Makes A Good License?
2:30 p.m. - 4:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

More and more of the top sellers are based on licenses -- from Advanced Dungeons & Dragons to WCW. Our panel of experts -- some of whom specialize in licenses, others who work only with original intellectual properties -- will explore what makes a good license. Is it worth risking big investments to develop proprietary intellectual properties when recognizable licenses can offer immediate and massive sales? Join the debate!

Moderator: Aaron Loeb, Daily Radar.com
Panelists: Germaine Goia, THQ; Peter Tamte, Bungie Software; Greg Zeschuk, Bioware; Chris Taylor, Gas Powered Games

5.4 - Collaborative Design: Are Two Heads Better than One?
9:00 a.m. - 10:30 a.m.
Friday, May 12

With the increasing complexity of game projects it can be overwhelming to have a single person responsible for all aspects of design. Yet the concept of "design by committee" is anathema to many. This panel will bring together designers and project leaders who have had very successful games to their credit in which the design responsibilities were shared. We will examine when collaborative design works and when it fails.

Moderator: Noah Falstein, The Inspiracy
Panelists: Sid Meier, Firaxis Games; Tim Schafer, Game Designer; Ken Birdwell, Valve Software; Brad McQuaid, Verant

5.5 - Getting Down to Business: Pricing & Margins
11:00 a.m. - 12:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

Learn the how's and why's of software pricing from a panel of experts including retailers, software manufacturing, sales executives, and distributors. Discover how prices are set, from wholesale to retail. Learn when prices should drop, and by how much, and when to bundle pack.

Moderator: Roger Lanctot, PC Data
Panelists: Arra Yerganian, Infogrames, NA; Scott Mortenson, KBGear Interactive; Bill Gardner, CAPCOM; Dave Dresden, Lucas Learning

5.6 - Reaching a Global Market: Obstacles and Solutions
2:00 p.m. - 3:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

Are the cultural, technical, marketing and regulatory barriers that prevent the creation of a single global market coming down or getting higher? To what extent can these barriers be avoided or overcome most effectively? What steps can the industry take collectively in order to bring down these barriers?

Moderator: Paul Gardner, Osborne Clarke OWA
Panelists: Demis Hassibis, Elixir Studios; Hermann Acilles, VUD Germany; David Gardner, Electronic Arts Europe; Chris Meredith, 3dfx
