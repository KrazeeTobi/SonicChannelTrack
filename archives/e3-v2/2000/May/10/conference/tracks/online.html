Wired and Online:
All the Time and Everywhere We Go

The foundation has been built. Now it's time to expand the market to fill the bandwidth. Are we inexorably heading to an online world -- if so, what will your role be? How are communities built and find out exactly what users want today and what they might want tomorrow. Learn successes directly from the pros that have built a thriving world. Soon, e-commerce will be everywhere. Are you ready?

Sessions:

2.1 - Building a Successful Online Community
10:00 a.m. - 11:30 a.m.
Thursday, May 11

Learn from a panel of developers who have already overcome many of the problems encountered in creating an on-line community. Discover if there's an audience for your on-line vision before you spend millions on development. Learn techniques for challenging experienced users without overwhelming newcomers and ensuring that players around the world, all using different hardware, receive the same play experience. Determine how to turn a profit without pricing yourself out of a following.

Moderator: Trent Ward, IGN PC
Panelists: John Taylor, Kesmai; Gordon Walton, Origin Systems; John Smedley, Verant; Scott Herrington, Turbine Games

2.2 - How Can E-Commerce Change Your Company?
12:30 p.m. - 2:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

A panel of CEOs examines the impact of e-commerce on electronic entertainment companies. Learn how they developed successful business models, strategic alliances, and other partnerships that optimize the value of their companies. The panel also explores how e-commerce can facilitate the start-up of new entertainment companies as well as re-define existing entertainment companies.

Moderator: Mariana Danilovic, Digital Media Incubator
Panelists: Rick Bender, American Film Institute; David Wertheimer, WireBreak.com; Olivier Zitoun, Eveo.com; Kevin Wendel, iFilm

2.3 - From an RPG World to a Persistent World?
2:30 p.m. - 4:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

Will the concept of a virtual reality be trumped by multiple virtual worlds? Explore scenarios for the future of on-line virtual communities -- consistent identities for users across different games and platforms, massively multiplayer games, and the evolution of web-based communities into virtual communities. Discussion will include the implications and possibilities of using one avatar in different virtual worlds.

Moderator: Scott Herrington, Turbine Games
Panelists: Graeme Devine, id Software; Mike Sellers, Maxis; Alan Patmore, Surreal Software

2.4 - Building Online Games
9:00 a.m. - 10:30 a.m.
Friday, May 12

This session helps you understand the unique perspective and needs of on-line gamers. Learn how to target the most avid players and gain insights you can use to develop the next wave of online games.

Moderator: William Harms, CNET Gamecenter.com
Panelists: Gary Carlin, Hasbro; Temple Fennell, BoxerJam; Robert Gehorsam, Sony; Johnny Wilson, Wizards of the Coast

2.5 - Broadband Homes: Where we are, where we're going and how it may just change everything!
11:00 a.m. - 12:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

Broadband access in the home is finally beginning to happen. This panel of content providers discusses how soon we can expect to see a critical mass of broadband homes and what this will mean to the future of interactive entertainment.

Moderator: Erick Hachenberg, pogo.com
Panelists: Richard Gingras, Excite @home; Ross Rubin, Jupiter Communications; John Blakely, VR-1; Rick Boyce, Snowball; Bret Bottger, Intel Corp.

2.6 - More Than Checkers?
2:00 p.m. - 3:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

Is online gaming getting more complex than most users want? Learn creative ways to respond to the demand for opportunities to play classic games -- like checkers -- against a live opponent. Explore what users really want from an on-line community perspective.

Moderator: Vince Broady, ZDNet/Gamespot
Panelists: Justin Hall, Gamers.com; Ray Miller, Shockwave.com; Steve Kane, Lycos/Gamesville; Mark Blecher, EA.com
