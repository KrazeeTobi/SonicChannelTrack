Development:
From Garage to Million Dollar Decisions

Development issues have never been so critical and decisions so cost-intensive. You cannot afford to miss out on what Game Gods and Masters are thinking. What alternative OS's and platforms will drive the next generation markets? What are the technologies and skill-sets will you need to survive and thrive? Learn the success stories and how they approached issues affecting development.

Sessions:

1.1 - Marketing vs. Developers
10:00 a.m. - 11:30 a.m.
Thursday, May 11

In this mass-market world, who will win the on-going battle of Marketing vs. Development? Listen to leading developers and marketers discuss their priorities and frustrations in creating and launching new games. Explore the ramifications of different decisions by each group, and hear ways the battle can be turned into an alliance.

Moderator: Mike Wilson, Gathering Of Developers
Panelists: Alex Garden, Relic; Tom Mustaine, Ritual; Chris Mike, Konami; Dusty Welch, Activision; Scott McDaniel, The 3DO Company

1.2 - Linux vs. Java . . . Which Should be the Internet Gaming Platform?
12:30 p.m. - 2:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

As we move into the post-PC centric era, the lines are blurring between desktop computers, PDA's, and gaming platforms. Linux and Java are now positioned to become pervasive on a range of different devices for accessing the Internet. This panel will debate the merits of Linux and Java as Internet gaming platforms, and the development environments required to support them.

Moderator: Michael Tiemann, RedHat
Panelists: David Taylor, Transmeta; Bernd Kreimeier, Loki Entertainment; John Leech, SGI; Matt Shea, Go2Net

1.3 - Graphics & Animation
2:30 p.m. - 4:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

With each passing generation of computing power, the realism and complexity of our gaming and on-line worlds move closer to reality and in some cases, hyper-reality. Explore the technologies looming on the horizon and what they mean for programming and game play.

Moderator: Chris Hecker, Game Developer Magazine
Panelists: Robert Huebner, Nihilistic; Jay Stelly, Valve; Jim Spoto, Electronic Arts

1.4 - Next Generation Development
9:00 a.m. - 10:30 a.m.
Friday, May 12

As we sprint towards a supercomputer standard for game machines, development is requiring longer (and larger) investments in time and technology. Is a 24-month development cycle inevitable, or can smart planning and tools enable you to deliver the experience consumers want in a profitable time-frame? Plus: just what do consumers expect from next-generation games and systems?

Moderator: Chris Charla, Next Generation Magazine
Panelists: Phil Harrison, Sony; Ed Fries, Microsoft; Greg Thomas, Sega

1.5 - Handheld Gaming: 2000 and Beyond
11:00 a.m. - 12:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

There's only been one major player for the last ten years; now two new companies look to challenge the throne. Who will entertain handheld gamers for the next ten years, and how? Explore the future of handheld gaming--where we're going next and how we'll get there.

Moderator: Vince Matthews, Imagine Media
Panelists: Craig Harris, IGN.Pocket; Mike Mika, Digital Eclipse; Jim Merrick, Nintendo; Darryl Williams, SNK

1.6 - Development Lessons from the Best
2:00 p.m. - 3:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

This panel investigates the recipe for developing a best-selling game, as told by developers with consistently successful track records. Learn how developers structure their creative teams, business, finances, marketing and management arms to succeed.

Moderator: Dan Huebner, Game Developer/Gamasutra.com
Panelists: Louis Castle, Westwood Studios; Matt Pritchard, Ensemble Studios; Jason Rubin, Naughty Dog
