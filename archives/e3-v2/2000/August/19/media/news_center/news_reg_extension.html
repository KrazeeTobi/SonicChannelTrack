MEDIA ADVISORY

Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3) Extends Online Pre-Registration to April 14

WHAT:E3 2000, the world's largest trade event exclusively dedicated to showcasing interactive entertainment and educational software and related products, has extended online pre-registration to April 14. Media, Expo and conference attendees may register at www.e3expo.com.
 
Please note:E3 2000 is a trade event, and it is not open to the general public. No one under 18 will be admitted, including infants. This policy is strictly enforced.
 
WHEN:Extended Online Pre-Registration Deadline Extension: April 14
Exhibit Dates: May 11 - May 13
Workshop and Conference Dates: May 10 - May 12
 
WHERE:Los Angeles Convention Center
 
WHY:Online E3 2000 attendees who register online save up to $50 by signing up before April 14. Themed "Beyond Imagination" for its first show of the millennium, E3 attendees will meet the most influential people in the interactive entertainment industry, discover trends, experience groundbreaking new technologies, and get a sneak peek at next generation titles and products for computers, video game consoles and the Internet.
 
WHO:
  • Leading interactive entertainment professionals from around the world including developers, publishers, retailers, distributors and industry executives
  • Members of the international financial community including investment bankers, venture capitalists, analysts and financiers
  • Worldwide electronic, print and broadcast media
 
GENERAL REGISTRATION: Pre-registration for E3 2000 is available for industry professionals by calling (800) 315-1133 until April 7 or at www.e3expo.com until April 14.
 
MEDIA REGISTRATION: Members of the media may register at the online Media Center at www.e3expo.com. Required credentials for a media pass, as outlined on the site, must be faxed or mailed before the application is considered complete. Questions? Call the E3 2000 Media Hotline at (310) 967-2946 or send an email to e3expo@cohnwolfe.com.
 
WHO OWNS E3 2000:E3 2000 is wholly owned by the Interactive Digital Software Association (IDSA), the U.S. association exclusively dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of companies that publish games for video game consoles, personal computers and the Internet.
