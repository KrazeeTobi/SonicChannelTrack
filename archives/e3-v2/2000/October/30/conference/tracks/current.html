A Buzz in the Air:
The Issues

Learn to deal with complex policy and business issues facing our industry. Hear from experts and industry leaders about the issues we are faced with. Learn firsthand the secrets of recruiting the talent you need to build your team in the changing marketplace, and what tradeoffs (if any exist) in developing for a "mass-market" vs. the core market.

Sessions:

3.1 - Is Single Player Gaming Dead?
10:00 a.m. - 11:30 a.m.
Thursday, May 11

With companies moving their hit franchises into online-only worlds, some would say that single player gaming is slowly fading away. Yet, plenty of developers are still cranking out AAA single player titles. Is multiplayer gaming truly is the wave of the future, or just another passing fad? Hear some of the world's most respected and outspoken designers debate where the future of gaming is headed and judge for yourself. Gain the insights you need to place -- and hedge -- your bets!

Moderator: Blake Fischer, Next Generation Magazine
Panelists: Brad McQuaid, Verant Interactive; Warren Spector, Ion Storm; Gabe Newell, Valve, LLC; Chris Roberts, Digital Anvil

3.2 - Recruiting -- and Keeping -- Talent
12:30 p.m. - 2:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

How do you create an environment that attracts talent, fosters creativity and stimulates huge growth? Learn the secrets of building a great development team from scratch, as well as how to retain and motivate the talent you've already got.

Moderator: Susan Lewis, YUMMY.com
Panelists: Rusty Rueff, Electronic Arts; Robert Huebner, Nihilistic Software; Mary-Margaret Ipser, Mary-Margaret.com Recruiting Services

3.3 - Understanding Game Design
2:30 p.m. - 4:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

Digital games have been around for 25 years, but game designers still have trouble talking about what it is they do. Game design seems to be lacking a critical discourse that bridges theory and practice, as contrasted with other design fields like graphic design or architecture. Is a general understanding of games really possible? If so, what would it look like? And how could it assist game development? The panelists in this session are game designers who in some way have begun to answer these questions. Their pioneering attempts to explore and define the field of game design shed light on the industry as a whole and point towards new directions for digital gaming.

Moderator: Eric Zimmerman, Independent Game Designer
Panelists: Marc LeBlanc, Looking Glass Studios; Doug Church, Game Designer; Gabe Newell, Valve LLC; Warren Spector, Ion Storm

3.4 - Does Mass Market Mean Lower Quality?
9:00 a.m. - 10:30 a.m.
Friday, May 12

Are the needs of hardcore gamers and mass-market gamers totally different? Can mass market games also be first-rate games? Learn the pitfalls of developing for just one segment of the market and discover techniques for appealing to gamers of all types, casual to hardcore.

Moderator: Nolan Bushnell, uWink.com
Panelists: Nick Rush, pogo.com; Frank Ballouz, Agora Interactive; Stan Bunger, KRON-TV; Al Alcorn, Alcorn Consulting

3.5 - Piracy and the Protection of Intellectual Properties
11:00 a.m. - 12:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

Piracy remains one of the most significant business challenges facing the entertainment software industry. Listen and learn as top industry experts talk about the problem here, abroad, and on the Internet, and share with you tips and strategies to protect your intellectual property.

Moderator: Frederic Hirsch, IDSA
Panelists: Jodi Daugherty, Nintendo; Riley Russell, Sony; Eric Roeder, Havas

3.6 - The Next Big Thing? Business Risks of Integrating New Technology
2:00 p.m. - 3:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

The decision to invest in innovation opens the door to stunning success as well as spectacular failure. Nowhere is the pressure to deliver ahead of the technical curve more intense than the world of PC hardware. New products must be flawlessly conceived, targeted and delivered to survive a hyper-competitive marketplace. How do hardware manufacturers decide where to invest their R&D dollars when shooting at a moving target? How do you convince developers to jump on board with your new technology and how do content developers decide what new technologies to implement? How do hardware manufacturers and developers work together to reduce time-to-market and how does the industry approach issues of backwards compatibility, API support and prolonged development cycles?

Moderator: Scott Sellers, 3dfx Interactive, Inc.
Panelists: Graeme Devine, id Software; Mark Rein, Epic Games; Jay Torberg, Microsoft; Mike Minahan, Intel Corp.
