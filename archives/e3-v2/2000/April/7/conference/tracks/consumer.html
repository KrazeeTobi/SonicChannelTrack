Consumers:
The Masses are Hungry

How does your branding and selling message survive the clutter and media bombardment every man, woman and child faces every waking moment? And how do you go about getting the girl gamer? Experts will showcase what's worked for them and where the industry is headed. And now for something completely radical: a panel of gamers and parents will provide their perspective.

Sessions:

4.1 - Girl Games: Sugar & Spice or Not-So-Nice?
10:00 a.m. - 11:30 a.m.
Thursday, May 11

What does it take to make successful games for all types of girls? Talk to a panel of girls' gaming experts about the games girls play, and why. Explore why you need to do more than put your product in a pink box to capture this growing market.

Moderator: Lori Moore Stacy, YouthScope
Panelists: Laura Groppe, Girl Games; Megan Gaiser, HerInteractive.com; Susan McLester, Technology & Learning Magazine; Isabel Wolcott, Smart Girl Internet

4.2 - The Brave New World of "Digital Convergence" How Will Interactive Games Be a Part of It All?
12:30 p.m. - 2:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

Top new media visionaries predict how the future of "digital convergence" will dramatically affect the way interactive games are designed, produced and distributed, and how they will be "experienced," not just played, by your customers. Learn how you should "play" the evolving merger and acquisition spree among the major Internet, media and entertainment conglomerates.

Moderator: Al Abrams, Creative New Media & PR Consultant
Panelists: Larry Kasanoff, Threshold Entertainment; Saul Berman, PriceWaterhouseCoopers, Entertainment & Media Group; Dr. Joan Van Tassel, Digital Media Futurist, UCLA; David Cole, DFC Intelligence

4.3 - Marketing to Kids: Targeting the 12 & Under Market
2:30 p.m. - 4:00 p.m.
Thursday, May 11

Explore the burgeoning kids interactive entertainment market and learn how the right marketing strategies can help you generate significant revenue from this niche. Examine techniques for reaching kids directly to create the "nag" factor. Learn how to develop a plan to convince parents that video and computer games and the Internet are a good source of fun and offer some educational value. Our expert panel shares lessons they've learned targeting this market.

Moderator: Sam Goldberg, New Kid Co.
Panelists: Peter Dille, THQ; Jerry Kapner, Lancit Media; John Hurlbut, Hasbro; Thom Ackerman Maslow, Mattel Interactive

4.4 - Research on Game Consumers: What the Numbers Mean
9:00 a.m. - 10:30 a.m.
Friday, May 12

The Internet has provided fertile ground for companies to get closer to consumers and understand their mindset and actions. A panel of consumer-oriented leading game businesses discuss what consumers are doing. Learn to use data gathered from consumers to improve your business. Learn how to tap the revenue potential of the audience and discover how businesses can retain their gaming audience over time.

Moderator: Anya Sacharow, Jupiter Communications
Panelists: Rex Ishibashi, Electronic Arts; Steve Kane, Lycos; Ilene Haase, The NPD Group; Chris Tice, Sony Computer Entertainment America

4.5 - Inventing a Successful Franchise
11:00 a.m. - 12:30 P.M.
Friday, May 12

Given that a successful game must have a sequel, it's important to ensure that any new game can be properly franchised. Learn to avoid the pitfalls of rushed sequels or poor cross-marketing by planning for franchising from the inception of your new games development. Discuss the planning and implementation of a successful franchise. Discover how to cope with the success of a character and take the brand further.

Moderator: Vijay Lakshman, Universal Interactive
Panelists: Frank Roan, 3DO; Lorne Lanning, Odd World Inhabitants; Jordan Weissman, Microsoft Corp

4.6 - Meet Your Consumer!
2:00 p.m. - 3:30 p.m.
Friday, May 12

After all the ads, the shouting, the co-op and the distribution, it all comes down to the last few inches of marketing. Talk face-to-face with a group of gamers and explore what they like, why they buy and where they buy it.

Moderator: Matt Firme, Imagine Media
Panelists: 5 gamers of all ages and genders
