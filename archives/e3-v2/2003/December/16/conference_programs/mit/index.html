Organized by MIT Comparative Media Studies with the Entertainment Software Association (ESA), The Education Arcade - Games in Education Conference will focus on key issues and emerging trends in the development, use, and market potential of games in education. The goal is to inspire collaboration among game industry professionals, university researchers, educators, and students that explore bold new approaches in the way games are used to support learning both in the classroom and beyond. The event will be held May 9-11,2004 at the Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3Expo) in Los Angeles, CA, immediately prior to the opening of the Expo show floor. Education Arcade Conference sessions will be open to the public and include an array of panels, talks, and discussions featuring well-known game designers, researchers, and educators, including Brenda Laurel, David Buckingham, Eric Klopfer, Eric Zimmerman, Henry Jenkins, James Gee, Jessica Irish, Ken Perlin, Kurt Squire, Roy Pea, Sid Meier, and Will Wright, among others. Minimum age of 18 required. Attendance at The Education Arcade Conference does not qualify one for admittance to the Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3Expo).
