Deadline for submissions is December 5th, 2003! All selected speakers will be notified by the end of January.

Mark your calendar as E3 will be celebrating its 10th year anniversary May 12, 13 and 14, 2004 at the Los Angeles Convention Center. We're currently looking for new, inventive, and exciting topics for our Conference Program. If you have one, we invite you to submit yours and hopefully join our faculty.

As a faculty member, you have the opportunity to educate and share your knowledge of what's going on in today's game industry with a core group of industry professionals. Over 2,000 dedicated attendees attend E3 not just for the excitement of the show floor, but also for this opportunity to improve their business and creative skills.

Whether your expertise is in marketing, wireless gaming, packaging, the online experience, retail, software development, or graphics, your ideas for a panel discussion or workshop topic may find a home in the Conference Program at the upcoming E3.

Our ground rules for participating in the Conference Program are simple and few:
1. Your participation should be in the spirit of commitment to your peers in the computer game industry.
2. All faculty members are responsible for their own travel and lodging. E3 Show Management does not offer any fees or remuneration for appearances on panels or in workshops.
3. This is not a sales opportunity. Our audience, the moderator and show management will not tolerate nor invite back anyone who uses this presentation time to sell their selves or their company.
4. As an E3 Conference Program faculty member, we consider you a VIP guest of E3. Registration is complimentary and a faculty badge allows you to attend all conference sessions and gives you access to the show floor and all its activities. Your affiliation as a speaker will be used in all appropriate marketing materials.

Unfortunately we're unable to notify all the speakers who submitted a proposal of our final decision. Thus we'll contact just the accepted speakers by the end of January.

If you're interested in this unique and exciting opportunity, please complete the following electronic proposal form and submit by December 5th - and don't forget to visit our E3 site for show and program updates.



E3 is a trade event and is not open to the general public. No one under the age of 18 will be admitted, including infants.
