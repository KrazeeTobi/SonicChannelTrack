What is E3?
The Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3) is the world's largest trade event exclusively dedicated to showcasing interactive entertainment and educational software and related products. Now in it's ninth year, E3 2003 will take place May 14-16, 2003. The E3 Conference Program runs from May 13-15, 2003.

[ back to top ]

Is the show open to the public?
No, E3 is not open to the public. E3 is a trade event and only professionals from the industry will be allowed to attend. Individuals who are not able to document their direct, professional affiliation to the interactive entertainment industry are not qualified to attend E3.

[ back to top ]

I would like to bring my child to the show. Is there an age restriction?
E3 is open to trade only. No one under the age of 18 (including infants) will be admitted. THIS RULE IS STRICTLY ENFORCED. E3 attendees will be required upon request to show government-issued photo I.D. (such as driver's license or passport) to verify age.

I have pre-registered. When will I receive my badge?
For attendees who register on or before April 4, 2003, and who have received a postcard confirmation, badges will be sent to you via U.S. mail beginning on March 15, 2003. If you registered after April 4, 2003, you must pick up your badge at the Attendee Will Call located in South or West Lobby of the Los Angeles Convention Center.

International badges will not be mailed. All international attendees (including Canadians) may pick up their badges at the International Attendee Registration Counter in South Hall beginning May 12, 2003.

All registrants must be prepared to show government issued photo I.D. (such as a driver's license or passport) in order to obtain the 2003 E3 badge and/or badgeholder required for entrance to the Exhibit floor.
