1.4 - Take Your Game Online: Four Case Studies
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM
Room: 411
 
Online gaming is rapidly becoming one of the dominant forms of interactive entertainment. And as consoles link up via the web, a wide swath of consumers is being introduced to networked gaming and massively-multiplayer games. Are you prepared to develop an online title? What are the design, technical, and management challenges? What are the costs? The common pitfalls? The potential upside? And what can the console community learn from the success of traditional, online gaming from the PC platform? This session will present case studies of four noteworthy online games, Battlefield 1942, MechAssault, SOCOM: U.S. Navy Seals, and Dark Age of Camelot. From development, to testing, through to launch, learn the basics of online game development from a panel of production and management experts.

Moderator:   Mark DeLoura, Manager of Developer Relations, Sony Computer Ent. America
Panelists:   Matt Firor, Exec. Producer, Mythic Ent. (Dark Age of Camelot team)
Mitch Gitelman, Exec. Producer, FASA Studios, Microsoft (MechAssault team)
Brian Soderberg, CTO, Zipper Interactive (SOCOM: U.S. Navy Seals team)
Patrick Soderlund, CEO, Digital Illusions (Battlefield 1942 team)

2.4 - Game Gems from Asia: The Lessons of A3, Fortress 2 Blue, and Project N.U.D.E.
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM
Room: 404AB
 
Most of us live and breathe the game industry as we know it in North America and Europe. But what about the Asian marketplace? Whether you view the region as one to sell in to, or one to comb for new ideas, it pays to know the current state of gaming in the Far East. Our Game Gems panel has assembled decision-makers from three of the most creative, most influential, and most daring games in release today. Listen in as members of our panel discuss their game's unique content and their team's special vision. Hear from Project N.U.D.E, a game out of Japan that lets you spawn, nurture, and communicate via voice with a virtual alien-woman; Fortress 2 Blue, a deceptively simple title that ranks as one of the most popular MMOL games in Korea; and A3, one of the first adult-only MMOL titles in distribution in Korean, China, and Japan today (from the developers of Legend of Mir2, a hugely successful MMOL RPG). Our panel will vividly expose ways the Asian marketplace is different from ours. Hear what the local markets crave and what sort of influence can we expect the region's most popular and unusual games to have here and around the globe.

Moderator:   Justin Hall, Game Culture Correspondent, Links.net
Panelists:   Yong-Joo Kwun, USA & Europe Team Manager, Actoz (KOREA: A3)
Yasuaki Nagoshi, President, Red Entertainment (JAPAN: Project N.U.D.E.)
Joon S. Oh, Global Business Manager, CCR Inc. (KOREA: Fortress 2 Blue)
Simon Chang, Director of Publishing, Greater China, En-Tranz (Shadowbane)

3.4 - Start-up Summit: How to Market Your Talent and Focus Your Business Strategy
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM
Room: 406AB
 
Changes to funding, development, distribution, and marketing means yesterday's start-up strategies may not work in the current market. This session will advise attendees how to structure a start-up development studio that takes advantage of current marketplace realities. Topics covered will include what publishers look for from development partners, how small- to mid- size studios and businesses can take advantage of the outsourcing needs of larger, more traditional publishers and developers, how direct developer licensing deals work, the way work-for-hire deals are creating new opportunities for small-scale companies, and, in general, where opportunities exist for the smart and talented enterprise. Developers and content creators will walk away knowing how to position their business for success and how to give themselves the best chance at succeeding as an independent studio.

Panelists:   Jeff Butler, Vice President, Sigil Games Online

Game AI is revitalizing the game industry, but not for reasons you might expect. 1.5 - How to Manage the Platform Divide in Mobile Gaming
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM
Room: 411
 
As mobile gaming becomes more prevalent and more attractive to both indie start-ups and the traditional game development community, understanding the impact of choosing one development environment over another should be your first concern. Join our panel of experienced developers and business people from all points on the mobile value chain as they drill down and discuss the current platform divide in mobile entertainment. Educate yourself on current adoption and usage figures associated with BREW, J2ME, SmartPhone, Palm OS, and other predominate mobile gaming platforms. Hear how SMS, EMS, WAP, and MMS are being enabled by carriers, and get an insider's view of how to choose among the differing platforms and languages in use today. Making the right platform choice for your unique game and for the market it is most likely to succeed in starts here, and starts now.

3.5 - Taking a Lesson from the Boom in Sports Games
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM
Room: 406AB
 
As a genre, sports drive the game industry, and often act to introduce non-gamers to the medium. The genre outsells all others, yet the games — and what makes them such strong performers — come in for little scrutiny. Sports have graduated from merely kicking a pigskin back and forth. Today's sports hits are a blend of top- quality music, sophisticated AI, and new in-game diversions designed to enrich pure team or individual play. Powerful marketing tools also come into play in the sim and action sports genres. A trio of industry executives with long histories of creating top-quality, top-selling sports games — 3DO's Trip Hawkins, EA's John Schappert, and Sega's Greg Thomas — will discuss the methodology of building a critically and commercially successful sports game, will share their expertise, and discuss ways publishers and developers of other genres can learn from their acquired wisdom. Learn how intelligent use of licenses, a creative approach to sequels, having a structure that encourages and uses player feedback, and original (and sometimes over-the- top) marketing can drive huge revenues toward anyone's bottom line.

Moderator:   Todd Zuniga, Associate Editor, Ziff-Davis Media
Panelists:   Trip Hawkins, Founder, Chairman and CEO, The 3DO Company
John Schappert, Sr. VP & General Manager, Electronic Arts
Greg Thomas, President, Visual Concepts Entertainment
