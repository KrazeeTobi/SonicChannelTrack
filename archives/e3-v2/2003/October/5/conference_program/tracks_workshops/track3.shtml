Effective Business Strategies for Today's Marketplace
The Business track provides a thorough understanding of the industry's current business trends, the most successful business models in play today, and what economic factors are likely to drive the game industry of tomorrow. Learn how to read and reach the marketplace, where new sources for funding can be found, and how a design studio - or any business in the interactive space -- can thrive in these challenging and competitive times.

3.1 - Reaching the New Gamer: Understanding the Mature Mindset of Today’s Consumer
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM
Room: 406AB
 
With 60 percent of gamers now over 18, it's only a matter of time before that age skews older - and older. Sooner or later, this shift in demographics will force publishers to release content to meet the demand of these new, more mature consumers. How will the market expand and what demographic groups will dominate? What genres will survive, what genres will fade away, and what new genres will be created? How can developers and publishers target their message to the right audience for a given title? And how should the industry react to the growing popularity of mature-themed games. Hear from a panel of game executives in positions of influence - executives who determine what types of games will be funded, executives who are influencing what gamers of all ages will be buying. Understand the opportunities and risks inherent in creating a generation of games targeted toward gamers of all ages.

Moderator:   Jason Rubin, President and Co-founder, Naughty Dog
Panelists:   Vince Broady, Senior Vice President, GameSpot
Bing Gordon, Exec. VP and Chief Creative Officer, Electronic Arts
Todd Hollenshead, CEO, id Software
Stuart Moulder, General Manager, PC Games, Microsoft

3.2 - Marketing Solutions for Every Budget
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM
Room: 406AB
 
How do you create and execute an effective marketing and promotion campaign that works with your game's unique content and company's fixed budget? Maybe you want your ads on network TV, but can you afford it? You might want your game previewed in Time and Entertainment Weekly, but do you know who to call? And how would you narrowcast a message to the enthusiast community for a game that is too niche for mainstream press or TV? A panel of marketing experts will use three hypothetical budgets - $100,000, $500,000, and $1 million -- and discuss the best methods to promote a game, create brand awareness, and drive sales within the limitations of each.

Moderator:   Brian Baglow, Founder and CEO, Indoctrimat PR
Panelists:   Liz Allen, Director of Marketing, LucasArts Entertainment
John Heinecke, Director of Global Brand Management, Activision
Brent Stafford, Development Director, Entertainment, Alliance
Greg Zeschuk, Joint CEO, BioWare Corp.

3.3 - Analyst Roundtable: How they Work, What they Know
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM
Room: 406AB
 
You spend your time making games, they spend their time analyzing the marketplace. Isn't it time you listened to what they have to say about which publishers have the best strategies, which platforms will find traction with consumers, and what retail models will prevail? Hear from some of the industry's top analysts as they discuss where the game business is headed. Understand their methodologies (to better analyze the marketplace yourself) as you hear them assess trends, sales figures, and industry growth. And understand the point of view of the publisher: How influential is the analyst community? What impact do their statements and reports have on a publisher's bottom line and ability to implement long-term strategy? This session will deliver information to make you a smarter interpreter of analyst output, and better equipped to manage your game business when news generated by the analyst community hits the street.

Moderator:   Alex Pham, Technology Reporter, Los Angeles Times
Panelists:   Alison Locke, Executive VP of North American Publishing, THQ

3.4 - Start-up Summit: How to Market Your Talent and Focus Your Business Strategy
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM
Room: 406AB
 
Changes to funding, development, distribution, and marketing means yesterday's start-up strategies may not work in the current market. This session will advise attendees how to structure a start-up development studio that takes advantage of current marketplace realities. Topics covered will include what publishers look for from development partners, how small- to mid- size studios and businesses can take advantage of the outsourcing needs of larger, more traditional publishers and developers, how direct developer licensing deals work, the way work-for-hire deals are creating new opportunities for small-scale companies, and, in general, where opportunities exist for the smart and talented enterprise. Developers and content creators will walk away knowing how to position their business for success and how to give themselves the best chance at succeeding as an independent studio.

Moderator:   Aaron Loeb, Producer, Planet Moon Studios
Panelists:   Jeff Butler, Vice President, Sigil Games Online
Tim Morten, CEO, Savage Entertainment

3.5 - Taking a Lesson from the Boom in Sports Games
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM
Room: 406AB
 
As a genre, sports drive the game industry, and often act to introduce non-gamers to the medium. The genre outsells all others, yet the games — and what makes them such strong performers — come in for little scrutiny. Sports have graduated from merely kicking a pigskin back and forth. Today's sports hits are a blend of top- quality music, sophisticated AI, and new in-game diversions designed to enrich pure team or individual play. Powerful marketing tools also come into play in the sim and action sports genres. A trio of industry executives with long histories of creating top-quality, top-selling sports games — 3DO's Trip Hawkins, EA's John Schappert, and Sega's Greg Thomas — will discuss the methodology of building a critically and commercially successful sports game, will share their expertise, and discuss ways publishers and developers of other genres can learn from their acquired wisdom. Learn how intelligent use of licenses, a creative approach to sequels, having a structure that encourages and uses player feedback, and original (and sometimes over-the- top) marketing can drive huge revenues toward anyone's bottom line.

Moderator:   Todd Zuniga, Associate Editor, Ziff-Davis Media
Panelists:   Trip Hawkins, Founder, Chairman and CEO, The 3DO Company
John Schappert, Sr. VP & General Manager, Electronic Arts
Greg Thomas, President, Visual Concepts Entertainment

3.6 - Grow Your Career in the Game Industry
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM
Room: 406AB
 
As the games evolve, so do the skill sets required to maintain those games' high technical and creative standards. Understand the growing importance of skills including server engineering, digital effects and adaptive audio expertise, art skills outside of computer art (sculpture, painting, musical composition), and you are on your way to growing and solidifying your standing in the industry. Attendees will learn what skills are in demand now, and what skills will be required to support the onslaught of massively-multiplayer, console, mobile, PC and web games due in the coming years.

Moderator:   Jason Della Rocca, Program Director, IGDA
Panelists:   Karthik Bala, CEO, Vicarious Visions
John Buchanan, Director, Advanced Technology, Electronic Arts
Don Daglow, President and CEO, Stormfront Studios
David Perry, President and Founder, Shiny Entertainment
