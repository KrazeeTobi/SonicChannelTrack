Online & Wireless (r)Evolution
Come away from the Online track with an understanding of what appeals to online PC and console gamers, how untethered devices are allowing for more pervasive gaming, what lessons can be learned from the world's leading players in the area of online persistent world gaming, and the current impact the web is having on game development, marketing, and distribution.

1.1 - Persistent Worlds: Toward what Future?
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM
Room: 411
 
Persistent world gaming is one of the fastest- growing sectors in the game industry. But not every release is guaranteed success. The market is getting more crowded, gamers are demanding more robust environments, and the importance of a clean launch cannot be underestimated. This session will ask an international panel of designers and publishers of MMOL games to discuss what was learned from the first generation of persistent world games, what they tried to accomplish with their own MMOL titles, and how the genre will advance in the coming years.

Moderator:   Jessica Mulligan, Executive Producer, Turbine Entertainment Software
Panelists:   Larry Dunlap, Founder and Creative Director, Intelligent Life Games
Mark Jacobs, President and CEO, Mythic Entertainment
Brad McQuaid, President & CEO, Sigil Games Online
Philip Rosedale, President & CEO, Linden Lab

1.2 - Design Secrets. Online: The Best of Asia and the U.S.
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM
Room: 411
 
A powerhouse panel of online masters — representing the major markets of Asia and the U.S. — meet face-to-face to discuss the design challenges, business opportunities, and the unwritten future of massively-multiplayer gaming. From Korea, the country with the highest penetration of broadband gaming, Nexon CTO Min Seo will address his company's repeated success with some of the most popular online games in the world including The Kingdom of the Winds, Legend of Darkness, and Shattered Galaxy. Joining Min Seo is NCsoft's Richard Garriott, whose rich and critically-acclaimed history in online gaming (the Ultima series of games, including Ultima Online) is now being applied to a number of highly-anticipated titles including Lineage II and "Tabula Rasa." Also on the panel is Raph Koster. Koster's career has placed him at the center of online game development, first as a key member of the Ultima Online team and now as a lead producer of Star Wars Galaxies. Attendees will have a unique opportunity to hear first-hand the status of MMOL gaming today, and where the leaders of the genre hope to take MMOL gaming in the future.

Moderator:   Rob Levine, Senior Editor, Wired Magazine
Panelists:   Richard Garriott, Executive Producer, NCsoft (Lineage, "Tabula Rasa")
Raph Koster, Creative Director, Sony Online Ent. (Star Wars Galaxies)
Min Seo, CTO, Nexon (KOREA: Kingdom of the Winds)

1.3 - The Brave New World of Console Gaming: The Web Changes Everything
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM
Room: 411
 
2002 will be known as the year massively-multiplayer online gaming jumped the gauntlet from the PC to the console. With great fanfare, and some serious success, console gamers got a taste of linked gaming - and so did a handful of companies who saw their products brave the new frontier of networked console gaming. How did this new generation of online console SKUs fair? And what's ahead for the industry and for gamers? This panel of game industry executives will discuss the challenges behinds bringing online gaming into the living room. What lessons can be learned from the first half-year online? Was the infrastructure there? Were the games robust enough? Live teams ready? What percentage of future titles are expected to land on consoles? And when can we expect a fully-functional persistent world experience from the current generation of consoles? Hear the three major players in the space today address the joys and agonies of a console's life online: Sony Online, publishers of EverQuest Adventures, meets Microsoft, purveyors of Xbox Live and publishers of numerous networked games, meets Sega, the only software publisher with titles on all three consoles (and its noteworthy experience with networked gaming via Dreamcast).

Moderator:   Daniel Sieberg, Technology Reporter, CNN/Headline News
Panelists:   Charles Bellfield, V.P. of Strategic Planning, SEGA of America
Cameron Ferroni, Director, Xbox Content Services, Microsoft
John Smedley, President, Sony Online Entertainment

1.4 - Take Your Game Online: Four Case Studies
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM
Room: 411
 
Online gaming is rapidly becoming one of the dominant forms of interactive entertainment. And as consoles link up via the web, a wide swath of consumers is being introduced to networked gaming and massively-multiplayer games. Are you prepared to develop an online title? What are the design, technical, and management challenges? What are the costs? The common pitfalls? The potential upside? And what can the console community learn from the success of traditional, online gaming from the PC platform? This session will present case studies of four noteworthy online games, Battlefield 1942, MechAssault, SOCOM: U.S. Navy Seals, and Dark Age of Camelot. From development, to testing, through to launch, learn the basics of online game development from a panel of production and management experts.

Moderator:   Mark DeLoura, Manager of Developer Relations, Sony Computer Ent. America
Panelists:   Matt Firor, Exec. Producer, Mythic Ent. (Dark Age of Camelot team)
Mitch Gitelman, Exec. Producer, FASA Studios, Microsoft (MechAssault team)
Brian Soderberg, CTO, Zipper Interactive (SOCOM: U.S. Navy Seals team)
Patrick Soderlund, CEO, Digital Illusions (Battlefield 1942 team)

1.5 - How to Manage the Platform Divide in Mobile Gaming
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM
Room: 411
 
As mobile gaming becomes more prevalent and more attractive to both indie start-ups and the traditional game development community, understanding the impact of choosing one development environment over another should be your first concern. Join our panel of experienced developers and business people from all points on the mobile value chain as they drill down and discuss the current platform divide in mobile entertainment. Educate yourself on current adoption and usage figures associated with BREW, J2ME, SmartPhone, Palm OS, and other predominate mobile gaming platforms. Hear how SMS, EMS, WAP, and MMS are being enabled by carriers, and get an insider's view of how to choose among the differing platforms and languages in use today. Making the right platform choice for your unique game and for the market it is most likely to succeed in starts here, and starts now.

Moderator:   Dan Scherlis, CEO, EtherPlay
Panelists:   Rio Caraeff, VP Wireless Services, Sony Pictures Mobile
Greg Costikyan, Chief Creative Officer, Unplugged, Inc.
Doug Dyer, General Manager, THQ Wireless
Adrian Sack, Founder and CEO, Ideaworks3D

1.6 - Charting Change in Mobile Entertainment: For Survival and Success
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM
Room: 411
 
How has the mobile beat changed over the past year? What are the new strategies for success? How does a company target growth in this increasingly competitive space? This panel will look closely at the prevalent business models and trends operating in today's mobile game sector including current development costs, realistic funding scenarios, current revenue share and charge models, ways to break into foreign markets, growth patterns for handsets and PDA's, and new opportunities to publish without a carrier deal in place. Increase your chances for success by understanding how the rules of mobile entertainment have changed.

Moderator:   Eric Goldberg, Principal, Crossover Technologies
Panelists:   Paul Goode, Developer Services Director, Metrowerks EMEA
Robert Huntley, Chairman & CEO, DWANGO, North America
Mitch Lasky, CEO, JAMDAT Mobile, Inc.
Elizabeth Schimel, Vice President, Business Development, AT&T Wireless
