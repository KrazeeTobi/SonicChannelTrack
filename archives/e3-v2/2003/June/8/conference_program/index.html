As advances and developments in the interactive entertainment industry continue unabated, the E3 Conference Program stays abreast — and often one step ahead of important industry trends.

Join us this year as the conference program pays special attention to some of the hottest issues facing the industry today, including: the business models behind online and mobile gaming, the latest console game trend of connecting gamers via the web; and continued advances in computer games, especially in the areas of technology and content development.

The conference program has always been the place for game industry professionals to share ideas and strategies, and learn from each other. E3 2003 will be no exception — this year's program will include an all-star group of panelists and moderators who will discuss the latest developments and current trends. And, as the interactive entertainment industry grows, matures, and broadens its appeal with mainstream consumers, the conference program grows as well. This year's conference program will include 35 sessions of workshops, panel discussions, and special interviews.

We invite you to participate in this unique opportunity to address the current issues, challenges, and opportunities which face the interactive entertainment sector today.

Late-Breaking Update: Three special Thursday luncheon sessions, designed to put attendees face-to-face with special guests in an informal, relaxed atmosphere, have been added to the Conference Program. Take your pick of lunches as we present three distinctly different options.

Hear MYST creator Rand Miller discuss Uru: Online Ages Beyond MYST, a project Miller and Cyan Worlds have been working on for the past five years. Given Miller's impressive success with MYST (to date, 12 million units sold), the industry will certainly want to know more about this upcoming game.

Also joining the Thursday luncheon program is Bandai veteran Shin Unozawa. Unozawa will take attendees behind the scenes of a number of Bandai success stories -- and will also offer his assessment of the state of gaming in Japan. This is a rare opportunity to drill down and know more about the Japanese game industry from a member of one of that market's most successful companies.

We also present Michael Gartenberg, Research Director, Personal Technology and Access, Jupiter Research, who will brief attendees on the changing PC and console marketplace, who the new gamer is, and what products are likely to sell into those new markets. Interested in knowing where to intelligently target your future efforts? Gartenberg will present recent Jupiter findings.

Join us as we offer unparalleled access to industry insiders. Lunch is provided for a limited number of attendees, so arrive early and be our guest. Sessions will take place on Thursday, May 15, from 12:45 pm to 1:45 pm sharp. Please arrive promptly.

Stay in touch with us as the program develops, and make your plans today to attend the 2003 E3 Conference Program next May 13, 14, and 15 in Los Angeles.
