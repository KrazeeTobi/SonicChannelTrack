Game Development: What Works
The Game Development track exposes attendees to the game industry's most successful and influential designers and publishers. Hear first- hand how designers create successful games, build and operate design studios, and work with publishers. Likewise, learn from publishers, what they look for in a game, what they expect from their development partners, and what creative risks they’re willing to assume.

4.1 - Hired Guns: The New Talent Pool and How to Fit In
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM
Room: 408B
 
The game industry has always used freelance writers and musicians, but is increasingly turning to independent artists, designers, and producers for help as well. Is the Hollywood model of a large pool of freelance talent right for the games industry-or just inevitable? What should a publisher or developer consider when hiring freelance talent, and where can they find it? How does a talented freelancer best operate within this new structure? How do we as an industry borrow on the production disciplines from Hollywood to create a more sustainable (and predictable) growth curve? Come away from this session with a better understanding of how outside talent is changing the way games are produced, and how to manage your studio or publishing company to take advantage of this change.

Moderator:   Noah Falstein, President, The Inspiracy
Panelists:   Keith Boesky, Head, Video Games Division, ICM
Nick deSemov, Art Director, A-Wake Productions
Ken Goldstein, Executive VP and Managing Director, Disney Online
American McGee, Chief Creative Officer, Carbon6 Entertainment

4.2 - Music Matters: What's Behind the Love Affair between Games and Music
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 11:30 AM - 1:00 PM
Room: 408B
 
It's not just graphics and storyline that determine which games get talked about. More and more, it's the game’s soundtrack that contributes to the splash it makes with the media, gamers, and retail. This panel will give attendees an understanding of how to use music to reach a specific, targeted demographic—and will look at titles which successfully engage gamers by the inclusion of top-quality music. Learn how to match the right music with your game, how deals are structured, how to find talent, and maybe the most important lesson you will learn–the reciprocal benefit record companies derive from their involvement in the game industry and how this relationship will likely grow. Hear from two of the game industry’s powerhouse music negotiators, and two key players from the music camp. Understand how the two industries plan to leverage their strengths and how you can benefit from this new found synergy.

Moderator:   Joe D'Angelo, Writer, MTV News
Panelists:   Germaine Gioia, Vice President Licensing, THQ, Inc.
Tripp Dubois, Vice President of Marketing, Capitol/EMI
Courtney Holt, Executive VP-Marketing Interscope Geffen A&M Records
Steve Schnur, Worldwide Executive of Music, Creative VP, Electronic Arts

4.3 - Sneak Peeks: Taking the Wraps off Halo 2, Lord of the Rings: Return of the King, and Starcraft: Ghost
Wednesday, May 14, 2003, 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM
Room: 408B
 
Last year's Sneak Peeks session marked the first public appearance of three of the industry's most anticipated games: Doom III, C&C Generals, and Star Wars: Bounty Hunter. This year, Sneak Peeks returns with a vengeance. Join us as moderator Geoff Keighley gives attendees an exclusive look at three games still in development: Bungie’s Halo 2, presented by Pete Parsons; Electronic Arts’ Lord of the Rings: Return of the King presented by Neil Young, and StarCraft: Ghost, presented by Blizzard Entertainment. Learn how the pros manage the game development process. Hear how they juggle the needs of their team, their publisher, and the media. Sneak Peeks is designed to inform by presenting a unique window into the game development process, so come prepared to take notes, ask questions, and learn how the top designers and project managers handle the pressures of developing AAA product-then use that knowledge to make your own game development process more efficient and more productive.

Moderator:   Geoff Keighley, Editor-in-Chief, GameSlice
Panelists:   Halo 2, presented by Peter Parsons and Hamilton Chu, Bungie
Lord of the Rings: Return of the King, presented by Neil Young, Electronic Arts
Starcraft: Ghost, presented by Blizzard Entertainment

4.4 - Game AI Update: New Ways of Engaging the Player
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 9:00 AM - 10:30 AM
Room: 408B
 
Game AI is revitalizing the game industry, but not for reasons you might expect. The renaissance in game AI isn’t coming from increased technical capabilities or more sophisticated programming tools alone, but from the simple fact that designers are using AI and scripting with goals of entertaining gamers in new ways. This session will look at games which use AI in unique ways and ask some of the industry’s most renowned AI masters to comment on the current state of game AI. Learn how our panel implemented AI and scripting language in their current games, what the market is responding to, and how they plan on using AI in their upcoming titles.

Moderator:   Richard Rouse III, Lead Designer, Surreal Software
Panelists:   Doug Church, Game Designer, ION Storm/Austin
Jonty Barnes, Lionhead Studios
Brian Reynolds, President, Big Huge Games

4.5 - Tap the High-Tech Pulse of Gaming
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM
Room: 408B
 
What role does technology play in the progress the game industry makes? In what ways does technology allow for more creative gameplay, more responsive AI, more realistic environments, more fun? Who are the technology leaders and what are their strategies for squeezing as much power out of a graphics card, audio card, or array of peripherals? 4.6 - New Hooks in Gaming
Thursday, May 15, 2003, 2:00 PM - 3:30 PM
Room: 408B
 
What do The Sims, Grand Theft Auto, and Black & White have in common? More than you think. Discover ways game designers use psychology, sociology, group dynamics, and artifacts of pop culture to make the new generation of games more appealing and engaging to hardcore, casual, and neophyte gamers alike. Understand how players’ need for community, the power of freedom, and even the allure of rock ‘n’ roll are shaping a new generation of computer and video games. Real Time Worlds’ David Jones (founder of DMA Design), Lionhead leader Peter Molyneux, and Maxis cofounder Will Wright are responsible for some of the most compelling games ever created. What’s their approach to game design? What do they consider essential elements great games must contain? What do they see as today’s, and tomorrow’s, hooks in gaming? This panel will provide unique insight from three designers at the top of their game.

Moderator:   David Kushner, Journalist
Panelists:   David Jones, Managing Director, Real Time Worlds
Peter Molyneux, Managing Director, Lionhead Studios
Will Wright, VP, Chief Designer, Maxis
