FAQ

What is E3 Expo?

E3 Expo is the world's premiere trade show for computer and video games and related products. The show is owned by the Entertainment Software Association (ESA), the U.S. association dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of the companies, publishing interactive games for video game consoles, handheld devices, personal computers, and the Internet. For more information, please visit www.E3Expo.com or www.theESA.com.

Is the show open to the public?

No, E3 Expo is not open to the general public. E3 Expo is a trade event and only professionals from the industry will be allowed to attend. Individuals who are not able to document their direct and current professional affiliation to the interactive entertainment industry are not qualified to attend E3 Expo. All E3 Expo attendees are required to show government-issued photo ID (such as a driver's license or passport) upon request.

When and where is E3 Expo 2010?

E3 Expo is scheduled to take place June 15-17, 2010, at the Los Angeles Convention Center. The Los Angeles Convention Center is located at the intersection of the Santa Monica and Harbor Freeways.

What are the requirements for attending E3 Expo 2010?

E3 Expo is not open to the general public. You must be part of the interactive entertainment industry in order to attend the show, and you must be able to provide documentation demonstrating your direct and current professional affiliation to this industry. To be considered for registration, two forms of industry ID must accompany your online application. At least one form of industry ID must contain both your name and the name of the company where you work. Incomplete applications will not be processed and the management of E3 Expo reserves the right to deny any applicant admission.

Do you check identification on-site?

Yes. Photo identification is checked on-site. All attendees will need to show a government issued photo ID (driver's license or passport) in order to receive the E3 Expo badge and badge holder. Please come prepared with your identification – no exceptions will be made.

Can I bring a child to the show? Is there an age restriction?

E3 Expo is open to trade only. No one under the age of 17 (including infants) will be admitted. THIS RULE IS STRICTLY ENFORCED. All E3 Expo attendees are required to show government-issued photo ID (such as a driver's license or passport) upon request to verify age.

I have pre-registered. When will I receive my badge?

If you are registered and confirmed by May 3, 2010, and have a U.S. mailing address, you will receive your badge in the mail, and must pick-up your badge holder on-site. If you are registered and confirmed after May 3, 2010, or have an address outside of the U.S., you must pick-up your badge and badge holder on-site at Attendee Will Call. All E3 Expo attendees will be required to show government-issued photo ID (such as a driver's license or passport) in order to receive the 2010 badge and badge holder.

All International Attendees (including Canadians):

Please visit the International Registration Counter located in the South Hall Lobby to receive your badge and badge holder. You will be required to show government-issued photo ID (such as a driver's license or passport) in order to receive your 2010 E3 Expo badge and badge holder.

I have my badge, do I need anything else in order to access the exhibit floor?

Yes, you need a badge holder. Each attendee who receives a badge by mail will need to go to a Badge Holder Pick-Up Counter to obtain a badge holder. Admittance without a badge holder is not permitted. A government issued form of identification (driver's license or passport) is required to pick up your badge holder.

Why isn't the badge holder sent with the badge?

E3 Expo is a trade-only show and is not open to the general public; therefore, we must ensure all attendees are industry affiliates before issuing a badge holder. A government issued form of identification (driver's license or passport) is also required in order to pick up your badge holder.

Where can I pick-up my badge holder?

Attendees may pick up their badge holders at any on-site Badge Holder Pick-up Counter located in both the South and West Hall Lobbies. Badge Holder Pick-Up Counters at the Los Angeles Convention Center will open on June 13 at 1:00pm.

Save time and pick up your badge holder before the show floor opens!
