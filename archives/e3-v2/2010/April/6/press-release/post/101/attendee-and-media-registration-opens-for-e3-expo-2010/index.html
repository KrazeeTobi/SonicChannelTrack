Attendee and Media Registration Opens for E3 Expo 2010
Global Game Event to Draw Thousands, Major Exhibitors Confirmed

Washington, DC - February 9, 2010 - The Entertainment Software Association (ESA) today opened attendee and media registration at www.E3Expo.com for the 2010 Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3 Expo), occurring from June 15-17 at the Los Angeles Convention Center. The E3 Expo welcomes all qualified computer and video game industry audiences, including international and U.S.-based media, analysts, retailers, developers, and business partners to preview the latest in interactive entertainment and technology.

"Exhibitors, leading video game publishers, and millions of consumers around the world are eagerly awaiting the opening of the 2010 E3 Expo," said Michael D. Gallagher, president and CEO of the ESA. "This year's event will premier cutting-edge technologies, immersive games, and innovations in consumer interfaces."

The E3 Expo works to meet the needs of exhibitors and attendees by focusing on value, while showcasing the creativity, excitement and energy that fuels the marketplace for computer and video game entertainment.

The event is open to ESA members and non-members alike. Eligible participants include: companies involved in console, PC, online, and mobile game publishing and development, qualified business partners, and makers of video game hardware and peripherals. Companies interested in exhibiting at the E3 Expo should contact IDG World Expo at (877) 216-6264.

About E3 Expo™ 

E3 Expo is the world's premiere trade show for computer and video games and related products. The show is owned by the ESA, the U.S. association dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of the companies, publishing interactive games for video game consoles, handheld devices, personal computers, and the Internet. For more information, please visit www.E3expo.com or www.theESA.com.

About the ESA™ 

The Entertainment Software Association is the U.S. association dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of companies publishing interactive games for video game consoles, handheld devices, personal computers, and the Internet. The ESA offers services to interactive entertainment software publishers including a global anti-piracy program, owning the E3 Expo, business and consumer research, federal and state government relations, First Amendment and intellectual property protection efforts. For more information, please visit www.theESA.com.
