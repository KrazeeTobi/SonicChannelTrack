Who should register as media for E3 Expo 2011?

E3 Expo 2011 media registration is open only to working journalists who are over the age of 17 with an accredited media outlet.

E3 Expo 2011 is a trade-only event and is not open to the public. No one under age of 17 will be permitted, including infants.

Qualifications of all media outlets will be determined by the E3 Expo 2011 media registration staff. Employees of media outlets who do not work in an editorial capacity will not be qualified for a media badge such as: Art Directors, District Managers, Graphic Designers, Publishers, Managers, Marketing Representatives, Public Relations Personnel, Sales Representatives and Senior Level Managerial staff.

If you are a member of the industry and wish to attend the show, please click here for general registration information.

If you work for a production company hired by an exhibitor, please click here for general registration information.

I have qualified as media and attended E3 Expo in the past. Do I have to re-register again?

Yes. If you were qualified as media for previous E3 Expo shows, you are not automatically guaranteed a media badge for E3 Expo 2011. You are required to submit new credentials each year to be considered for a media badge.

How do I register as media for E3 Expo 2011?

Online media registration is not open at this time. Please continue to check back for updated information. Before getting started, first read over our credentialing requirements.

After completing your online registration, please scan and email the requested credentialing requirements to media@e3expo.com. You will receive an email confirming or denying your registration request after the E3 Expo 2011 media registration team receives your required industry credentials.

Please provide your direct contact information should we need to contact you concerning your registration request. (Please note: E3 Expo is a trade event and is not open to the public. No one under the age of 17 will be admitted including infants.)

What are the credential requirements for an E3 Expo 2011 media badge?

Working journalists over the age of 17 that fall under one of the below categories may register for a media badge:

Print Media & Newswire Reporters:

To qualify as media, print and newswire journalists must provide:

  • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, and
  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and
  • A bylined article from your publication written by you and published within the last six months (the copy must clearly show the name of your publication and your byline).
Freelance Journalists:

Freelance writers from unqualified media outlets will not be admitted. In order to qualify as media, we require that you provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and
  • A letter from a publisher, editor or producer (on their letterhead) stating that you are covering E3 Expo 2011 on assignment, and
  • A bylined interactive entertainment industry-related article from your publication (above) written by you and published within the last six months (the copy must clearly show the name of the publication and your byline), and
Online Reporters:

Web sites and podcasters must be previously established and updated regularly with original and current interactive entertainment industry news. To qualify as online media, please provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and
  • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, and
  • A printed copy of your online publication with your name and title appearing in an editorial capacity, including a bylined interactive entertainment industry-related article from your publication written by you and published within the last six months (the copy must clearly show the name of the publication and your byline), and
Broadcast Journalists & Photographers:

Each member of a broadcast crew must register separately. To qualify as media, please provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and
  • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, and
  • A letter from a producer/editor (on company letterhead) stating that you are covering E3 Expo 2011 on assignment. Industry and Financial Analysts:

    Please provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and
  • A copy of your business card with name, title and company logo, and
  • A recent report or bulletin on interactive entertainment with your byline.

What facilities are available for media on-site at E3 Expo 2011?

The E3 Expo 2011 media center, in Petree Hall, offers convenient support for journalists covering the show free of charge. It features a fully equipped, large working newsroom including:

  • Apple and PC computers
  • High-speed Internet access and free wireless
  • Standard office equipment
  • Phones
  • Photocopy machine
  • A "news wall" featuring important exhibitor news and information
  • Media-only hospitality suite

Who can I contact to answer my questions?

Please contact the E3 Expo 2011 media relations team at media@e3expo.com or 415-848-8124.

What access is granted with my media badge?

Your media badge will allow you access to the show floor, meeting rooms, media center, media hospitality suite, breakfast and lunch. Please note, breakfast and lunch is first come, first served.

Will my media badge grant me access to press conferences?

No. Off-site press conferences are by invitation only as determined by the individual companies. Please contact those companies directly.

Who owns E3 Expo?

E3 Expo is owned and operated by the Entertainment Software Association (ESA). The ESA is the U.S. association dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of the companies publishing interactive games for video game consoles, handheld devices, personal computers, and the Internet.
