Industry Profile

Whatever these games "mean" to the people who play them... they mean a lot. Fifteen years ago, video games were barely more than a cottage industry, if by cottage you mean the sticky back corner of a strip-mall bowling alley. Last year game sales hit $7 billion, in the same exclusive ballpark as movies (about $9 billion). We should count ourselves lucky. The video game is a brand-new medium, and we get to see it evolve from the very beginning.

- Time, by Lev Grossman, November 8, 2004

Whatâ€™s the Bottom Line with the Computer and Video Game Business?

  • In 2004, U.S. computer and video game software sales grew to $7.3 billion, more than double industry sales of 1996. U.S. sales of interactive hardware and software together totaled more than $10 billion.
  • In 2004, more than 248 million computer and video games were sold. That's two games for every household in America. Interactive entertainment is the fastest growing segment of the American entertainment industry.
  • Today, more than 75% of American heads of households play computer and video games.
  • The average player is 30 years old; the average game buyer is 37 years old.
  • In 2005, 95% of computer game buyers and 84% of console game buyers were over the age of 18.
  • Women now account for 43% of all game players.
  • Almost 20% of Americans aged 50 and older play computer and video games.
