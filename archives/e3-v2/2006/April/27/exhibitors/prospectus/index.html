What a Difference an Interactive Year Makes

In the world of computer and video games, one year can make all the difference. In this environment of rapid-fire change, how can you keep abreast of all that's new? How can you make sure that you’re ahead of the pack? How do you catch the first glimpse of what the future holds? There's only one answer—the Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3). In just three days, you can boost your business savvy with a comprehensive overview of the entire video game industry in one place, at one time.

The computer and video game industry is one business that simply doesn't stand still—not for a nanosecond. New hardware introductions are happening fast and, thanks to eye-popping advances in technology, the quality of games continues to amaze players everywhere. More changes are coming as online, mobile and episodic games become increasingly more popular.

This is what makes E3 so exciting and why E3 can provide exclusive tangible benefits to your business. Video Games — Superstars of the Entertainment Industry

Video game software and hardware continues to grow at a double-digit rate. It is now an industry with total sales over $10 billion in the U.S. alone. This growth has garnered the full attention of the movie, music and publishing industries. Everybody wants to be part of this fast growing industry.

Think about it. There are big-budget movies based on video games. Books are written about video games. Major recording stars fight to get their music into video games. What's helped to fuel this trend? Not just more people, but different types of people now play video games in significant numbers.

Let's look at some of the facts. Today, 75% of American heads of households play computer and video games. The average age of a game player is 30; the average buyer is 37. In 2005, 95% of computer game buyers and 84% of console game buyers were over the age of 18. Additionally, women now account for 43% of all game players.

This is one business that's never been "business as usual." All indications point to continued accelerated growth for years to come. Industry experts proclaim that video games will become the dominant form of entertainment of the 21st century. Retailers know it. The media knows it. Wall Street knows it. This is one more reason you can't afford to miss E3.

To debut a product, see what’s new, find out what your competition is doing or catch the latest industry buzz, you need to be here. Make your commitment today for E3 2006.

Games are definitely big business—and E3 is "Where Business Gets Fun."

Into the Pixel—A Digital Game Art Exhibition

Into the Pixel is a showcase for the art of the video game, judged by interactive entertainment veterans and experts of the art world. This panel of judges selects up to 16 works of video game art for exhibit at E3.

Now entering its third year, Into the Pixel beautifully demonstrates how talented artists are utilizing the medium of video games as a canvas for fine art. It’s a collaborative effort of the Electronics Entertainment Expo (E3), the Los Angeles County Museum of Art's Graphic Art Council and the Academy of Interactive Arts and Sciences. The exhibit takes place in the Concourse Lobby at the Los Angeles Convention Center.
