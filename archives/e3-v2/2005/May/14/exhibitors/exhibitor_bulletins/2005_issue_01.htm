 E3 Tradeshow University
Early this month, the Show Management of the Electronic Entertainment Expo brought back their E3 Exhibitor University. This one-day educational seminar program was tailored to the needs of the E3's Exhibitor's. The agenda addressed the challenges that tradeshow managers and event planners face in preparation of their company's participation in an industry events while attempting to maximize their RETURN ON INVESTMENT.

Seminar topics focused on new rules and regulations relating to structural build outs and covered structures on the show floor, architectural plans and the need for early booth review process to ensure approvals and cost effective booth construction presented by Tom Fields, building superintendent for LACC. Fire regulations were reviewed as well as some new regulations regarding the use of smoke detectors and fire watches.

GES, the General Service Contractor for the E3 show, gave a detailed presentation on how to save time and money with regard to rigging, shipping, and labor. Kelly Green of Trade Show Rigging, a GES subsidiary, shared his knowledge and expertise relating to load capacity calculations for rigging and the need for making sure GES has a copy of you final plan by the deadline date in order to save the exhibitor money as well as eliminate on site challenges.

Cohn & Wolfe, the Public Relations firm that services this account, reviewed the menu of opportunities available to exhibitors to promote their presence at the show.

The Los Angeles Convention and Visitors bureau outlined what new and exciting renovations, construction and new properties are coming available in the near future.

For those of you that attended the worthwhile educational experience, we thank you for your participation and hope that your budgets and your exhibiting experience will be the beneficiaries at the 2005 event. For anyone who missed out on the seminar but wishes to receive copies of the presentations, please contact a show management representative via the E3 hotline at 877-216-6264.

 Booth Approvals:
Each exhibitor that contracts for 400 sq.ft. or more is required to send two copies of the overview design/layout of their booth to Tom Fields, Building Superintendent, Show Operations, LACC, 1201 S. Figueroa St., Los Angeles, CA 90015, Tel: 213-741-1151 ext 5608 - Fax: 213-765-4267 e-mail: tfields@lacclink.com. The layout is to include renderings and isometric drawings that clearly define the size and shape of the structures, identify all covered areas, identify any dynamic or moving elements used within the display and state the intended use of said dynamic/moving elements.

All booth approvals must be submitted by March 31, 2005. Installation of booths cannot begin before the Building Superintendent has notified the exhibitor of plan approval.
 Sound Policy
A maximum level of 85dbs will be permitted on the exhibit floor. This will be strictly enforced. Any exhibitor found in violation of this ordinance will receive ONE verbal warning. If a second violation occurs, the exhibitor will receive ONE written warning. If a third violation occurs, power will be disconnected for the remainder of the show and 50% of the exhibitor's priority points will be deducted. To avoid any on-site issues, all speaker/sound systems must be directed towards the center of the booth. There will be a sound check conducted by show management on Tuesday May 11 from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM to ensure sound level compliance.
 Character of Exhibits
Products or services to be exhibited are limited to software/content for computer and gaming consoles and accessories; virtual reality products; online gaming; multimedia products; associated publications and trade associations. Material, including live models, and/or conduct that is sexually explicit, including nudity, are prohibited on the Show floor and at any access points to the Show. Nudity, partial nudity and sexually explicit materials are prohibited on the Show floor and all common areas. ESA, in its sole discretion, will determine whether material is acceptable.
