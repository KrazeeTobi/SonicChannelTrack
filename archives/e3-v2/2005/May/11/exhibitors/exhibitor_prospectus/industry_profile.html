From a Niche Market to a Rich Market
In its first ten years, E3 saw the electronic entertainment industry grow from a niche market to a major player in the entertainment industry, competing head-to-head with movies, music and television. In 2003 U.S. computer and video game software sales topped $7 billion in revenue, and U.S. sales of interactive entertainment hardware and software combined totaled just under $10 billion. Thanks to a significant growth spurt over the past 10 years, interactive entertainment continues to be the fastest growing segment of the American entertainment industry.

The next ten years are every bit as promising as the last in terms of not only growth and innovation, but especially consumer demand. Think about this: A child born in 1995, E3’s inaugural year, will be 29 years old in 2014. According to Census Bureau data, by the year 2020, there will be 174 million Americans between the ages of 5-44. That means 174 million Americans will have grown up with interactive games from their early childhood and teenage years – and they’ll keep on playing. According to the Entertainment Software Association’s 2004 consumer research, more than half of all gamers expect to play as much or more 10 years from now as they do today.

This year more than half of all U.S. households will purchase one or more computer or video games . Advances like next generation handheld and console platforms, wireless games, faster broadband connections and GPS technology point toward an abundance of future opportunities and new revenue sources.

As more consumers get into games, and as game culture and veteran gamers continue to play, game brands and related products can only benefit. Why not put the incredible growth of this industry to work for you?
