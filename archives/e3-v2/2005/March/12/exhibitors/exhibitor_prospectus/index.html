Set Your Sights on the Interactive Horizon
Things change quickly and constantly in the worldwide interactive entertainment industry. Find out where you stand, where you need to be, and how to get there. Each year the Electronic Entertainment Expo gives you an up-to-the-minute snapshot of the industry in just three days.

This is one business that’s never “business as usual.” For instance, 10 years ago “wireless” wasn’t even a blip on the screen, but last year over 18% of the products on the floor were for wireless platforms. And thanks to incredible advances of technology, the quality of the games themselves continues to amaze players everywhere. Other changes are just around the corner as online games (fueled by faster network connections such as broadband) and episodic games become more prevalent. And, of course new hardware introductions coming fast, raising the level of anticipation for the industry’s products to new heights.

All the excitement at E3 has tangible benefits for your business. An industry survey conducted by Exhibit Surveys Inc. revealed that E3 has more attendees who plan to purchase products exhibited at the show within six months than any other trade event. E3 was also recently honored as the U.S. Trade Show with the highest buying power. That’s why 65,000 industry players came to E3 2004 – because it’s the most profitable venue in the world for key industry decision-makers to conduct business.
