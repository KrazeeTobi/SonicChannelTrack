Welcome to the 2005 E3Expo.com website.

Some big names have been added to the Conference Program line-up.

We are proud to announce that Hironobu Sakaguchi of Final Fantasy fame has joined the conference program. Sakaguchi has recently started his own studio, Mistwalker, and is already committed to creating two new role-playing titles for the new Xbox. Not only is Hironobu Sakaguchi one of the industry greats, this will be the first time he has participated in a North American conference such as ours.

Joining Sakaguchi on stage is Xbox Japan General Manager Yoshihiro Maruyama. Newsweek's N'Gai Croal moderates and leads this session which takes place on Thursday. May 19th at 11:30 am. Mark your calendars.

Noted screenwriter and film director John Milius joins an already talent-heavy session on Wednesday morning, May 18. Milius, who co-wrote the screenplay for Apocalypse Now with Francis Ford Coppola, is currently working with the Medal of Honor team on that franchises upcoming Dogs of War title. He joins game designer Toby Gard of Tomb Raider fame, Psychonauts designer Tim Schafer, and Bungie Studios cinematics director Joe Staten.

Few know the new consumer of digital content (including entertainment) as does Rishad Tobaccowala, the Chief Innovation Officer of Starcom IP. Tobaccowala participates is a session that also includes Vince Broady of GameSpot/CNET Networks, LucasArts VP of Global Sales and Marketing John Geoghegan, Sharon Lee of Look-Look, and Ubisoft Entertainment and Licensing Analyst Haden Walling.

Wedbush Morgan analyst Michael Pachter returns after last year's standing room only presentation on the next-gen consoles. And Maxis' Will Wright is on a special Tuesday panel of creative masters.

Our two in-game advertising sessions - one on Tuesday, another on Wednesday - are filling up with leaders in that fast-developing space. Mitch Davis from Massive, Guy Bendov of Double Fusion, Licensing Director Dave Anderson of Activision, Electronic Arts Director of Advertising Julie Shumaker, and Michael Dowling of Nielsen Interactive Entertainment are just a few of the thought leaders and actors in the in-game sector participating in sessions this year.

Of course, the Sneak Peeks session (presenting The Godfather, King Kong, and the Call of Duty sequel) returns.

Some of the industry's most renowned designers included in this year's program include Tetsuya Mizuguchi (Meteos, Lumines), Alex Ward (Burnout, Black), Todd Howard (Morrowind), Toby Gard (the upcoming Tomb Raider title), Yoot Saito (Odama), and Tim Schafer (Psychonauts) are on board.

From the corporate suite, Chief Marketing Officer Steve Allison of Midway Games, Jamdat CEO Mitch Lasky, Digital Chocolate founder Trip Hawkins, Square Enix President and COO Ichiro Otobe, and Activision's Senior Director of Licensing, Lori Plager join the program.

The program again brings attendees face-to-face with some of the most influential game industry players from overseas including Shanghai-based Shanda's COO Monte Singman, Dhruva Interactive CEO Rajesh Rao comes from India, and Gregoire Gobbi, Director of North America Content Strategy from Ubisoft Montreal.
