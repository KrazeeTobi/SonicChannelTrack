The Audience You Need to Reach
The Electronic Entertainment Expo's direct mail program reaches trade buyers across all relevant sectors of the industry, including PC and console software, educational software, reference software, special interest software and related products. Marketing programs are also directed at other influential industry players, such as software developers, financial analysts and venture capitalists. In other words, we're all business.

  • Over one million direct mail pieces are mailed to bring in the decision-makers you want to meet.
  • Extensive research - such as mail and phone surveys to VIP Retail attendees and exhibitors - identifies your key prospects and delivers a more qualified audience.
  • Advertising appears in major trade publications such as Next Generation, Video Game Advisor and Computer Retail Week, generating thousands of impressions to attract the most highly qualified audience to the show.

Roll the Cameras and Start the Presses
In 1997, E3 delivered media coverage from major news and print media, including CNN, CBS This Morning, NBC Nightly News, USA Today, The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, Entertainment Weekly, Los Angeles Times, San Francisco Chronicle, Houston Post, Dallas Morning News, San Jose Mercury News, Seattle Times and leading trade and gaming publications. The 1997 show drew over 1,600 registered press covering exhibitors' new innovations and product releases. With a state-of-the-art communications center on-site to get the word out about you, the press has what it needs to spread the news about your company all over the world.

An Unmatched Conference Program
E3's seminar program keeps the industry's buyers, sellers and creators in touch and on top of the latest trends in interactive entertainment and education. Our strategic partnerships with leading industry players in 1997, such as Computer Game Developers' Association, Interactive Services Association and Coopers & Lybrand, brought some of the most respected experts from every facet of the industry to the E3 Conference program.
