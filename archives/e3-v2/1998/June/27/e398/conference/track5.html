Track 5: Retail and Distribution
Hear proven retail and distribution strategies for selling your products and keeping them on the shelf. Reach customers creatively and effectively. Retailers, developers and publishers square off to discuss the most controversial issues in this competitive arena.

The Challenge of Retail: Self-Service and the Information Void
Consumers are finding it harder and harder to obtain essential game buying information through retail outlets. In this self service industry, where can the consumer obtain product information? What are retailers doing to provide information to facilitate informed purchase decisions? Hear how retailers are coping with on-line direct to consumer sales.
Jan Brzeski, President and CEO, STV Communications, Inc
What Can Retailers Do To Maintain Our Business -Survival Strategies in a Competitive Marketplace
How are today's retailers keeping pace with ever changing consumer buying patterns? How are retailers becoming more imaginative? A panel of top retailers present strategies and techniques for building customer loyalty and discuss future trends in retailing.

What segments of retail are experiencing the most dramatic growth? What classes of trade dominate the sale of interactive entertainment products? Hear a thorough examination of each retail category and their relative importance in the sale of the PC and console games.
Ann Stephens, President, PC Data
Anne Chen, New Business Development, Sony Computers of America

Get Your Game to Market!
With today's multitude of sales vehicles available, analyze the various options that are available in getting your product to market. Explore distribution alternatives from manufacturers reps and creating your own direct sales organizations to an affiliate label program. Decide what distribution avenues are most effective for you.
Susan Hughes, VP Sales, Mattel Media
Tom Frisna, VP, Business Development, Electronic Arts
Ozzie Katz, Senior Partner, Strategic Marketing Partners
Analyzing and Evaluating the Gaming Product Life Cycle
Embark on an analysis of the sales cycle from introduction to markdown. With limited shelf time, how do you get your product to stay on the shelf and maximize sales? What does the buyer want to hear from the publisher? What does the publisher expect from the buyer? Examine the challenges surrounding extending product longevity and managing product lifecycles.
Adopting Merchandising Strategies that Work!: Publishers Roundtable
This unique forum will enable you to bring your questions to a panel of publishers that will discuss their experiences on merchandising strategies that work! Benefit as they reveal a different perspective on merchandising your gaming product.
Kim Hannaway, Channel Marketing Manager, Hasbro Interactive
Tim Murphy, Senior VP, Sales and Marketing, Sound Source Interactive
