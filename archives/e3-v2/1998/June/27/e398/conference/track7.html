Financing New Media
Explore the essential steps of funding a new media company. Gain insight on how to survive in the competitive search for funding and evaluate the best deals for your company.

Merging & Acquisitions (M&A) and Accelerated Transition Management
The interactive media industry is well-versed on the benefits of M&A transactions. This session focuses on how to accomplish an M&A deal - successfully. Explore the most important things that need to be achieved in the last 90 days before and the first 90 days after a transaction deal has been linked.
Moderator: Jim Stone, Director, C&L Securities
Clark Spurrier, JP Morgan
Jeff Weiss, Electonic Arts
Terry Kinnikger, MetaCreations
Investment Trends: Following the Money
Embark on this financial journey that traces the trends in money flow in the interactive media industry. This session will analyze which types of interactive media companies are getting the money and how much they are getting.
Moderator: Andy Gregor, GT Interactive
Bob Wright, Ion Storm
Mike Dalton, Hughes and Luce

Stock Compensation and the Knowledge Worker
Stock compensation is a key element of the overall compensation value mix that today's interactive companies offer to get and keep knowledge workers. Hear how some companies have gained success with stock compensation plans, and learn how to avoid costly mistakes.
Doug Scott, Hollywood Stock Exchange
Joseph Conroy, Hunton & Williams
Andy Gregor,Gt Interactive
Valuation Criteria of an Interactive Media Company
What determines an interactive media company's value? Take an in-depth look at the impact of growth stage, cash flows, revenues, management, and more. Expert panelists analyze and critique the critical factors that are necessary to value a company for the successful IPO or venture capital financing.
Moderator: Chad Abraham, Piper Jaffrey
Larry Markus, BT Alex Brown
David Bohnett, GeoCities
