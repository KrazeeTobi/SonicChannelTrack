Media FAQ

Welcome to E3 Expo 2009! We want to make the registration process as painless for you as possible. If you have any questions about E3 Expo's media credentialing, please contact us:

EMAIL: media@e3expo.com
PHONE: 415-848-8124
MAIL: C/o Double Forte
600 Townsend Street, Suite 220e
San Francisco, CA 94103

What should I bring with me to E3 Expo?

Consistent with previous years, media who cannot produce their photo ID at the door to the Media Center will not be permitted to gain entrance into the Media Center under any circumstances.

Who should register as media for E3 Expo 2009?

E3 Expo 2009 media registration is open only to working journalists with news organizations who are over the age of 17. E3 Expo 2009 is a trade-only event and is not open to the public. No one under age of 17 will be permitted, including infants. Qualifications of all media outlets will be determined by the E3 Expo 2009 Media Registration staff.

Employees of media outlets who do not work in an editorial capacity will not be qualified for a media badge, such as art directors, district managers, graphic designers, publishers, managers, marketing representatives, presidents, public relations personnel, sales representatives and vice presidents.

If you are not a journalist, but are a member of the industry and wish to attend the show, please click here for general registration information.

I have qualified as media and attended E3 Expo in the past. Do I have to re-register again?

Yes. If you were qualified as media for previous E3 Expo shows, you are not automatically guaranteed a media badge for E3 Expo 2009. You are required to submit new credentials each year to be considered for a media badge.

Is there a form I need to complete?

Yes, along with providing the below credential requirements, you will also need to complete the
Media Registration Form.

How do I check the status of my registration request?

If you have already filled out a registration form and would like to check the status of your registration, click here for Media Verification.

What are the credential requirements for an E3 Expo 2009 media badge?

Working journalists over the age of 17 that fall under one of the below categories may register for a media badge:

Print Media & Newswire Reporters:
To qualify as media, print and newswire journalists must provide:

  • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, and

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and

  • A bylined article from your publication written by you and published within the last six months (the copy must clearly show the name of your publication and your byline).

Freelance Journalists:
Freelance writers from unqualified media outlets will not be admitted. In order to qualify as media, we require that you provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and

  • A letter from a publisher, editor or producer (on their letterhead) stating that you are covering E3 Expo 2009 on assignment, and

  • A bylined interactive entertainment industry-related article from your publication (above) written by you and published within the last six months (the copy must clearly show the name of the publication and your byline), and

  • If you are a freelance journalist for online media, you must also provide a copy of the Web site's business license or government issued documentation showing the business's federal tax identification number.

Online Reporters:
Web sites must be previously established and updated regularly with original and current interactive entertainment industry news. To qualify as online media, please provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and

  • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, and

  • A printed copy of your online publication with your name and title appearing in an editorial capacity, including a bylined interactive entertainment industry-related article from your publication written by you and published within the last six months (the copy must clearly show the name of the publication and your byline), and

  • A copy of the Web site's business license or government issued documentation showing the business's federal tax identification number.

Broadcast Journalists & Photographers:
Each member of a broadcast crew must register separately. To qualify as media, please provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and

  • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, and

  • A letter from a producer/editor (on company letterhead) stating that you are covering E3 Expo 2009 on assignment.

Industry and Financial Analysts:
Please provide:

  • A copy of your driver's license or government issued photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 17 will be admitted), and

  • A copy of your business card with name, title and company logo, and

  • A recent report or bulletin on interactive entertainment with your byline.

If I pre-register for a media badge will it be mailed to me before the show opens?

U.S.-based journalists and analysts who complete their registration by May 8, 2009 will receive their badges in the mail before the show. All journalists and analysts who receive a badge must still go to on-site Media Registration, in Petree Hall, at the Los Angeles Convention Center to pick up a badge holder.

International and Canadian badges will not be mailed. International media must pick up their badges and badge holders on site at Media Registration in the Los Angeles Convention Center.

NOTE: All journalists and analysts must present photo identification on-site in order to obtain a badge and/or badge holder.

I am a journalist under age 17. Can I attend E3 Expo 2009?

No. E3 Expo 2009 is a trade-only event, and is not open to the public. No one under the age of 17 is permitted, including infants.

If I submit my media registration information after the May 8, 2009 registration deadline, is it possible to register on-site?

Yes, you may register on-site at E3 Expo 2009. You must bring photo identification and the appropriate credentials detailed above.

What facilities are available for media on-site at E3 Expo 2009?

The E3 Expo 2009 Media Center, in Petree Hall, offers convenient support for journalists covering the show free of charge. It features a fully equipped, large working newsroom including:

  • Apple and PC computers
  • High-speed Internet access and free wireless
  • Standard office equipment
  • Phones
  • Fax machine
  • Photocopy machine
  • A "News Wall" featuring important exhibitor news and information
  • Media-only hospitality suite

What is included with my media badge?

Your media badge will allow you access to the show floor, media center, media hospitality suite, and breakfast. Please note, breakfast is first come, first served.

Who owns E3 Expo?

E3 Expo is owned and operated by the Entertainment Software Association (ESA). The ESA is the U.S. association dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of the companies publishing interactive games for video game consoles, handheld devices, personal computers, and the Internet.
