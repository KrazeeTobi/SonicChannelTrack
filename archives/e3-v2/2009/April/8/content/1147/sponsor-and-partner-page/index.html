Sponsor and Partner Page

Children's Technology Review

Now with a new monthly ad-free online/print format, and a review alert email service, Children's Technology Review makes every subscriber an insider. Since 1993, CTR has provided independent, objective coverage of games, tech toys and sites for parents, teachers and librarians. Visit www.childrenssoftware.com.

The Escapist

The Escapist covers gamer and digital culture in a progressive editorial style, with articles and columns by the top writers in and outside of the industry. A weekly publication with supplemental daily editorial and news updates, its magazine-style articles offer content for a mature audience of entertainment enthusiasts, industry insiders and other "NetSet" readers. The Escapist is the 2008 winner of both the Webby Award and the People's Voice Award for best games-related website. The weekly portion of The Escapist is published every Tuesday and explores a central theme, addressing head-on the topics relevant to modern entertainment aficionados. Combining print-quality writing and magazine-style aesthetics with the accessibility of the web, The Escapist is available for free online at www.escapistmagazine.com.

Future Games Group

Future plc is an international special-interest media group with operations in the UK and US creating over 180 special-interest publications, websites, emerging media and events for people who are passionate about their interests. Future is the US's 13th largest publisher by audited retail sales, with strong market positions in games, music, technology and action sports.

With official licenses from Microsoft, Sony, and Nintendo, our gaming titles reach 14 million gamers through titles that include Official Xbox Magazine, PlayStation: The Official Magazine, Nintendo Power, PC Gamer, Maximum PC, GamesRadar and Qore (the emergine media experience built directly into the home menu of the PS3 console). Visit Future at www.futureus.com.

GameDev.net

GameDev.net has emerged as the leading online community for game developers of all levels, from the green beginner to the seasoned industry veteran. Over 350,000 developers from around the world return here regularly to take advantage of our frequently updated developer news, thousands of articles and other resources, amazingly active forums and most importantly to be a part of the growing international community of game developers. www.GameDev.net.

GamesIndustry.biz

/GamesIndustry.biz/ is the market-leading online resource for news and information about the global videogames industry, covering all aspects of the interactive entertainment value chain from development through to retail. The site is UK-based, but has a significant global audience, with writers across the world who cover global events and issues. It is aimed at anyone involved with the *design*, *development*, *publishing*, *marketing*, *distribution* or *retail* of videogames - as well as anyone seeking a general insight into the sector. Visit www.GamesIndustry.biz.


GamePro

GamePro has been one of the most trusted sources for games information for 20 years this summer, June 2009. In both print and online, GamePro the first place gamers look for the most up-to-date and reliable news, previews, reviews, features, strategies, and videos. GamePro.com's influential community is essential to building "buzz" that can't be matched by any other games site. GamePro.com provides advertisers with a variety of innovative advertising and promotional opportunities aligned with superior content that will drive this community's awareness and affinity for their brands. Get involved at GamePro.com.

GameStar

GameStar is Europe's leading journalistic brand for pc-gaming. It stands for tough reviews (of both games and hardware) and editorial independence.
The monthly magazine offers concise purchase advice, in-depth-features on all things gaming and workshops for hardware and home entertainment enthusiasts. With the mag comes a covermount DVD with full games, patches, tools, demos and two hours of tv-quality video reviews. The website is all about interaction and databases: users can take part in a full-blown gamer community with a complete social media featureset. Or look up everything about a specific game in the biggest german-language pc-games database.

The main brand is supplemented by a special issue line called "GameStar World of Warcraft" -- a seperate team edits a monthly magazine on MMOs and provides a giant WoW-database under speedydragon.gamestar.de.

IGDA

The International Game Developers Association is the largest non-profit membership organization serving individuals that create video games. The IGDA is committed to advancing the careers and enhancing the lives of game developers by connecting members with their peers, promoting professional development, and advocating on issues that affect the developer community. For more information on the IGDA, please visit www.igda.org.

KidScreen

KidScreen is the leading international trade publication serving the informational needs and interests of kids entertainment professionals. Published nine times a year, the magazine's editorial content provides comprehensive analysis of the news, issues, trends, market shifts and opportunities having an impact on all sectors of the business, including TV production, distribution & programming, licensing & retail, marketing & promotions, gaming & digital media, feature films, home entertainment and publishing. KidScreen's cross-disciplinary approach to coverage mirrors the way the kids entertainment operates, and makes it unique in the trade publishing space serving this audience. Visit www.KidScreen.com.

Its powerful online and print products reach are essential reading for everyone in the business, as are its networking and awards events. MCV covers games publishing, development, retail and investment. To subscribe, visit www.mcvuk.com.

Music4Games.Net delivers an invaluable spotlight on today's emerging video game soundtrack industry and is the leading provider of game music infotainment for developers, publishers, producers, audio directors, music supervisors, composers, soundtrack collectors, dedicated gamers, students, marketers and music industry executives. For more information about Music4Games, click here.

PocketGamer.biz

Created by the team behind consumer website Pocket Gamer, PocketGamer.biz is the trade website for the mobile games industry, offering news, interviews and opinion on the hottest topics and most innovative companies. We talk to developers, publishers, mobile operators and everyone else in the mobile games ecosystem to ensure we give the industry the trade website it deserves." Visit Pocketgamer.biz.

WarCry Network

The WarCry Network is the leading source for news, information, and community in the entertainment industry since 1997. With its addition of the WarCry Network Affiliate Program, the WarCry Network boasts an aggregated unique base of over 5 million readers generating 75 million page views monthly. WarCry Network properties includes the flagship site, WarCry.com, as well as over 40 leading content destinations in its various game, comic, film, and hardware channels, and hundreds of forums and message board communities. WarCry Network is a division of WarCry Corp. Visit www.warcry.com.
ESA - Entertainment Software Association

The Entertainment Software Association (ESA) is the U.S. association exclusively dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of companies that publish computer and video games for video game consoles, personal computers, and the Internet.
