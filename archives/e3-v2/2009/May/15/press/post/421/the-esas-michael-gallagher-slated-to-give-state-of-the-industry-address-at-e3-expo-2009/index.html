The ESA's Michael Gallagher Slated to Give "State-of-the-Industry" Address at E3 Expo 2009

Washington, D.C. (April 16, 2009) - Join Michael D. Gallagher as he offers ESA insight on the state of the computer and video game industry. Gallagher will underscore that even in difficult financial times the video game industry continues to support our country’s local, state and national economies with record-breaking sales figures and rapid technological innovation. A question and answer session for attendees with Mike Gallagher will follow his remarks.

Overall computer and video game industry hardware, software and peripheral sales climbed to a record $22 billion in 2008, with entertainment software sales comprising $11.7 billion of that total figure - a 22.9% jump over the previous year. What does this mean for the industry and how dominant a force is the computer entertainment industry in the economy moving forward? Please join him and find out.

WHAT: A State-of-the-Industry Address by the ESA's CEO Michael D. Gallagher

WHY:To gain a perspective on where the entertainment software industry is heading

WHO: All 2009 E3 Expo attendees

WHEN: The opening day of E3 Expo 2009 -- Tuesday, June 2

TIME: 2:00pm

WHERE: Los Angeles Convention Center, Theater 411

ADMISSION: Free to anyone exhibiting and attending E3 Expo 2009

MEDIA CONTACT: Double Forte - Media@E3Expo.com

***EDITOR’S NOTE***
Camera space will be provided in theater. Please contact Media@E3Expo.com to determine logistical needs.

About E3 ExpoTM

E3 Expo is the world's premiere trade show for computer and video games and related products. The show is owned by the Entertainment Software Association (ESA), the U.S. association dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of the companies, publishing interactive games for video game consoles, handheld devices, personal computers, and the Internet. For more information, please visit www.e3expo.com or www.theesa.com.

About the ESA

The Entertainment Software Association is the U.S. association dedicated to serving the business and public affairs needs of companies publishing interactive games for video game consoles, handheld devices, personal computers, and the Internet. The ESA offers services to interactive entertainment software publishers including a global anti-piracy program, owning the Electronic Entertainment Expo (E3), business and consumer research, federal and state government relations, First Amendment and intellectual property protection efforts. For more information, please visit www.theESA.com.
