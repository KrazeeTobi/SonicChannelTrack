  1. Who should register as media for E3 2002?
    E3 2002 is a trade-only event and is not open to the general public. No one under age 18 is permitted, including infants. E3 2002 Media Registration is only open to working journalists with commercial news organizations who are over the age of 18.

    Employees of media outlets who do not work in an editorial capacity will not be qualified for a media badge. Titles that are not considered acceptable include, but are not limited to: publishers, sales representatives, public relations personnel, marketing representatives, presidents, vice presidents, district managers, art directors, graphic designers and managers.

    If you are not a journalist, but are a member of the industry and wish to attend the show, please click here for General Registration information.

  2. I've qualified as media and attended E3 in the past. Do I have to re-register again?
    Yes. If you were qualified as media for E3 2001, you ARE NOT automatically guaranteed a media badge for E3 2002. You are required to submit new credentials each year to be considered for a media badge.

    To request a media badge click here for Media Registration. Note that submitting the registration request form does not automatically qualify you for a media badge at E3 2002. The management of E3 2002 reserves the right to deny any applicant a media badge.

  4. What are the credential requirements for an E3 2002 media badge?
    Working journalists over the age of 18 who wish to register for a media badge must first submit a media registration request. Click here to complete the request form.

    After submitting a media registration request, working journalists MUST FAX OR MAIL the following credentials to E3 2002 Media eRegistration at:
    FAX: 310-967-2912 or
    MAIL: c/o DCW, 8730 Sunset Blvd.
    Fifth Floor
    Los Angeles, CA 90069

    Print Media & Newswire Reporters:

    To qualify as media, print and newswire journalists must provide:
    • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, AND
    • A copy of your driver's license or official photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 18 will be admitted), AND
    • A bylined interactive entertainment industry-related article from your publication written by you and published within the last six months (The copy must clearly show the name of your publication and your byline.)
    • Please note: The name on your business card and your photo identification must match to be accepted.

    Freelance Journalists:

    Please note: Freelance writers from unqualified media outlets will not be admitted. Qualification of all media outlets will be determined by the E3 2002 Media Registration staff.

    In order to qualify as media for E3 2002, we require that you provide:
    • A copy of your driver's license or official photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 18 will be admitted), AND
    • A letter from a publisher, editor or producer (on their letterhead) stating that you are covering E3 2002 on assignment, AND
    • A bylined interactive entertainment industry-related article from your publication (above) written by you and published within the last six months (The copy must clearly show the name of the publication and your byline.)
    • Please note: The name on your business card and your photo identification must match to be accepted.

    Online Media:

    Only journalists with commercial news web sites qualify for E3 2002 media badges. Sites must be previously established and updated regularly with original and current interactive entertainment industry news. Fan sites, personal web site writers, designers, editors or creators cannot be registered as media. Click here to register as a general attendee for E3 2002.

    To qualify as online media, provide:
    • A copy of your driver's license or official photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 18 will be admitted), AND
    • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, AND
    • A PRINTED copy of your online publication with your name and title appearing in an editorial capacity, including a bylined interactive entertainment industry-related article from your publication written by you and published within the last six months (The copy must clearly show the name of the publication and your byline.) AND
    • A copy of the web site's business license or official documentation showing the business's federal tax identification number
    • Please note: The name on your business card and your photo identification must match to be accepted.

    Broadcast Journalists & Photographers:

    Each member of a broadcast crew must register separately.

    To qualify as media, please provide:
    • A copy of your driver's license or official photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 18 will be admitted), AND
    • A copy of your business card with name, editorial title and media outlet's logo, AND
    • A letter from a producer/editor (on company letterhead) stating that you are covering E3 2002 on assignment
    • Please note: The name on your business card and your photo identification must match to be accepted.

    Industry and Financial Analysts:

    Please provide:
    • A copy of your driver's license or official photo identification clearly showing your name and date of birth (no one under 18 will be admitted), AND
    • A copy of your business card with name, title and company logo, AND
    • A recent report or bulletin on interactive entertainment with your byline (published within the last six months)
    • Please note: The name on your business card and your photo identification must match to be accepted.

  5. I submitted my online media badge request, but have not received a response. How long should I wait?
    To complete your application, you MUST FAX OR MAIL backup documentation (see question #4). Two to three weeks after you submit the online application form, AND send your credentials, you will receive email communication.

    To check the status of your registration, please visit our online media registration verification system at http://register.rcsreg.com/regos-1.0/e3la2002/adv/pr. You will need your name as it was entered in the online registration request form, your registration number and registration date, to check the status of your media registration.

    [top]

  6. If I pre-register for a media badge, will it be mailed to me in advance?
    US-based and international journalists who register by April 19 will receive their badges in the mail by the end of April. All journalists who receive a badge MUST go to on-site Media Registration in the West Hall at the LA Convention Center to pick up a badge holder. Journalists MUST wear a badge and badge holder to enter the show floor.

    NOTE: All journalists will be asked to present photo identification on-site, in order to obtain their badge and/or badge holder.

  10. What facilities are available for media on-site at E3 2002?
    The E3 2002 Media Center offers convenient support for journalists covering the show. It features a large newsroom equipped with Macintosh and PC-compatible computers and high-speed Internet access. Standard office equipment, such as local telephones, fax and photocopy machines, will also be made available to the media free of charge. The Media Center also includes a press conference room, a media kit room and media-only hospitality suite.
