SINOCES
The China International Consumer Electronics Show (SINOCES) is China’s most prominent consumer electronics exhibition. As an influential, annual CE event, SINOCES serves as a good platform for consumer electronics companies to gain global exposure for their new products, new technologies, brand and vision.

CEA is partnering with the China Electronic Chamber of Commerce (CECC) to expand the international scope of SINOCES to help strengthen trade and business opportunities between the U.S. consumer technology industry and China.

It's never to early to plan for the 2007 SINOCES, July 6-9, 2007, and go global.

Show details
Exhibit Dates
Friday, July 6 - Monday, July 9, 2007

Location
Qingdao International Convention Center
Shandong Province, Qingdao, China

Qingdao, a coastal city in east China, is on one of three peninsulas—Shandong Peninsula—along China’s east (northeast) coast.

Exhibitor Profile
The 2006 event attracted more than 62,000 attendees from across the globe, including 5,300 attendees traveling from outside China. Industry professionals experienced the latest products from more than 390 global exhibitors, across a sold-out show floor comprised of 35,000 square meters of exhibit space, up from 24,000 square meters in 2005. Key global exhibitors highlighting their new products on the show floor included Haier, Intel and Sony. Several major international retailers were in attendance including Best Buy, Carrefour, Karstadt Metro, Obi, Quelle, Radio Shack and Walmart.
