Today's televisions offer great picture and sound quality along with extensive features, all for a reasonable price:

  • Electronic program guides which provide programming information and enables viewers to sort programs by theme
  • Picture-in-picture which allows viewers to watch two programs simultaneously
  • Channel lock out which lets parents control what channels their children can view
  • Channel surfing which lets viewers select their ten favorite channels to access with one touch of a button on the remote
  • Built-in stereo sound

By combining a big-screen television with an assortment of digital products including a direct broadcast satellite system (DBS), a digital video disc player (DVD), or a D-VHS videocassette recorder and digital audio products, consumers can create a spectacular home theater experience.

Big screen televisions range in screen size from 27-inches to 40-inches (direct view) and 40-inches to 80-inches (projection) with prices starting at $400.

TV FACTOIDS

  • One out of every four households purchases a television every year, and sales have been at 25,000,000 TVs each year for the past five years
  • 18 million households have bought a TV that costs $2,000 or more
  • Nearly 50 percent of TVs sold in the U.S. are produced in the U.S.
  • Consumer spend approximately $9.5 billion annually on TVs
  • The average household owns 2.5 televisions
  • Roughly 250 million TVs are in use today in the U.S.

THE DIGITAL PRODUCTS
Today's home entertainment experience can be greatly enhanced by connecting any of thefollowing products to a big-screen television:

Direct Broadcast Satellite (DBS)

  • Brings digital video to the living room using a small satellite dish and set-top box
  • Provides outstanding pictures and CD quality sound
  • Offers up to 200 channels of sports, movies, entertainment and information, including extensive pay-per-view options

Digital Video Disc (DVD)

  • Plays movies and music on a five-inch disc
  • Ability to have more information on each discs; a two-hour movie can be stored on one side
  • DVD players play current audio CDs
  • Discs can accommodate more features including different language tracks, subtitles, camera angles, multiple aspect ratios and movie information

D-VHS Videocassette Recorder

  • New VCR that lets DBS users make digital recordings
  • Provides full digital benefits for DBS owners with digital video outputs on their systems
  • Plays regular VHS tapes and records traditional analog broadcasts

Digital Audio System

  • Enhances the audio portion of home theater systems
  • Utilizes digital decoder and five speakers plus a subwoofer to create theater-like sound

WHAT ABOUT DIGITAL TELEVISION?
Digital television (DTV) is coming, but the transition will be gradual with approximately 30 percent of U.S. households expected to have DTV by the year 2006. When digital TV programmingbecomes available, set-top boxes will be on the market to enable today's televisions toreceive the DTV signals.
