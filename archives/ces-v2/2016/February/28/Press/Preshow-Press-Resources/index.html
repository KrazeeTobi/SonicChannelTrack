    Enhanced Security Measures 
    We want a safe CES and work to make safety a priority across our event. With this in mind, please note that CES is implementing new bag restrictions and enhanced security measures for CES 2016. We are doing this with the goal of maintaining the safety of all of our guests -- general attendees, exhibitors, members of the media -- and show personnel, while creating as little inconvenience as possible. 

    Members of the media attending CES will be subject to the same security and safety restrictions and procedures as general show attendees with some allowances: 
    • Members of the media with an official show badge will be permitted to hand carry equipment onto show properties, in excess of our two bag restriction, through designated entrances. This equipment will be subject to search and tagged as approved for entry.
    • Media must have a fully visible CES media badge before entering. Security will check CES badges and government-issued photo ID. You should carry your official show badge and/or press credentials and a personal ID with you while on show properties.
    • We strongly encourage all members of the media to preregister and pick up your badge prior to arrival at any show venue. 
    Please reference the media security guidelines page for further information.  

    We appreciate your understanding and compliance with these security measures. Should you have specific questions, please contact us at CESops@CE.org.

    CES 2016 will feature Hollywood stars, professional athletes, TV personalities and celebrity chefs discussing and promoting technology that is revolutionizing the way consumers live, work and play. Check out the full press release for details.

    Policymakers and Regulators Join CES 2016 
    More than two dozen international, federal, state and local policy makers and regulators will be delivering remarks or speaking on panels at CES. Speakers include: Transportation Secretary Anthony Foxx, Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) Administrator Michael Huerta, every member of the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) and Federal Trade Commission (FTC), U.S. Chief Technology Officer Megan Smith, Director of the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office Michelle Lee, Sens. Dean Heller (R-NV) and Mark Warner (D-VA), Rep. Darrell Issa (R-CA) and France's Minister of Economy, Industry and Digital Affairs, Emmanuel Macron. In total, more than 160 top policymakers, regulators and staff are expected to attend.
