How One Organization is on a Mission to Make Cooking in the Developing World Healthier, Cleaner and Safer

Almost 3 billion people across the world still cook their food over open fires. The risks of this cooking method do not just affect the environment –  cooking over open fires can also lead to a myriad of health issues, including child pneumonia, lung cancer and heart disease.
 
 
The Global Alliance for Clean Cookstoves, hosted by the United Nations Foundation, aims to alleviate the health, environmental and safety issues that come with traditional cooking methods by promoting the adoption of new technologies that allow safe, clean and healthy cooking.
 
We had a chance to speak to Kip Patrick, director of global communications of the Global Alliance for Clean Cookstoves about why clean cookstoves are necessary and what technologies are making their mission a reality.
 
What are the health issues associated with using traditional open fires or inefficient cookstoves?
 "Household air pollution from cooking over open fires and inefficient cookstoves kills over four million people every year and sickens millions more, according to the World Health Organization. Daily exposure to toxic smoke from traditional cooking practices is one of the world’s biggest – but least well-known killers. These fires can emit the equivalent of 400 cigarettes per hour. Penetrating deep into the lungs of its victims, the acrid smoke causes a range of deadly chronic and acute health effects such as child pneumonia, lung cancer, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, and heart disease, as well as low birth-weights in children born to mothers whose pregnancies are spent breathing toxic fumes from traditional cookstoves."
 
What are the environmental impacts of traditional cooking methods?
"Nearly three billion people around the world still cook their food as our ancestors did, burning wood, charcoal, animal dung or coal in open fires or in inefficient stoves for daily cooking and heating. This reliance on inefficient cookstoves and fuels leads to a wide variety of environmental problems including deforestation, air pollution, and climate change.
 
In addition to deforestation and air pollution, burning solid fuels releases emissions of some of the most important contributors to global climate change: carbon dioxide, methane, black carbon and other short-lived climate pollutants (SLCPs). Unsustainable wood harvesting also contributes to deforestation, reducing carbon uptake by forests.
 
Black carbon, which results from incomplete combustion, is estimated to contribute the equivalent of 25 to 50 percent of carbon dioxide warming globally, and residential solid fuel burning accounts for 21 percent of global black carbon emissions, about 84 percent of which is from households in developing countries. In South Asia, for example, more than half of black carbon comes from the use of inefficient cookstoves. Black carbon also disrupts the monsoon and accelerates melting of the Himalayan-Tibetan glaciers. As a result, water availability and food security are threatened for millions of people. These problems are compounded by crop damage from ozone produced in part by cookstove emissions and from surface dimming, as airborne black carbon intercepts sunlight."
 
What is the mission of the Global Alliance for Clean Cookstoves?
"The Global Alliance for Clean Cookstoves is a public-private partnership hosted by the United Nations Foundation to save lives, improve livelihoods, empower women, and protect the environment by creating a thriving global market for clean and efficient household cooking solutions. The Alliance has an ambitious 10-year goal to foster the adoption of clean cookstoves and fuels in 100 million households by 2020."
 
What kinds of technologies are used to create clean cookstoves?
"Creating a clean cookstove is extremely complex. Designers must consider the types of materials used in the stoves, the weight, costs, as well as source-ability of the materials used. In addition, a stove’s performance can vary widely based on geography, weather conditions, fuel types and even the person using the stove. One of the key ingredients in stove design must be testing. Laboratory-based tests evaluate stove performance and quality in a controlled setting with repeatability, allowing for differentiation between stoves. Field-based tests demonstrate how stoves perform with local cooks, foods, practices and fuels. The Alliance maintains a comprehensive list of protocols (for a range of stove and fuel types) and archives of earlier versions (available upon request).
 
To review the performance of hundreds of stoves currently on the market around the world, check out the Clean Cooking Catalog. The Catalog is a global database of cookstove and fuel performance information using key indicators such as stove features, pricing, specifications, emissions levels, efficiency and safety from laboratory and field-testing."
 
How are you embracing innovation to promote clean cookstoves?
"Innovation is a cornerstone of the clean cookstoves movement. The Alliance provides funding for research, capacity building, training, entrepreneur support, in-country alliances and other initiatives to drive innovation. We are working closely with our more than 1,200 partners to build a thriving global market for clean cookstoves and fuels."
 
How can the technology industry help you tackle your challenges?
"With three billion people still cooking over open fires or with inefficient stoves, there’s a significant role for technology. Technological advancements are helping to drive innovation in the clean cooking movement. New rocket stoves being introduced burn fuel far more efficiently and effectively, reducing harmful pollutants and saving people time and money. Recently developed solar technologies are enabling people to take advantage of sun-powered cooking like never before. There are also stoves on the market now that allow people to charge a cell phone or power a light while they cook, which goes a long way, particularly in regions where electricity is scarce or unreliable."
 
What can our readers do to get involved and support the cause?
"There are numerous ways to support the clean cookstoves cause. The Alliance currently works with over 1,200 partners focused on building a thriving global market for cleaner cookstoves and fuels. Interested organizations can visit our Partners Page to learn more about becoming a Global Alliance partner.  In addition, we encourage everyone to learn more and help us spread the word about this under-reported issue that impacts millions of people every day. Follow us on Twitter, like us on Facebook and sign up for our informative monthly newsletter."
 
Which countries and regions is the Global Alliance for Clean Cookstoves currently focused on?
"To allow for maximum impact in the field and strengthen the market for clean cookstoves and fuels, the Alliance has prioritized eight countries for deeper in-country engagement – Bangladesh, China, Ghana, Guatemala, India, Kenya, Nigeria and Uganda. These focus countries form the foundation for the Alliance’s efforts to promote a thriving market for clean cookstoves and fuels and serve as a learning lab for best-practices and toolkits that are shared with our other implementing partner countries.
 
The Alliance has worked closely with leading sector stakeholders in each of our eight focus countries to develop robust country action plans (CAPs) which identify intervention options, detail partner commitments and highlight areas for potential Alliance and stakeholder engagement in each country.
 
The Alliance continues to consult with and provide technical assistance, targeted resources and partner toolkits to national stakeholders in partner countries such as Colombia, Ethiopia, Indonesia, Mexico, Nepal, Peru, Rwanda and Tanzania.  In addition, with some exceptions, Alliance funding opportunities are open to qualified stakeholders operating in the broader range of Alliance partner countries and we work with national counterparts, local alliances and clean cooking stakeholders to develop a thriving market for clean cooking solutions."
