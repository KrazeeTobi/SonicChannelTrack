2011 Innovations Judges



Tor Alden
HS Design
Principal

Tor Alden is a principal of HS Design, a full-service product development firm, headquartered in Gladstone, NJ. HS Design is known for its successful user driven product design solutions in a wide range of markets including industrial, commercial, medical, electronics, and consumer products. HS Design is a recognized leader in the field, producing over 400 successful products since its inception some 35 years ago. Services include design research, product/brand development market strategy, industrial design, packaging, mechanical design, prototyping, vendor sourcing and manufacturing liaison.

Alden's experience includes more than 18 years in product design and corporate branding, during which he has received more than 30 patents and multiple design awards. Alden is an active participant in industry and educational associations. He is a member of the Industrial Designers Society of America (IDSA) and currently serves as co-chair of the society's medical section. He serves on the advisory board of the Department of Design at Kean University in New Jersey.

Alden received his bachelor's degree in industrial design from Syracuse University and his Master of Science degree in technology management from Stevens Institute of Technology.



Gary Arlen
Arlen Communications,Inc.
Editor and Commentator/Columnist

Gary Arlen is President of Arlen Communications Inc., a Washington-area research and consulting firm specializing in converging media, telecommunications and information services. Arlen's primary focus is on the applications and services enabled by digital, broadband and interactive media platforms. His current work focuses on interactive content and its relationships to distribution technology – including broadband and digital TV. Gary has explored the role of consumer electronics products and providers in the evolving media ecoscape.



Rob Baranowski
Park-Tours,Inc.
President/CTO/Founder

Robert Baranowski founded Park-Tours in 1999, designing software for PDA's and later cell phones for providing theme park guests information about the park, line bypassing, current wait times, scheduling, shopping and more. Since then, with Baranowski's lead technical role, the company has evolved into an engineering and technology company, with products ranging from wireless soil monitoring systems to high-end audio and room-measuring surround sound to portable IV pumps and hand-held wireless inventory counters. In 2008, Rob set up the Left Coast Engineering dba to separate the consulting work from the theme park software.

Prior to founding Park-Tours, Rob designed hardware and RF for Motorola and Sony cellular. Before starting his wireless career, Rob spent over four years designing software systems for many divisions of Six Flags Great Adventure, one of the largest parks in the Six Flags system. He has 14 patents issued and an additional 19 filed.



Rich Buttiglieri
Bentley University Design and Usability Center
Senior Usability Consultant

Rich Buttiglieri is a Senior Usability Consultant at the Design and Usability Center at Bentley University. Rich brings more than 24 years of experience in product design and evaluation to his role at the center. He has considerable experience conducting user-centered research and design of software, consumer electronics, and medical devices for both mass markets and highly specialized industries.

Rich holds a BS in Computer Science and a Graduate Certificate in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). He is a member of the Usability Professionals' Association, HFES Product Design Technical Group, UPA Boston, and BostonCHI.



Benjamin Chia
elemental8
Chief Creative Officer/Partner

Benjamin Chia is the Partner and Chief Creative Officer of elemental8. He is an experienced and multi-faceted industrial designer capable in all phases of the design process. As a partner and CCO, he mentors and manages the creative staff and develops new business potentials.

Benjamin's design work has been recognized with numerous design awards from BusinessWeek/Industrial Design Excellence Awards (IDEA), the Consumer Electronics Show Innovation Showcase (CES), International House wares Awards (IHA), Good Design Awards, ID Magazine Design Annual, and others. His work can be seen as permanent collections at the New York Museum of Modern Art, San Francisco Museum of Modern Art, and the National Archives in Washington DC.

Since 2000, Benjamin has been teaching at Stanford University, Academy of Art College, San Francisco State University, and San Jose State University, focusing on design process, practice and application.



Bill Clem
Strategix ID
Founder and CEO

Bill Clem is the founder and CEO of Strategix ID, a product design firm, which integrates and balances multidisciplinary expertise, research methodologies, and state of the art technology to quickly translate customer focused product research into innovative, award-winning products. Bill has 25 years experience and has been an inventor or co-inventor on many engineering and design patents. His firm has won numerous design awards, and has three products on permanent display in the Chicago Museum of Modern Art. Bill is on the advisory board of Western Washington University, and previously was an assistant professor at Art Center in Los Angeles. Bill was one of the first Industrial Designers in the US to embrace complex 3-D solids modeling software as a tool for quick visualization. This proficiency led to his becoming a beta tester and software demonstrator for HP while he lived in Seattle. His extensive knowledge of design, engineering and manufacturing processes, combined with his leadership in user focused innovation, have been the cornerstones for both his, and his company's success.



Tony Costa
Altitude
Senior Design Strategist

Tony Costa is a Senior Design Strategist at Altitude, a product innovation consultancy based on Boston, MA. Altitude believes that true innovation and client success arises when talent and spirited intellectual engagement meet business acumen and a deep understanding of consumer needs and desires. Their expertise in design strategy, design innovation, product realization, and brand expression allow them to help clients clearly define their goals, differentiate themselves from competitors, lessen their impact on the environment, and offer solutions that are wholly relevant to consumers. At Altitude, Tony draws on his extensive experience in front-end planning activities including ethnographic research, trend analysis, competitive intelligence, portfolio mix analysis, and new opportunity definition & evaluation to help clients across a variety of industries.

Tony has a Masters in Design from the Institute of Design at the Illinois Institute of Technology and Bachelor of Arts degrees in Semiotics and Visual Arts from Brown University.

Solomon Daniels
Principal

He began his career as a car stereo installer and grew into positions covering retail sales, product technical support, product development, marketing and design. His extensive knowledge of aftermarket products for the vehicle, portable integration and his own personal love of gadgets has helped companies meet their short- and long-term marketing goals. Daniels continues to participate in and support the industry with analysis and insight through his Website (righthand1.com) and newsletter, 12 Volt Industrywide (12voltindustrywide.com).

VP,Director Video & Telepresence Engineering IMCCA

He has also served as a National Association of Broadcasters conference "Pick-Hits" judge for the last nine years.
David is a frequent contributor to industry publications and presenter at industry events. He is also the author of the popular "View From The Road" series of blogs.



John Devlin
Essential,Inc.
Engineering Director

John Devlin oversees the engineering function at Essential. As a widely recognized engineer, Devlin has received multiple design industry awards and several patents. He manages multi-disciplinary development projects, long-term relationships, and advises clients on product development process and strategy. In addition, Devlin is an authority in critical-path project management in a range of industries including consumer electronics, healthcare and industrial equipment.

As a master engineering/development tactician, Devlin identifies potential obstacles and leads teams past them to engineer efficient and effective product development programs. Working closely with clients such as Shure, Owens Corning, Lexicon, Motorola, Candela, Fishman, and others, Devlin helps clients successfully commercialize their technologies and realize manufacturing efficiencies, while improving product quality and addressing user expectations.

Prior to Essential, Devlin was a Vice President of Engineering at Fitch, and a mechanical engineer at a/d/s/. He is a member of the Society of Mechanical Engineers and Mass Medic. Devlin holds a Bachelors of Science, Mechanical Engineering, from the University of Massachusetts at Lowell.

Steve Ewing
Ewing Design Group Inc.
President/CEO

Steven Ewing is the founder and president of Ewing Design Group, a top Southern California medical device, equipment, and consumer product design firm.
Mr. Ewing consults with business leaders to help them establish brand identity and brand trust through better products. His philosophy is: "Design gives products emotional appeal, appealing products build strong brands, and strong brands build market share. Design is the only way for a company to make their products different and better than the competition. In today's global marketplace, brand differentiation strategies are crucial to sustainability."

Seeing his most critical role as that of an advocate, Ewing's clients not only use his services to implement their product design goals, but to shape future branding goals as well. Past clients have included Stanley, Belkin, IBM, Steelcase, LEGO and Hewlett Packard. Mr. Ewing has been featured speaking about design on PBS and ABC Television, published in international design books and magazines, won international design awards and regularly mentors design students from his alma mater, Cal State Long Beach.

Ewing Design Group is a past winner of the 2001 CES Innovations Design & Engineering Showcase Award for the BELKIN Gold Series Mobile Power Cord. Most recently, his firm was a 2007 INDEX: AWARD Top Nominee for the Cybertech MAT Tourniquet, which also won a Gold IDEA2006 Industrial Design Excellence Award from the IDSA.
