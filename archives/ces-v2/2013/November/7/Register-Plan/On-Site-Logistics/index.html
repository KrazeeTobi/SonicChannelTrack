          So, you’ve registered for the show, selected a hotel and ordered a plane ticket. Before you hit the CES show floor, you’ll want to plan the logistics of your experience on-site, such as getting food, stowing your bags and emergency contact phone numbers. Here are some of the basics...

          Food

          Look for a variety Grab-N-Go kiosks located throughout the show floor. These food stations feature pre-made lunches, snacks and beverages. At the Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC), you can also grab lunch at Lucky's Restaurant, located in the Grand Lobby or Aces, located in the South Hall 1 lobby. You can also find great options and restaurants at the LVH and Renaissance hotels. At The Venetian and Palazzo, you can grab a quick lunch at the food court located on the Casino Level or at the food court located on the Grand Canal Shoppes Level (next to the entrance to the parking garage). There are also many restaurants throughout The Venetian where you can have lunch or dinner, including the Grand Lux Café, located at the base of the Venetian Tower and at a second location in the Palazzo, Casino Level.

          Suitcases and Strollers in the LVCC

          Bag and coat check is available at the FedEx Office locations in the LVCC: Grand Lobby and in the front of South Hall 1. There will also be a coat and bag check in the South Hall 2 lobby. All locations are on a first-come, first-served basis.
           
          Small, two-wheeled bags are permitted. For safety reasons, strollers are not allowed on the show floor. All attendees must be at least 18 years of age.

          Cameras

          All attendees are permitted to bring camera equipment onto the show floor. Exhibitors retain the right to restrict photography of their products or displays and such decisions are within the discretion of the exhibitor and are not controlled by CES.

          Security

          Your safety at the show is important to us. International CES show management employs strict security measures, while still balancing safety concerns with traffic flow challenges. Attendees should allow themselves extra time to arrive at meetings and events, as it may take a little while to pass through the show floor. Here are a few security measures to expect:

          • A personal, government issued ID (driver's license or passport) is required to obtain your badge. Only exhibitors with proper ID will be permitted to pick up multiple badges.

          • All bags will be subject to search upon entry into CES venues starting noon the day before the show opens. This includes personal items such as purses, laptop cases and luggage.

          • Bomb dogs will be on-site as a precautionary measure.

          • There will be an increased presence of Las Vegas Metro Police and Las Vegas Convention and Visitors Authority officers during show days.

          • Surveillance cameras will be installed at key entrances and freight door areas.

          • Private vehicles will only be allowed on LVCC or The Venetian premises to drop off passengers. No standing or unloading will be permitted at any time.

          Emergency Numbers at CES Show Venues – Please Do Not Dial 911

          • LVCC: 702-892-7400 or 7400 from an in-house phone

          • LVH: 702-732-5414 or “0” from an in-house phone

          • Mandalay Bay: 702-632-7911 or 77911 from an in-house phone

          • Renaissance: 702-784-5728 or “0” from an in-house phone

          • The Venetian: 702-414-9311 or 49311 from an in-house phone
