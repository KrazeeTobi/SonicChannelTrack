New Documentary to Premier at CES from the Internet Entrepreneurs Behind Reddit

Dec 21, 2012
After watching the U.S. government threaten the Internet with bills like SOPA and PIPA, Internet entrepreneurs from Reddit, including Alexis Ohanian, co-founder, and Erik Martin, general manager, set out to “campaign for the Internet” during the 2012 presidential election season. Traveling from Denver to Danville, Ky. during the presidential and vice presidential debates, the Internet 2012 Bus Tour explored the potential that the Open Internet holds to promote our economy — not just in Silicon Valley but also in cities and towns (and farms!) all over America. At 2013 CES they will debut their new documentary, Silicon Prairie: America's New Internet Economy. Following the screening, filmmakers and Internet entrepreneurs will discuss why government and citizens alike should care about preserving (and developing) the Internet Economy in every city and every town in America. 2013 CES Brand Matters Keynote Focuses on the Cloud

Dec 13, 2012
The Consumer Electronics Association (CEA) has announced that executives from American Express, AT&T, Coca-Cola, Salesforce.com, Samsung and Unilever will join the Brand Matters keynote at the 2013 International CES. The Brand Matters keynote brings together executives from the world’s top brands to discuss how technology and digital platforms are impacting marketing and brand strategy on a global scale. Kicking off with a fireside chat featuring Michael Kassan, chairman and CEO of MediaLink, and Marc Benioff, chairman and CEO of Salesforce.com, the keynote will then feature a spirited panel discussion, featuring top marketers, on the benefits of the cloud to connect with consumers. The keynote is scheduled for 11 a.m. PT on Tuesday, January 9 in the LVH Theater.
