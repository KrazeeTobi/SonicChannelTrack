12/31/2012

Enabling Safe Driving at the 2013 CES

With the increasing public focus on distracted driving, the Safe Driver TechZone at CES is an amazing opportunity to see new technologies that empower drivers to use their in-vehicle electronics in a safe and responsible way. Safe Driver will feature products that assist in auto collision avoidance, land drift assistance, parking, speed monitoring, hands-free, text-to-voice, driver drowsiness detection, GPS, mapping technologies and much more. A record number of automotive manufacturers will exhibit at the upcoming 2013 International CES.

12/28/2012

Wireless Technology Takes Center Stage at 2013 CES

The 2013 International CES will unveil the latest wireless technologies and trends across the mobile connected universe. With more than 1,200 exhibitors showcasing wireless technologies at the 2013 CES, mobile connected devices are driving the industry as consumers seek to stay connected anywhere, anytime. CEA research shows that smartphone sales will see continued growth in 2013 and will remain the primary revenue driver for the industry. Shipment revenues for smartphones are expected to reach $37 billion in 2013, with more than 125.8 million units shipping to dealers, up 16 percent from 2012. Tablet computers also are expected to see phenomenal growth. In 2013, unit sales of tablets are projected to surpass 105 million (up 54 percent), resulting in $35.6 billion in shipment revenue, up 22 percent year-over-year. [Read more]

12/27/2012

2013 CES Expands Focus on Public Policy

The focus on public policy will grow at the 2013 CES during the Consumer Electronics Association’s Innovation Policy Summit. The summit will host discussions from top government officials and representatives from startups and established consumer electronics companies to highlight the relationship between groundbreaking products and technology policies at the 2013 CES, the world’s largest innovation event. Speakers include representatives from the U.S. Congress, Federal Trade Commission (FTC), Google and Verizon as well as the Chairman and all four Commissioners from the Federal Communications Commission (FCC). "By bringing leading government officials together with individuals from some of America’s greatest and most innovative companies, we hope to encourage a more collaborative future between policy efforts and the technology industry,” said Gary Shapiro, President and CEO, CEA. [Read more]

Born Mobile Topic of Qualcomms Preshow Keynote Address

Mobile has become the largest technology platform in history and it’s about to take center stage at the 2013 International CES. This year’s preshow keynote address will be delivered by Dr. Paul Jacobs, Chairman and CEO of Qualcomm – the world leader in mobile and connectivity technologies, and a company that was “Born Mobile.” Dr. Jacobs will highlight the transformative nature of mobile and the opportunities it presents for the consumer electronics industry, as well as bring on stage some very special guests. Don’t miss this insightful and entertaining kickoff to CES, at 6:30 p.m. Monday, January 7 in The Venetian’s Palazzo Ballroom.

12/24/2012

Consumer Confidence in Technology Reaches Highest Level in History of CEA Index

Consumer confidence in technology reached the highest level in the history of the CEA Index, according to the latest figures released recently by the Consumer Electronics Association (CEA).
 
“Technology products are the most wanted gifts this holiday season, and we’ve seen a tremendous amount of activity, with several new product launches over the past few months,” said Shawn DuBravac, CEA’s chief economist and senior director of research. “Consumers are increasingly dedicating a significant portion of their overall gift budgets to electronics, which has crowded out other categories for holiday gift spending.” [Read more]

12/21/2012

New Documentary to Premier at CES from the Internet Entrepreneurs Behind Reddit

After watching the U.S. government threaten the Internet with bills like SOPA and PIPA, Internet entrepreneurs from Reddit, including Alexis Ohanian, co-founder, and Erik Martin, general manager, set out to “campaign for the Internet” during the 2012 presidential election season. Traveling from Denver to Danville, Ky. during the presidential and vice presidential debates, the Internet 2012 Bus Tour explored the potential that the Open Internet holds to promote our economy — not just in Silicon Valley but also in cities and towns (and farms!) all over America. At 2013 CES they will debut their new documentary, Silicon Prairie: America's New Internet Economy. Following the screening, filmmakers and Internet entrepreneurs will discuss why government and citizens alike should care about preserving (and developing) the Internet Economy in every city and every town in America. [Get more information]

12/13/2012

2013 CES Brand Matters Keynote Focuses on the Cloud

The Consumer Electronics Association (CEA) has announced that executives from American Express, AT&T, Coca-Cola, Salesforce.com, Samsung and Unilever will join the Brand Matters keynote at the 2013 International CES. The Brand Matters keynote brings together executives from the world’s top brands to discuss how technology and digital platforms are impacting marketing and brand strategy on a global scale. Kicking off with a fireside chat featuring Michael Kassan, chairman and CEO of MediaLink, and Marc Benioff, chairman and CEO of Salesforce.com, the keynote will then feature a spirited panel discussion, featuring top marketers, on the benefits of the cloud to connect with consumers. The keynote is scheduled for 11 a.m. PT on Tuesday, January 9 in the LVH Theater.
