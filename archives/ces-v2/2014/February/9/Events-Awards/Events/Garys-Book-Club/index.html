            Presented by Barnes & Noble
            January 7-10
            LVCC, Grand Lobby, Booth # GL6

            Gary's Book Club showcases the works of prominent authors in the tech industry. Attendees have the opportunity to meet the authors, purchase books through Barnes & Noble, and have your copies signed. Book signings will follow lively discussions by the authors on the CEA Stage, all in the LVCC, Grand Lobby.



            Tuesday, January 7

            Larry Downes & Paul Nunes
            Big Bang Distripution: Strategy in the Age of Devastating Innovation
            Interview: 10:30-11 a.m.; Signing: 11 a.m.-Noon

            Based on research by the Accenture Institute for High Performance, this major contribution to business strategy argues that the nature of technology innovation has changed utterly, requiring incumbents to embrace a radical new approach.
             
            The book describes the four new stages of the innovation lifecycle, and offers twelve rules for innovators large and small to survive and thrive in this brave new world.


            Robert Scoble & Shel Israel
            Age of Context: Mobile, Sensors, Data and the Future of Privacy
            Interview: Noon-12:30 p.m.; Signing: 12:30-1:30 p.m.

            Their new book Age of Context examines five converging technology forces: mobile, including wearables; social media, sensors, data, and location technologies that are allowing our devices to know us better than our own spouses do. It examines how these forces impact, work, life and privacy.

            Wednesday, January 8

            Eric Taub
            Does This Plug into That?: Simplify Your Electronic Life
            Interview: 11:30 a.m.-Noon; Signing: Noon-1 p.m.

            Does This Plug into That? demystifies the technologies that can make our lives easier, but usually leave us bewildered, flummoxed, and defeated. It’s the book that finally cracks the geek code, offering practical advice and directions for everything from purchasing a new TV, to setting up a Web connection, installing a home network, and more. Does This Plug into That? is every consumer’s technology Rosetta Stone.

            Nolan Bushnell
            Finding the Next Steve Jobs: How to Find, Hire, Keep, and Nurture Talent
            Interview: 12:30-1 p.m.
            Book Signing: 1-2 p.m.

            Bushnell uses his long-time friendship and mentor-employee relationship with Jobs as an example of how to identify and foster excellent work in brilliant employees.
             


            John Dearie & Courtney Geduldig
            Where the Jobs Are: Entrepreneurship and the Soul of the American Economy
            Interview: 1:30-2 p.m.; Signing: 2-3 p.m.


            New research shows that new businesses – start-ups – account for virtually all net new job creation.  But, alarmingly, the rate of new business formation has declined significantly in recent years.  In Where the Jobs Are, the authors recount what they heard from entrepreneurs at roundtables they conducted across the country, and propose policies to unleash the job-creation power of America’s innovation economy.


            Randi Zuckerberg
            Dot Complicated: Untangling Our Wired Lives
            Signing: 4-5 p.m.; Interview: 5-5:30 p.m.


            Randi Zuckerberg has been on the front lines of technology since her days as an early executive at Facebook. In Dot Complicated, she shares her story with a fresh guide to understanding how technology influences and informs our lives online and off. The book addresses the multifaceted complications that technology brings into our lives today, including issues of social identity, authenticity, and social change.

            Thursday, January 9

            Gary Shapiro
            Ninja Innovation: The Ten Killer Strategies of the World’s Most Successful Businesses
            Interview: 11-11:30 a.m.; Signing: 11:30 a.m.-12:30 p.m.

            Ninja Innovation is the companion piece to The Comeback, spotlighting innovative individuals and businesses in the context of the consumer electronics industry and the International CES. Ninja Innovation looks at what it takes to succeed in business: Discipline. Mission-oriented strategy. Adaptability. Decisiveness. And a will for victory. Today’s most successful executives embrace the same qualities that defined the historical and mythical ninja clans. They have become “ninja innovators.” And like the disciplined, determined Japanese warriors, the ninja innovators examined in the book recognize that above all, the choice is to innovate or die! 


            Gary Beach
            The U.S. Technology Skills Gap: What Every Technology Executive Must Know to Save America’s Future
            Interview: 1-1:30 p.m.; Signing: 1:30-2:30 p.m.

            For nearly 50 years American students have lagged behind the rest of the world in math and science. Why is that the case? It certainly isn’t genetics. Why can’t a nation that invests $600 billion in education do better? “The U.S.Technology Skills Gap” connects the dots and warns why this situation threatens America’s future.
