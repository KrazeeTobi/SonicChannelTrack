                      Get the Publicity Your Company Deserves

                      More than 5,000 media are expected to attend the 2015 International CES, generating unprecedented coverage via newspaper and magazine articles, television and radio reports, online stories and up to the second social media posts. Follow these guidelines, tips and samples to garner the publicity your company deserves.

                      CES Exhibitor Attire Policy

                      CES Show Management reserves the right to make determinations on appropriate exhibitor/presenter attire. If for any reason an exhibit and/or its contents are deemed objectionable by Show Management, the exhibitor may be asked to alter the attire of its employees, exhibit staff and/or models. If necessary, the exhibitor may be asked to remove the individual(s) in question at the exhibitor’s sole expense. Exhibitors with questions about compliance with these guidelines should consult with Show Management in advance of the show.

                      Press Conference Dos & Don’ts

                      Do...

                      • Submit your 2015 CES press conference request form by December 19, 2014
                      • Establish clear and precise newsworthy objectives for your event
                      • Book the appropriate space in a setting that will enhance delivery
                      • Identify and invite targeted media to your event using our pre-registered media list, available November 1, 2014 to exhibitors hosting press conferences
                      • Promote your event through targeted press releases, calls and pitches
                      • Send invitations to your targeted media one month before CES; follow up two weeks prior to the event
                      • Have staff on site to help with registration and seating
                      • Notify CEA staff if your event is invitation-only
                      • Have a lead retrieval unit or business card collector at the door to see who attends your event
                      • Distribute product spec sheets and digital press kits
                      • Upload brochures, press kits, press releases and other materials along with your company logo to the 2015 CES Mobile App
                      • Allow for Q&A time at the end of your event
                      • Feel free to invite press to your booth for more information

                      Don't...

                      • Leak major product announcements or news before your press conference
                      • Start Late
                      • End Late
                      • Read straight from a Power Point
                      • Schedule a press conference if you have no news
                      • Eliminate Q&A with media
                      • Hold a conference that lasts more than 45 minutes
                      • Expect Media to attend without proactive work on your part
                      • Schedule an event that conflicts with any of the CES Keynotes

                      Publicity Tips

                      Follow these tips to garner the publicity your company deserves:

                      1) Media Positioning - CES defines innovation. Develop press materials that capture this innovative spirit and reflect how your company is on the cutting-edge of forward thinking.

                      2) Announce New Products at CES - Debut new products at the show to generate global publicity. Consider a CES exhibitor press conference to showcase your latest innovations.

                      3) Name Drop CES - Use your exhibitor status in your announcements and benefit from CES branding. Don’t forget to list your 2015 CES exhibit number and venue so media can find you at the show.

                      4) Use Social Media - CES has an abundance of social media tools available to its exhibitors. Make sure you take advantage of our outlets, to peak interest and increase your outreach and coverage.

                      5) Use the Web - Post releases via our Online Media Center or at your own URL.

                      6) Media Materials - CES is the hotspot for the consumer electronics industry, with more than 20,000 anticipated product launches. Make sure your press kit materials stand out from the competition. View press kit tips.

                      7) Exhibitor Profile - Update your exhibitor profile to identify PR reps that will be available for media interviews.

                      8) Think TV - Make your pitches visually appealing and be practiced and prepared to present to television journalists. CESTV coverage increases annually, with more programs reporting directly from the show floor every year. If you would like to be included in these promotions, fill out the CESTV Coverage Consideration Form.

                      9) Tell Your Local Media - Announce to local media that you’ll be at the 2015 CES. Local newspapers will be interested in your upcoming announcements. For maximum coverage, participate in our Regional Media Outreach Campaign (details under Media Outreach Opportunities).

                      10) International CES Promotions - We want to keep your company’s name in front of industry decision makers. CEA promotes the CE industry and CES all year long. View all 2015 CES promotional opportunities.

                      • The dateline should include date and location (Las Vegas, if being released during show).

                      Other tips:

                      • Deal with the facts.

                      • Do not make the release sound like a sale pitch.

                      • Keep it simple.

                      • Think and write like a reporter.

                      • Make sure the piece can stand on its own and does not need further background.

                      • Bullet points clearly display information.

                      • Leave white space – Clutter is not good.


                      Social Media Posts

                      Sample Tweet
                      When writing a Tweet about CES from your company’s Twitter account, we encourage the following:

                      • When writing about CES either tag our handle (@intlCES) or use our official hashtag #CES2015.

                      • Use only our official hashtag. #CES can mean many things in both English and other languages, using our official hashtag is the best way to ensure your tweets are seen by those searching for information about the show.

                      • Include a photo or link to an article or video for readers to learn more about your tweet.

                      Check out CES Tweets.
                       

                      Sample Facebook Post
                      When writing posts about CES in Facebook on your page and on ours, we encourage the following:

                      • Writing posts that reflect a continuing positive dialogue with our community.

                      • Tag our Facebook page in a post about CES (examples below show our page doing this with Qualcomm and TAO Nightclub).

                      • Use a picture or video when possible to help further engagement.

                      • Respond to questions and comments that your community has for the post.

                      Check out CES Facebook posts


                      Sample Blog Post
                      When writing blog posts related to CES, we encourage:

                      • Writing for a business-to-business audience and of relevance to professionals. This can range from emerging trends in the marketplace to innovative ways retailers are designing their stores.

                      • Use a personal tone that is casual but keep in mind that you are writing for business professionals with an interest in and passion for the CE industry.

                      • Short is good – aim for no more than 4-5 paragraphs (approx. word count: 350-600 words).

                      • Fill your posts with visually representative photos from the experience pulling the reader through the copy.

                      • Don’t feel like you have to include paragraphs in every post. You can also promote videos, infographics and other engaging and fun pieces of content. There are a number of ways you can tell your story.
