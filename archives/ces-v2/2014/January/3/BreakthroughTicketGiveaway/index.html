            Consumer Electronics Association 2014 CES Breakthrough Awards Ticket Giveaway Official Rules
             
            2014 CES BREAKTHROUGH AWARDS TICKET GIVEAWAY sponsored by cea is A Contest open only to legal u.s. residents 18 years of age or older, WHO RESIDE IN THE u.S. AT THE TIME OF ENTRY. VOID WHEREVER PROHIBITED.

            1.       Contest Dates: The Contest begins 12:00:01 PM EST January 2, 2014, and will be open until 2:59:59 PM EST on January 3, 2014.
             
            2.       Contest Sponsor: Consumer Electronics Association (“CEA”), 1919 S. Eads St., Arlington, VA 22202. Prize Provider: CEA.
             
            3.       Eligibility: Contestants may submit one (1) answer per question, per day, but may only win once throughout the entire Contest. This Contest is open only to persons who, as of date of entry are legal residents of one of the fifty (50) United States or the District of Columbia, at least 18 years of age or older (19 years of age or older in Alabama and Nebraska) attending the 2014 International CES. Employees of CEA, its subsidiaries, sales representatives, advertising, promotion and all other service agencies involved with design, execution or fulfillment of this Contest, and members of the immediate families (defined for these purposes as parents, children, siblings and spouse) or households (whether related or not) of any of the above are NOT eligible to participate.
             
            4.       How to Enter: To enter this Contest, contestants must visit Twitter during the Contest Dates (see above). CEA will post a question on CEA’s Twitter account at approximately 12:00:01 PM EST both days. Contestants must submit their answers by tweeting at @intlCES before 2:59:59 PM EST each day throughout the Contest period. Contestants will only be allowed to submit one (1) answer per question each day, and CEA will only count contestants’ first submission if multiple submissions from one user are submitted. No third party entry or entry through sweepstakes service permitted. Entries deemed by CEA in their sole discretion to be immoral, obscene, indecent, profane or not in keeping with CEA’s image will be disqualified. CEA decisions are final and binding.
             
            5.       Winner Selection: Five (5) winners will be selected each day during the Contest Dates. Winners will be randomly drawn from the group of correct entries that are submitted during the time of the Contest for that day by 3:29:59 PM EST. The random drawing will be conducted by CEA. Winners will be announced the same day of when each question is posted. Odds of winning depend on the number of eligible entries received. Designation as a prize winner is subject to proof of compliance with Official Rules, maintaining compliance with these Official Rules and approval by CEA. As a condition of winning the prize, winners will be required to supply contact information including, but not limited to name, phone number and email address. Personal information will be used solely to distribute prizes and will not be used for marketing purposes. Prizes not won and claimed by an eligible winner in accordance with these Official Rules will not be awarded and will remain the property of CEA. For a list of winners (available after January 3, 2014), send your request with a self-addressed stamped envelope to Don Schaefer, Consumer Electronics Association, 1919 S. Eads Street, Arlington, VA 22202.
             
            6.       Prize:  Each winner will be awarded two (2) tickets to the Breakthrough Awards at CES. Total maximum ARV of all prizes: $1000. Prizes are not transferable. No cash redemptions. No substitutions, except that CEA reserves the right to substitute a prize of equal or greater value. All prizes will be awarded provided they are properly claimed in accordance with these Official Rules and a sufficient number of eligible entries are received. Limit one (1) prize per household. If a prize winner is 18 but deemed a minor in his/her state of residence, the prize will be awarded on behalf of the minor to his/her parent or legal guardian, who must comply with the prize claim requirements.
