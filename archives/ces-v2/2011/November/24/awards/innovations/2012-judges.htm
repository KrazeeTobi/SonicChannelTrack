2012 Innovations Judges



Eyal Akler
Omrani &Taub P.C.
Director of Technology

With over 20 years in the technology industry, Eyal is a serial entrepreneur and a bona fide techie. As CEO he has helped grow a small gaming PC company into a multi-million dollar luxury PC brand that was acquired by a publicly-traded, Silicon Valley firm in 2007. Following that, Eyal was chosen to head the North American re-launch of the famous Commodore PC brand. His award-winning PC designs have been praised and featured on TV and magazine covers. He has since been lending his creativity and keen eye for details to a number of successful companies.


Gary Arlen
Arlen Communications, Inc.
Editor and Commentator/Columnist

 

Gary Arlen is President of Arlen Communications LLC, a Washington-area research and consulting firm specializing in converging digital media, telecommunications and information services. His work focuses on technology and policies that affect applications, including devices and delivery systems. Details at www.ArlenCom.com.

 


Stephen Baker
NPD
Vice President of Industry Analysis for Consumer Technology

 

Stephen Baker is NPD’s Vice President of Industry Analysis for Consumer Technology.  Baker offers expert insight and commentary to clients and the media about consumer technology sales and trends across the U.S. and regionally, including retail and e-commerce, computer hardware and peripherals, holiday and yearly sales results, and analysis of new products and their impact on the consumer technology market. Baker is available to offer expert insight and commentary on these and many other industry-related topics:

  • Consumer technology retail and e-commerce sales
  • Retailer and manufacturer sales trends
  • PCs and related technologies
  • Printing and imaging markets

Baker has been tracking and analyzing the industry for nearly 15 years.  He was ranked as a leading industry analyst by AdWeek’s Technology Marketing. 

Baker is quoted extensively in industry publications such as TWICE, CNET, and CE Daily, as well as business and news publications including The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, USA Today, Investor’s Business Daily, Business Week, and The San Jose Mercury News.  He has also appeared on CNBC, Fox News, and CNN.

Baker has presented and served on panels at industry events such as RetailVision, and CES.

He began his career at Staples in 1985, helping to open the first office superstore in the United States. Baker spent nine years with the company where he gained the extensive knowledge and understanding of retail and the office supplies and consumer technology industries.  

Baker joined NPD when it acquired PC Data Inc., in March 2001, where he was the principal hardware analyst and director of the overall hardware point-of-sale business. Prior to joining PC Data, he was a senior analyst in the distribution channels service at International Data Corporation (IDC).

Baker received his MBA from Northeastern University and a BSFS from Georgetown University.


Robert Baranowski
Park-Tours, Inc.
Founder and President

 

Robert Baranowski founded Park-Tours in 1999, designing software for PDA's and later cell phones for providing theme park guests information about the park, line bypassing, current wait times, scheduling, shopping and more. Since then, with Baranowski's lead technical role, the company has evolved into an engineering and technology company, with products ranging from wireless soil monitoring systems to high-end audio and room-measuring surround sound to portable IV pumps and hand-held wireless inventory counters. In 2008, Rob set up the Left Coast Engineering dba to separate the consulting work from the theme park software.

Prior to founding Park-Tours, Rob designed hardware and RF for Motorola and Sony cellular. Before starting his wireless career, Rob spent over four years designing software systems for many divisions of Six Flags Great Adventure, one of the largest parks in the Six Flags system. He has 14 patents issued and an additional 19 filed.

Victor Berrios
Nomomoto Group
Managing Director  

Victor Berrios is Managing Director of the Nomomoto Group, a Phoenix-based consulting firm focusing on embedded systems and standards development. With over 18 years of experience in the wireless communication industry (primary with Motorola and Freescale Semiconductor), Victor is a recognized expert in the short range wireless industry as evidenced by his contributions to the RF4CE Network, ZigBee Remote Control, ZigBee Input Device, ZigBee Healthcare, and ZigBee Low Power End Device specifications. He has served as chairman of the ZigBee RF4CE Compliance Working Group and Technical Editor for the ZigBee Low Power End Device Specification. He was Recognized by the Continua Health Alliance as its Spring 2011 Key Contributor in recognition for his efforts to the success of the of the Test and Certification Work Group.

Victor's engineering experience has been focused on the development of deeply embedded systems using 8, 16 and 32-bit processing architectures. While at Freescale, he led the global R&D organization responsible for the development of IEEE 802.15.4 compliant radio solutions. He built and managed an international software engineering organization responsible for the development, deployment and support of wireless connectivity solutions.

Victor received his bachelor's of science in computer engineering from Iowa State University, his master's in electrical engineering from Arizona State University (ASU, and his master's in business administration from ASU.

Robert C. Chapin
Andrew Seybold, Inc 
Partner

He has conducted hundreds of focus groups for technology-based organizations with an emphasis on product design and consumer demands and opinions. Bob is the creator of RapiData, the first implementation of PDA-based methodology providing real-time reporting of mission-critical, actionable data at live events such as tradeshows, conferences, college campuses and retail shopping malls. Bob began his wireless telecom career in 1987 providing marketing and advertising consulting to the land mobile communications market. He has moderated or presented on more than 100 wireless telecommunications and mobile computing panels, including the International Consumer Electronics Show, the Korean Chamber of Commerce (Seoul), and the Latin American Cellular Telecommunications Association (ALACEL in Mexico City). Bob is a Fellow of the Radio Club of America.
