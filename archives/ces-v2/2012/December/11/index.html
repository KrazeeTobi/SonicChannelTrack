Schedule Your Global Entry Interview at CES

 Are you a U.S. citizen or foreign national from select countries who frequently travels internationally? If so, apply for the Global Entry Program and schedule your interview at CES! Global Entry is a program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States.  Though intended for frequent international travelers, there is no minimum number of trips necessary to qualify for the program.  Participants may enter the United States by using automated kiosks located at select airports. The U.S. Customs and Border Protection Agency will be at CES January 8 and 9 to conduct interviews. To participate, you must complete the application, schedule your interview at FOLA January 2013 and pay the application fee by December 12. Click here for complete details.

11/20/2012

Taking the Pulse of Holiday Shopping Season

The holiday shopping season is quickly approaching.  According to the Consumer Electronics Association (CEA)’s 19th Annual  CE Holiday Purchase Patterns Study, overall holiday spending is expected to increase by 11 percent and spending on consumer electronics (CE) gifts is also likely to increase. Consumer electronics, like tablets and smartphones, continue to be some of the most wanted gifts and three in four holiday gift givers plan on purchasing a consumer electronic product this holiday season.
 
Throughout the next week, CEA’s Market Research team will be gathering information on holiday spending, beginning on Black Friday and continuing through the weekend. Check out CEA’s Holiday Shopping Season page for more information.

11/8/2012

Tablet Computer Ownership Continues to Grow, CEA Research Reports

U.S. ownership rates for tablet computers continued to increase in the third quarter of 2012, according to recent research released by CEA. The Consumer Outlook on Tablets: Q4 2012 shows that tablet ownership rates among online U.S. consumers reached 31 percent as of September 2012, more than doubling the ownership rate from October 2011(14 percent). “We expect tablets to play a big role in the upcoming holiday shopping season; not only in sales but also in complementary purchases such as accessories and gift cards," said Kevin Tillman, senior research analyst, CEA.

10/30/2012

Digital Health and Fitness Technology Expands by Nearly 25 percent

Interest in digital health and fitness technology is red hot, and the category has grown by nearly 25 percent at the 2013 International CES, the Consumer Electronics Association announced this week. More than 215 exhibitors will showcase devices beyond weight, body mass index, exercise and calorie tracking, accounting for 27,000 net square feet of exhibit space. The next generation of health and fitness devices and apps offer access to complete medical history, comprehensive biometric data and remote access to medical professionals. Over the next five years, wearable devices in sports and fitness will grow to 169.5 million devices, with the body becoming an input/output device where everything can be tracked.

10/26/2012

Auto Industry Booms at 2013 International CES

Seven of the top 10 automobile manufacturers will showcase their latest technologies on the CES show floor at this year's CES. Audi, Chrysler, Ford, General Motors, Hyundai, Kia and Subaru will join more than 110 automotive tech companies displaying their technologies and services on the show floor as consumers increasingly demand technology innovation within their automobiles. CEA industry forecasts predict sales of factory-installed vehicle technologies will increase by 11.3 percent in 2013 to nearly $8.7 billion, and CEA consumer research shows approximately 15 percent of U.S. households now own a vehicle with a connected communication/entertainment system.
