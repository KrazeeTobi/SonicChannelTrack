International Gateway Sells Out at The Venetian

The Consumer Electronics Association (CEA) announced today that the International Gateway TechZone has sold out in its new location, The Venetian, at the 2013 International CES.

The International Gateway allows attendees to expand their global networks at CES. The TechZone will feature more than 460 exhibitors in Hall D of The Venetian, showcasing innovative technologies and products from international destinations, including China, Korea, Hong Kong and Taiwan. Due to the sell out in Hall D, International Gateway exhibits will also be found on Level 1 at the Venetian, in the Marco Polo ballroom.

Consumer Electronics Association, Google to Share Insights from 2012 Debates

The Consumer Electronics Association (CEA) and Google are partnering on “Insights from the 2012 Debates,” a joint effort to provide voters with insightful and compelling information about the presidential and vice presidential debates using Google’s innovative technologies. It will debut during tonight’s first debate in Denver.

The effort includes a state-of-the-art digital wall that displays search and video trends, insights and consumer research. The digital wall features the top search trends around the debates and user-generated content from Google+. Check out Google’s election hub for more information on the 2012 election.

DBL Distributing Founder David Lorsch is Finalist for 2012 Executive of the Year Award

When he started DBL Distributing in 1989, David Lorsch saw an exciting business opportunity to serve an untouched market in the West. The first annual Innovation Entrepreneur Awards, sponsored by the Consumer Electronics Association's (CEA) new flagship publication It Is Innovation (i3) and CEA's Small Business Council, honors excellence in entrepreneurship and recognizes visionaries leading businesses with revenues under $30 million. Winners will be announced at the upcoming CEA Industry Forum in San Francisco.

8/13/2012

Call for Entries Open for Innovations 2013 Design and Engineering Awards Honoring Best in CE Innovation

The industry-recognized annual competition honors outstanding design and engineering across 29 consumer electronics categories. Two new product categories were added to the 2013 awards program: Tech for a Better World and Accessible and Universal Design Technologies. Tech for a Better World recognizes technologies that share a common goal and the ability to impact the world in a positive way, either domestically, or around the globe. Accessible and Universal Design Technologies focuses on technologies that have innovative features that enable ease of use by anyone, including, but not limited to, seniors and people with disabilities, regardless of cognitive ability, hearing, mobility, visual or other impairments.
