Consumer Electronics Sales Expected to Grow this Holiday Season

The Consumer Electronics Association (CEA) recently released its 19th Annual CE Holiday Purchase Patterns Study at this week’s Industry Forum in San Francisco. The study found that consumer electronics continue to be the most desired gifts. These gifts also account for almost a third of all gift spending. The most coveted gifts for this year are mobile devices, tablets, and laptops.

“Consumers are feeling more confident in the overall direction of the economy this holiday season compared to last year and, as a result, will spend more on gifts, including electronics,” said Shawn DuBravac, CEA’s chief economist and senior director of research. “CE continues to play a leading role in holiday shopping and this year is no exception, as consumers will dedicate a large portion of their overall gift budget towards electronics, specifically mobile connected devices.”

Check back at the end of November for CEA’s Black Friday sales study reflecting consumers’ post-Thanksgiving shopping habits! Click here to learn more.

“Speakers are what I do. It’s my art.”

After a career that included creating two successful loudspeaker companies, Sandy Gross contemplated retirement. But he couldn’t leave the work he loved. “Speakers are what I do. International Gateway Sells Out at The Venetian

The Consumer Electronics Association (CEA) announced today that the International Gateway TechZone has sold out in its new location, The Venetian, at the 2013 International CES.

The International Gateway allows attendees to expand their global networks at CES. The TechZone will feature more than 460 exhibitors in Hall D of The Venetian, showcasing innovative technologies and products from international destinations, including China, Korea, Hong Kong and Taiwan. Due to the sell out in Hall D, International Gateway exhibits will also be found on Level 1 at the Venetian, in the Marco Polo ballroom.

Consumer Electronics Association, Google to Share Insights from 2012 Debates

The Consumer Electronics Association (CEA) and Google are partnering on “Insights from the 2012 Debates,” a joint effort to provide voters with insightful and compelling information about the presidential and vice presidential debates using Google’s innovative technologies. It will debut during tonight’s first debate in Denver.

The effort includes a state-of-the-art digital wall that displays search and video trends, insights and consumer research. The digital wall features the top search trends around the debates and user-generated content from Google+. Check out Google’s election hub for more information on the 2012 election.
