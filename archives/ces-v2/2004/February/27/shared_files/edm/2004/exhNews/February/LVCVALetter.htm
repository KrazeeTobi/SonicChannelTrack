A Message From The Las Vegas Convention and Visitors Authority


January 21, 2004

Dear International CES Attendee,

Thank you for joining us in Las Vegas this past week for the 2004 International CES. Las Vegas was thrilled to host this event, and we hope you enjoyed the destination’s hospitality and experienced some of its legendary excitement during your stay.

We are aware that some of you experienced delays leaving McCarran International Airport due to security procedures, and we regret any inconvenience you faced. During your next visit to Las Vegas, we anticipate that such delays will be eliminated as a result of the following factors:

  • By the end of this year, McCarran Airport will have completed construction of eight additional checkpoint lanes for the C and D gates.
  • The staff of McCarran Airport and their Federal Security Director are pursuing implementation of off-site baggage check-in.
As successful as the 2004 International CES was, we predict even greater things for next year’s show. Be assured that Las Vegas is always developing new ways to make its visitors’ experience more enjoyable and hassle-free. Next year, the Las Vegas Monorail will be in operation, affording 2005 International CES delegates easier transportation between the Las Vegas Convention Center and area hotels.

We look forward to welcoming you back in 2005—or sooner.

Sincerely,


Rossi Ralenkotter
Executive Vice President
Las Vegas Convention and Visitors Authority
rralenkotter@lvcva.com


P.S. Mark your calendar for January 6-9, 2005, and let the International CES notify you when registration opens for the 2005 International CES.
