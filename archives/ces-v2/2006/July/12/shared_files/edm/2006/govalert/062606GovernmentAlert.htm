Telecom Bill Passes Full House
On June 8, the full House passed H.R. 5252, the Communications  Opportunity, Promotion, and Enhancement Act of 2006.  The vote for passage was 321-101.  As passed, the bill establishes a nationwide franchise process for telecommunications companies to enter the video services marketplace, maintains application of Section 629 (securing manufacturers ability to build and sell devices that attached to new IP-enabled video services) and gives the FCC authority to adjudicate complaints and violations of the net neutrality principles.

NY Agency Preparing Recommendations for Energy Limits on CE Products
On June 29, consultants for the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority (NYSERDA) will present research and recommendations for energy consumption standards for televisions, compact audio products, DVD players and recorders, digital television adapters, and external power supplies.  NYSERDA was directed to develop energy standards for several product categories as a result of legislation passed by the state legislature and signed by the governor last year.  CEA is actively involved in NYSERDAs regulatory process.  CEA is also revisiting the issue with key legislators.  The legislation enacted last year was not carefully debated or considered, and since that time, there have been significant developments relevant to the question of energy standards for CE products.  CEA is asking its members with a presence in the State of New York to be prepared to support the associations efforts, possibly by attending a future meeting with key policy makers, or submitting comments.

Contact: Doug Johnson


CEA Launches Study on the Energy Consumption of Consumer Electronics
On many occasions, CEA and its members have noted the use and publication of inconsistent and misleading estimates of energy consumption for consumer electronics, both on a product category level, and overall.  Many estimates of CE energy consumption rely upon data developed in the late 1990s.  When California regulated the energy use of several consumer audio and video product categories, the state relied on old power draw values and educated guesses for product operating time.  Many CE products have changed dramatically over the last decade, as have their energy consumption characteristics (due to technological change and the success of the Energy Star program).  It is critical that the CE industry develop high-quality estimates and a current baseline for the energy consumption of consumer electronics.  The findings will have significant value to CEAs public policy, communications and technical initiatives related to energy efficiency.  CEA has engaged an outside consulting firm, TIAX LLC, to assist with this project, which was initiated earlier this month and is expected to be completed in September.

Contact: Doug Johnson

Computer Sales Tax Holidays Continue to Spread
The list of states that have created temporary sales tax exemptions for computer purchases continues to grow, with new tax holidays scheduled in Alabama and Tennessee this summer, in addition to Georgia, Missouri, New Mexico, North Carolina, and South Carolina.  Since CEA began promoting this concept six years ago, 11 states have enacted such programs.  Legislation to create or renew sales tax holidays is pending in Massachusetts and Rhode  Island.  For details on sales tax holidays for computer products in 2006, see www.ce.org/taxholiday.

Contact:  Doug Johnson
