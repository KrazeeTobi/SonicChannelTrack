CEA Files Amicus Brief in XM Lawsuit
CEA and the Home Recording Rights Coalition (HRRC) filed a joint amicus curiae supporting XM's motion to dismiss the lawsuit brought by the record labels. The motion requests that the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of New York dismiss the lawsuit filed May 16 by the recording industry.

Ban on Family-Friendly DVDs Favors
Copyright Owners Over Consumers
On July 6, Senior U.S. District Court Judge Richard Matsch of the U.S. District Court for the District of Colorado ruled against CleanFlicks, a business that bought DVDs of movies and removed obscene and offensive portions and then resold the movies. The court found that CleanFlicks violated the copyright law and must cease operations. According to CEA President and CEO Gary Shapiro, "This is an example of the copyright laws going awry. The movie industry suffers no economic harm. The defendant, CleanFlicks, purchased a DVD for every movie that it edited and sold to parents. Mr. Shapiro went on to say, "This has nothing to do with piracy. This is all about further limiting fair use rights of consumers to make choices of what they view, when they view it and how they view it."

Additional Links: Press Release 07/11/06

Contact: Michael Petricone

CEA Sends Letter to Senate Regarding DTV Border Station Language
On July 17, CEA sent a letter to the full Senate expressing concerns with language in the telecom reform bill regarding Spanish-language stations and the DTV transition. Specifically, CEA is concerned about the late addition of a provision that would extend the DTV transition date for Spanish-language stations on the U.S. border to February 17, 2009.

Contact: Julie Kearney

CEA Enthusiastic About Participating in NTIA's Proceeding on the Digital Television Converter Box
On July 24, NTIA released a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking (NPRM), which seeks comments on ways to implement the digital-to-analog converter box coupon program pursuant to the Digital Television Transition and Public Safety Act of 2005.

According to CEA President and CEO, Gary Shapiro, "This NPRM marks an important milestone in the nation's transition to digital television. Thus far the transition to DTV has been extremely successful. CEA forecast that U.S. consumers will purchase more than 18 million DTV sets and displays this year, marking a 50 percent increase over 2005 sales. 2006 will also be the year that high-definition television (HDTV) outsells analog television set units."

Additional Links: Press Release 07/25/06

Contact: Julie Kearney

SINOCES 2006 Ends on High Note
The fifth annual China Consumer Electronics Show (SINOCES) was held July 7-10 in Qingdao, China. The event, which was co-sponsored by CEA, included more than 390 exhibitors and more than 62,000 attendees. According to Todd Thibodeaux, CEA's Senior Vice President, Industry Relations, "CEA's partnership with the China Electronic Chamber of Commerce (CECC) and SINOCES paves a road for our members to explore new international business opportunities and allows us to promote our tradeshow, the International CES, to an international audience."

Washington State Issues Draft Rule on Electronic Product Recycling
The Washington Department of Ecology has issued the first set of rules on Electronic Product Recycling (WAC 173-900) related to the new Washington State law, which was passed by the State Legislature during the 2006 session. The rule will be published in the Washington State Register on August 2 and comments are due by September 7.

The notice includes information about the public comment period, public hearings and a link to their rule development website.

Additional Links: Notice for Publication: Draft Electronics Rule

CEA Commends EPA Ruling on Cathode Ray Tube Recycling
On July 19, the Environmental Protection Agency issued a ruling to improve standards for cathode ray tube (CRT) recycling. According to CEA Senior Director and Environmental Counsel, Parker Brugge, "Yesterday's ruling is an important step towards increased electronics recylcing and resource recovery on a national scale. To promote legitimate recylcing of CRTs, we encourage the states to implement rules consistent with the EPA's regulation."

CEA Scores Victory in Massachusetts
HB 5112, a bill pending in the Massachusetts legislature, includes a provision for mercury-added products. On July 13, Massachusetts Governor Mitt Romney struck the word "removable" from the bill, which would make CEA products exempt from the legislation.

Contact: Parker Brugge
