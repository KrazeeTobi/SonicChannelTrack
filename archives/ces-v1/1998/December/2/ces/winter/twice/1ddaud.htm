High-End Audio/Video Firms
Showcase Their Wares At CES

High-end audio and video products are well represented at this year's Winter CES. Here is a quick review of some of the companies and their products. Denon showing its DVD-2000 DVD player along with a number of other home and car audio products during Winter CES.

In home audio, is is showing the AVR-3200 Dolby Digital receiver, the AVR-2400 Dolby Ready/Dolby Pro Logic receiver and the AVR-1400 Dolby Digital Ready/Dolby Pro Logic receiver.

In car audio, Denon introduces several products featuring RDS Smart Radio, including the DCT-1OOOR CD Tuner with RDS Smart Radio and Radio Text, 20-bit D/A converters and an ALPHA processor; and three cassette receiver/changer controllers, the DCR-67ORD, the DCR-570 and the DCR-47ORD.

Also new is Denon's the DCT-670 CD receiver, the DCT-770 CD receiver/changer controller, and two 12-disc CD changers the DCH-47ORF CD changer RF package, which includes a wireless remote control, and the DCH-470 CD changer.

In multimedia, Denon offers the DRD-1408 200-disc Dual Transport CD-ROM Drive with 8x speed, and a prototype of the PMA-PCX Digital Amplifier.

Meridian will also show a DVD player among the following:
  • Model 800 reference CD machine, which is a fully digital and uses CD-ROM drives and can also serve as a CD-ROM reader, a CD changer and a CD recorder;

  • Model 861 reference Surround Processor, a digital unit with DTS and MPEG capability, the unit is a full-system controller preamp that can control up to 12 analog or digital audio sources and 12 video sources;

  • The 561 Surround Sound preamplifier offers more convenient programming and more aggressive pricing-and will be shown with DTS capability;

  • A new 565 Surround Sound Controller updated with DTS capability.
Celestion is introducing the A Series, a trio of speakers developed with laser interferometry techniques and technology based on studies of drive unit behavior. The series, which replaces the highly SL line, consists of the Al, a two-way bookshelf design capable of handling 100 Watts; the A2, a two-and-a-half-way compact floorstaing model capable of 150 Watts; and the A3, a large three-way floorstanding model capable of 200 Watts. All are available in black ash, cherry and rosewood finishes.

Also shown will be prototypes of A Series center and surround speakers, and a subwoofer. Delivery and pricing information were not available at press time.

KEF will introduce and showcase several significant groups of speakers:
  • The Monitor Series, enhancing the heritage of the SL3/5a, consists of the RDM One and RDM Two-bookshelf-size near-field monitors employing KEF's "Uni-Q" technology for enhancements a broad sweet spot, smooth off-axis response and enhanced imaging, MSRPs for the RDM One and Two are $900/pair and $1,200/pair, respectively.

  • The Q Series, which includes the QIS, Q35, QSS, Q65, Q75, and Q95C Center Channel. All share a new Uni-Q "core" composed of a 6.5-inch mid/woofer of exceptionally light, stiff polypropylene and a 3/4-inch soft-dome tweeter.

  • Also in this series is the successor to KEF's B139 bass drive-the B169, an oval "racetrack" bass driver.

    Suggested retail prices for these products are: $349/pair for the Q15; $599/pair for the Q33; $799/pair for the Q55; $1,199/pair for the Q65; $1,499/pair for the Q75; and $369 each for the Q95c THX Home Theater System.

    Shown in prototype, this THX approved system consists of left and right front speakers, and a horizontal center channel speaker, using dual B169 "racetrack" bass drivers. DS2 Dipole Surround Speakers also use the new B169 bass unit and Uni-Q technology for excellent detail and a wide sound field dispersion. The system's 45B Subwoofer offers a 15-inch driver complemented by a 300-Watt amplifier in a closed box design.
NAD will showcase the Model 118 digital preamplifier featuring DSP which will optimize two-channel frequency response, dynamic range, and stereo-imaging. Key features include a dynamic 18-bit A-to-D system, five DSP modes, four analog and four digital inputs, and a tape-direct switch for analog dubbing. The suggested retail is $1,599.

The Model AV 711 Dolby Pro Logic receiver features left/center/right channels and a 20-watt surround amplifier deliver 115 Watts front-channel dynamic output and drives reactive, 4-Ohm loudspeakers. Other key features include a full-function remote and NAD-Link compatibility. Suggested retail is $499.

The Model 710 stereo receiver produces 80 Watts dynamic power while its all-discrete design yields 20 Amps peak-current capability and can deliver power to reactive, low-impedance speaker loads. Included is NAD's 412 AM/FM tuner section for outstanding FM signal quality. Suggested retail is $299.

Niles is showing the IntelliControl Home Theater Automation System and two new home theater in-wall speakers, the HD-CTR center channel and the HD-LRS main/surround speaker.

The Home Theater Automation System consists of two priraary components, a 44-button tabletop backlighted controller; and the IntelliControl Main System Unit, a behind-the-scenes microprocessor controlled "brain" that constantly monitors both the theater components and the tabletop remote, the firm said. The suggested retail is $1,195.

The HD-CTR shielded center-channel in-wall speaker utilizes dual 5-1/4-inch polypropylene woofer/midrange drivers in conjuction with the tweeter. Baffle mounted adjustment switches enable installers to custom configure response to match the installation, the firm said.. Suggested retail price is $299.95 each.

The HD-LRS utilizes the same driver configuration in an unshielded pair pack. A crossover mounted jumper switches the speaker's phase response from main speaker to Surround speaker. Baffle mounted adjustment switches enable additional customization. Suggested retail is $549.95 a pair.
