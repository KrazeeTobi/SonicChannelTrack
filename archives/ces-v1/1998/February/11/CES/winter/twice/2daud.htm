High-End A/V Manufacturers
Unveil New Products At CES

Here is another quick review of some of the high-end audio and video products that are being showcased or introduced for the first time during the Winter Consumer Electronics Show.

PSB introduces two new lines of speakers, the Century Series and the Alpha Family.

The Century Series is a line of eight speakers for listeners committed to high performance in products designed for modern living rooms, the company said.

Scaled to meet different needs and budgets, Century Series speakers include: three bookshelf-size systems-the Century 300i ($299/pair), the Century 400i ($399/pair) and the Century 500i ($499/pair); two dual-woofer tower models-the Century 600i ($599/pair) and the Century Booi ($799/pair); and the flagship Century 1000 [$999/pair), featuring a unique two-and-a-half way design. The Series also includes two center channel speakers designed for Home Theaters the Century 10OCi ($179/pair) and the Century 20OCi ($299/pair).

The new Alpha Family includes the Alpha A/V, the Alpha Mini and the Alpha Mite, "Compact Monitors" that share the original's ability to reproduce music at low cost. Additional Alphas include: the Alpha Midi, a center-channel speaker using the same drivers and cabinet as the Mini, the firm said.

Suggested retail prices are: $249/pair for the Alpha A/V; $199/pair for the Alpha Mini; $169/pair for the Alpha Mite; $109 each for the Alpha Midi; $439 each for the Alpha SubSonic 1.

Spectral introduces two new state-of-the art reference components:

    The SDR-3000 Reference CD Transport System is built with instrumentation quality components. Design highlights include a cast aluminum floating CD drive, a lathe turned precision disc turntable, a laminated brass disc damping system, and independent power supplies to ensure maximum isolation for the drive, display, logic and Spectralink data clock interface.

    The DMA-180 Series 2 Reference Amplifier has a fully discrete, dual-monaural design rated at 200 Watts into 8 Ohms with a peak current output of 60 Amps per channel.
Tandberg introduces several new products including the TIA 4062 Integrated Amp. Fully remote controlled, this two-channel amplifier is rated at 50 Watts/channel into 8 Ohms, it offers peak output current of more than 10 Amps per charmer.

The Troll is a cube-shaped design whose key features include a passive input selector in combination with a power amp, a voltage amplification stage connected in a local open-loop configuration, an output stage with a local negative feedback circuit, and heavy duty terminals with gold plated connectors.

Shipment dates and suggested retail pricing were not available at press time.

Ultech is a new brand in consumer electronics and the creation of Ultimate Technology, a U,S. company involved in aspects of the audio/video industry ranging from R&D to manufacturing and distribution, is introducing several new products, including:
    HDCD-10 D/A Converter, a high-performance product designed for consumers seeking to upgrade an existing player or employ an outboard CD transport. Highlights include Pacific Microsonics' High Definition Compatible Digital [HDCD] enhancement process, two matched 20-bit Burr-Brown DIA converters, and digital input through coaxial and TOSLINK formats.

    UCD-100 HDCD CD player is a one-piece unit combinging a stable, precise disk drive with two matched 20-bit Burr-Brown D/A converters and Pacific Microsonics' High Definition Compatible Digital [HDCD] enhancement process, the firm said.

    The Theater Center is a preamplifier/control unit designed for A/V systems combining state-of-the-art surround sound with superior audio quality. Design highlights include Zoran's latest Dolby Digital DSP processor for top AC-3 decoding, HDCD decoding, Dolby Pro Logic decoding and six 20-bit Burr-Brown D/A converters.

    Suggested retail pricing for the line was not available at press time.

Velodyne introduces two new subwoofers, the FSR-12 and FSR-15. Both feature numerous improvements over the P-120OR and F-1500R, which they replace, the firm said. Refinements include smaller cabinets, increased amplifier power, improved power supplies for better voltage regulation, a unique "Subwoofer Direct" feature for connecting the sobs to a processor or receiver without an additional crossover, switchable signal sensing circuits, a high-pass filter, a O/180-degree phase switch, and five-way binding posts.

Velodyne will also showcase its new F-1800R Series 11 subwoofer, featuring several previously unannounced features in particular, enhanced crossover circuitry, balanced inputs, and a 35 Hz subsonic filter switch to improve the impact of Home Theater movie sound. The suggested retail is $1,999.

Vidikron introduces its Helios Projector, a compact, radically different high-performance projector. Helios combines a two-chip implementation of Texas Instruments' revolutionary Digital Light Processing subsystem, using TI's Digital. Micromirror Device, with new Vidikron-engineered digital-video processing circuitry, in a dramatic chassis created by Pininfarina Industrial Design, the internationally famous Italian design firm.

Helios displays deep reds and blues, accurate greens, and high-contrast, high-output gray-scales at levels previously unavailable from comparable compact projectors, Helios also displays PAL and NTSC video, and accepts data and multimedia output directly from PCs.
