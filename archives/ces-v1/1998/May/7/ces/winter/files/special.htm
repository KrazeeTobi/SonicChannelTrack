Special WCES Pavilions for Exhibiting
Tomorrow's Technologies Today

NEW YORK, NEW YORK, November 21, 1996. With the theme "Beat Yesterday's Performance," the organizers of the 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®), the premier event for the consumer electronics industry, will be showing buyers and decision-makers attending the show just how to beat yesterday -- by offering tomorrow's technologies now.

"CES, through its special exhibits and pavilions for evolving technologies, is putting the focus on the future and excitement of consumer electronics by highlighting and displaying the most dramatic new technologies. The Emerging Technologies Pavilion, the Intelligent Transportation Systems Pavilion (ITS), and Innovations `97 all will offer far-reaching insights for the retailers and buyers into the technologies of tomorrow today," said Jonathan Thompson, CES Vice President.

The Emerging Technologies will be located on the lower level of the Sands Expo and Convention Center just left of the Registration lobby. The ITS Pavilion and Innovations `97 is on the upper level of the Sands Expo on the left side toward the back.

The first-time Winter CES Emerging Technologies Pavilion focuses on small companies manufacturing products who produce less than $2 million in sales and have fewer than 15 employees. This is an entrepreneurial showcase which highlights small companies efforts which may become the Microsoft or Motorola of tomorrow.

The ITS Pavilion features products designed to improve passenger and freight efficiency safety along surface transportation modes. It includes exciting in-vehicle navigation systems, traveler information systems, radar, Mayday and collision notification systems.

Innovations '97 includes those "best-in-class" products that are winners in the Innovations `97 Design & Engineering Honors Program. These select product winners are honored as the most prestigious product and design advancements at the 1997 Winter CES.
