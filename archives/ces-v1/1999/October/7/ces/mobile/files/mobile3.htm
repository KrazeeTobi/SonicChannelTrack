CES® Mobile Electronics to Provide Business
Tools for Installers & Retailers

MEXICO CITY, MEXICO, October 8, 1996. The seventh annual CES® Mobile Electronics '97 -- The 12-Volt Educational Forum will provide superior business tools for the mobile electronics world community to enhance retailing success. CES Mobile Electronics is the world's only trade show dedicated exclusively to the 12-volt industry and offers a targeted educational and international forum for manufacturers of mobile electronics products and accessories to conduct business with retailers and installers of these products. The show will be held April 4-6, 1997 at the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta, Georgia.

CES Mobile Electronics spotlights some of the most innovative consumer electronics products -- car audio, vehicle navigation, vehicle security, radar detection, cellular, paging and mobile electronics accessories. Aftermarket mobile electronics equipment sales in the United States will rise six percent in 1996 to $4.4 billion and 10 percent to $4.8 billion by 1997. As factory installed autosound sales drop to $2.5 billion for 1996 (from $3.1 billion in 1995) and more vehicle manufacturers offer cars with only optional mobile electronics products, retailers should be able to capitalize on the trend, boosting sales of the autosound aftermarket in particular.

CES Mobile Electronics is the most cost effective platform for retailers and installers from around the world to discuss the latest developments in the 12-volt industry with the manufacturing community and decide which products to buy. Show management offers a well-rounded, educational program that includes installer and management seminars sponsored by Installation News as well as retail management seminars sponsored by the Mobile Electronics Division of the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA).

Since 1991 when the first Mobile Electronics Show debuted with 54 exhibitors, the show has grown dramatically in size and prestige. The 1996 show featured 199 exhibitors, covering more than 90,000 net square feet. With 298 international trade professionals representing 46 countries, total show attendance stood at 5,516 in 1996. Already 125 exhibitors have signed up for the 1997 show, reserving 69,200 net square feet of space -- 77 percent of the 1996 show total.

"The 1997 show will offer retailers and installers the latest business tools to improve their operations and the hottest products for profit-making opportunities," said Karen Chupka, CES Mobile Electronics show manager. "Show management is launching several innovative marketing campaigns to increase overall attendance and is further expanding its seminar program to provide the mobile electronics retailing community a comprehensive educational forum."

Last year more than 100 new products were exhibited at CES Mobile Electornics. The 1997 show should bode just as well with some of the world's leading mobile electronics companies already among the list of exhibitors: AAMP, Anaba USA, Audio Art, Audiovox, Aura Systems, Boss Audio Systems, Cerwin-Vega, Clarion Sales Corp., Clifford Electronics, Cobra Electronics Corp., Directed Electronics, Eclipse by Fujitsu Ten, International Jensen, Kenwood, Matsushita Consumer Electronics, McIntosh, Metra Electronics Corp., MTX, Polk Audio, Precision Interface Electronics, and Sherwood America.

Show Management

CEMA owns, manages and sponsors several industry trade shows including CES Mobile Electronics, the International Winter Consumer Electronics Show, CES® Mexico, CES Spring and CES® Habitech. All profits from CES Mobile Electronics are reinvested into industry services and the programs and activities of CEMA, including the Mobile Electronics Certification Program (MECP) and other services for the mobile electronics industry that are offered by CEMA's Mobile Electronics Division.
