Smart Digital Products, Prototypes and Hot Converging
Technologies Drive Buyer Interest for 1997 Winter CES

Buyers to Descend on 1997 WCES

NEW YORK, NEW YORK, November 21, 1996. The Consumer Electronics Industry's most influential buyers will descend on the 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®) in Las Vegas, and they will have plenty of hot new digital products to see as well as the latest prototype products and technologies. The CES show, which runs from January 9-12, will be hosted at the Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC), the Las Vegas Hilton, the Sands Expo & Convention Center, and the Alexis Park Hotel.

"Based on a 1996 CES attendee audit, 88 percent of the buyers come to CES looking for products fresh on the market, nearly half come to see prototypes, while 6 out of 10 percent are looking for mature categories. If you combine the plethora of exciting, digital convergence products that just hit the market, and converging technologies that are becoming intertwined at a compounded rate, and the thousands of buying decision-makers, there will be a lot of happy campers at the 1997 CES," said Jonathan Thompson, vice president of the Consumer Electronics Shows.

Another interesting comparison based on last year's CES which adds to the convergence spin is 85 percent of the top computer retailers attended the show, while nearly 80 percent of the TWICE Magazine top 100 consumer electronics retailers attended the CES.

"With more than 1500 companies exhibiting along with nearly 400 brand new exhibitors, coupled with the first-time Emerging Technologies Pavilion, the Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) Pavilion, and Innovations `97, the prestigious consumer electronics honor roll exhibit, there will be more than enough convergence and technological developments to keep the buying community more than motivated for the full four days," Thompson reported.

1997 CES is already off to a tremendous start with approximately 10 of the largest buying companies represented at the very highest levels. Among some of the major retailers registered this year are the senior vice president of merchandising, AAFES; the senior vice president, BJ's Wholesale; president, Brandsmart; president, Best Buy; chief executive officer, CompUSA; president, Elek-Tek; president, Fedco; senior vice president, Fingerhut; president, H.H. Gregg; president, Nobody Beats the Whiz; president, Hardlines-Sears; chief executive officer, Service Merchandise, and chairman, Tweeter.

According to this survey, buyers as a group brought $46.4 billion in retail buying power to the 1996 CES with an average of $1.6 million in retail buying power per buyer. Additionally, 65 percent of the buyers attending the show indicated they have final decision-making authority while some 25 percent of the buyers responded that they have significant influence in retail buying.

In addition to wanting to find the latest and greatest products in the world of consumer electronics at the 1996 CES show, buyers said that they also were interested in networking with their peers, and comparison shopping of vendor lines in one location. On average, they prefer the broad horizontal appeal of WCES, while 80 percent prefer to attend one large, multi-category show rather than other smaller, single category shows.

Last year's WCES, repeat attendees among the buyers reached 70 percent, while new buyers reached 30 percent. The audit indicated that the entire buyer audience turns over every 5-6 years.

"With more than 80,000 distributors, manufacturers, market analysts, importers/ exporters, and journalists also descending on the 1997 WCES, the industry buyers will be among a vast audience from over 50 states, and 110 countries. The 1997 WCES, with nearly one million of square feet of exhibit space, is sizing up to be the best consumer electronics showcase ever, particularly in the areas of new converging products -- from TV/PCs to multi-function centers to satellite products to digital cameras to computer and telephony products. This year's industry showcase promises to be just what the buyer is looking for plus a whole lot more," said Thompson.
