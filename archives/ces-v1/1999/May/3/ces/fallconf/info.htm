1998 EIA Fall Conference and CEMA Management Forum
Growing Industry & Attracting Skilled Workers

The high-technology manufacturing industry currently employees more than 1.9 million individuals in the U.S. That's more jobs than the automotive and steel industries combined. However, as electronic industry products and consumer goods have become more technically sophisticated, the ability to hire highly skilled workers to design, manufacture, distribute, and service these products has become a daunting task for many companies.*

Education and training for the U.S. workforce may be the single most important issue we face as an industry as we approach the end of this century. Because of this, the Electronic Industries Alliance and CEMA will host several special sessions at this year's Fall Conference on workforce development for the 21st century. These sessions are part of a larger Alliance initiative for workforce development
which will be introduced at the conference.

Our success in the 21st century depends upon our ability to respond now to the growing need for our industry's involvement and support in workforce development initiatives, and we strongly encourage your company's participation in this event.

Managers involved with your company's strategic planning, human resources,
education & training, and market research should plan to attend.

*A recent survey revealed that 70% of high-tech industry executives see the lack of skilled workers as a major barrier to the future growth of their companies. Is it a barrier for yours?

Program highlights include:

  • Opportunities to network & build relationships with industry leaders,
    colleagues & potential business partners
  • Noted speakers, industry executives, and government officials will address the challenge facing our industry related to the lack of skilled workers and EIA's plan to deal with it
  • CEMA Product Division Programs to guide association and industry
    initiatives related to:
    - Accessories
    - Antennas
    - Audio
    - Communications & Information Technology Products
    - Digital Imaging
    - Home Theater
    - Integrated Home Systems
    - Mobile Electronics
    - Video
