Home Systems, Home Theater & High-Performance Audio Command Retailer Attention at Hilton and Alexis Park

NEW YORK, NEW YORK, November 21, 1996. Families increasingly want more home entertainment. Retailers ready to capitalize on this trend will find a host of new home entertainment profit makers at the Las Vegas Hilton and Alexis Park hotels during the International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®), January 9-12, 1997. Home systems exhibits will be placed alongside home theater and multi-room entertainment exhibits at the Las Vegas Hilton; specialty audio and some high-end home theater exhibits will be shown at the Alexis Park. Some home control products also will be displayed at the Las Vegas Convention Center.

"This year at the Hilton, CES is promoting the growing synergy of custom home theater products into integrated home systems by providing a one-stop venue to see these fast-growing products and services," said Robbi Lycett, Winter CES Show Director.

Attendees at the 1997 Winter CES will see some new companies on the Hilton exhibit floor. Among the first-time CES home systems exhibitors are: AMP, AVSI, Dancraft Enterprises, Diablo Research, IES Technologies, Lutron Electronics and PHAST. Home theater companies such as Atlantic Technology International, Definitive Technology, Lucasfilm/THX, Madrigal Audio Labs, Marantz America, Niles Audio, Sonance and Stewart Filmscreen will also be exhibiting at the Hilton. In total, more than 170 exhibitors, covering approximately 69,000 net square feet of space, will be housed in the Las Vegas Hilton.

Lycett added, "We see home and building control and home theater as some of the fastest-growing segments of our industry. CES' commitment to these product categories can be seen in exhibit space expansion, a comprehensive seminar track, and a major effort to attract system designers, installers, integrators and retailers. Winter CES has become an invaluable showcase for the home systems and home theater industries."

Alexis Park is THE Trade Show Venue for Specialty Audio

Specialty audio manufacturers and retailers will view the Alexis Park Hotel as their personal trade show venue at Winter CES. During the 1997 show, all companies with a specialty audio focus will be located at the Alexis Park. More than 150 specialty audio exhibitors, covering approximately 32,000 square feet of space, are expected, making the Alexis Park host to one of the largest gatherings of the specialty audio community ever.

"Some of our largest exhibits at the Alexis Park include Adcom, AudioQuest, Burmester Audio Systems, Krell Industries, Martin-Logan, Mobile Fidelity Sound Labs, Nakamichi, TEAC America, Thiel and Vandersteen Audio," said Lycett. "With the Sahara undergoing major renovations this year, we had to move the specialty audio exhibitors to another venue. Fortunately, we found the Alexis Park, a hotel which can offer both exhibitors and attendees superior benefits and services."

Located at 375 East Harmon Avenue, the Alexis Park is less than two miles from the Las Vegas Convention Center. The hotel has ample self-parking, valet parking, a four-star restaurant, and a multilingual concierge staff. There is no casino at the Alexis Park, so finding exhibitor rooms will be quick and uncomplicated. Sleeping rooms are available at the hotel for both exhibitors and attendees.

Express shuttle bus service is available between all CES facilities. Plus, on-site registration, a CEMA member lounge and on-site housing assistance will be available at the Alexis Park. Journalists can pick up Alexis Park exhibitor press kits outside the CES Show Office at the Alexis Park Hotel.
