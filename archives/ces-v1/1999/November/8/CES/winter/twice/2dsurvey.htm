Exhibitor Survey Shows Upbeat Mood On 1st Day

The near-record crowds that flooded the exhibits at this Winter CES can be considered a reflection of the generally upbeat retailer outlook for 1997 as indicated by the results of TWICE's exclusive survey of exhibitors conducted on the show floor yesterday.

Asked what kind of feedback they are getting from dealers with respect to the prospects for 1997 business, fully 79% of exhibitors said retailers were looking for the year to be better than 1996.

But, realistically, said one respondent, 1996 "was such a bad year, there's nowhere to go but up." Things apparently have already picked up to a modest degree, with another exhibitor noting that he has seen improvement in orders from both retail and OEM accounts. However, pointed one retailer pointed out, "some of our retail customers are doing well, others are out of business."

As indicated in past surveys, the majority of exhibitors (63%) said they were not here at the show expecting to write any major orders. Fully 74% said they were looking to sign on new accounts. That's a somewhat higher percentage than in past years, and may be the result of last year's rash of store closings and bankruptcy filings.

The survey results indicate that, at least for this year, the Winter CES will continue to outshine its summer counterpart. Only 42% of exhibitors at this show said they were considering displaying at the June 2-5 Summer CES to be held in Atlanta in conjunction with COMDEX. But 53% said they were interested in showing at the Fall COMDEX, scheduled to be held in Las Vegas in November.

Also, the survey shows, the growing internationalization of CES continues to be of major importance to exhibitors. Some 58% of queried exhibitors said foreign buyers at the show are creating new business opportunities for them. Several said they saw international accounts as a way to lessen the impact of the soft U.S. market, and one expressed appreciation for the list of foreign attendees supplied by WCES show management.
