 1998 Fall Conference
Program Highlights

The following is a highlight of events at the 1998 Fall Conference. These sessions are open to all conference attendees:

EIA and Workforce Development
for the 21st Century

EIA President, Peter McCloskey, will be joined by leaders from the electronics industry to roll-out a new national industry initiative for the 21st century workforce. Join us for this exciting event as our industry comes together to address a vital component to our industry's continued success - the skilled workforce.
- Monday, October 12, 1:45 pm - 3:30 pm.

Building A World-Class Company with
Employees of the Future

CEMA is proud to present Editor-in-Chief, John Brandt of Industry Week.
Brandt will be discussing key strategies for business success that are achieved through hiring and retaining your most valuable asset - skilled talent. Exploring the real stories behind management and manufacturing strategies, he details practical examples of how organizations can position themselves as market
leaders through investment in their people.
- Monday, October 12, 3:45 pm - 5:15 pm.

Building the 21st Century Workforce...
How Do We Get There?

As we embark upon a new era, what will your company's role be in educating, training, retraining and retaining your skilled workers? What new demographic groups will make up the next generation workforce, and what implications will that have on your business? These and many other questions that could impact your organization, will be addressed by our panel of leaders from academia,
government, industry, and the HR community.
Panelists include: Dr. Ronald Sega, former MIR astronaut and Dean of the College of Engineering and Applied Science at the University of Colorado at Colorado Springs; Bill Payson, CEO, The Senior Staff; Phil Rittmueller, Vice President of Automotive Electronics, NEC Technologies, Inc.; Robert Bellinger, Managing Editor, The Profession - EE Times; Richard Pinola, Chairman and CEO, Right Management Consultants, Inc. (invited); and a top representative from the U.S. Department of Labor.
- Tuesday, October 13, 1:45 pm - 3:45 pm.

Technology and the Skilled Workforce

A special keynote from the U.S. Department of Commerce will address workforce development for the electronics industry. The DOC has been on the leading edge of the tech-worker shortage, and will discuss the Administration's efforts in addressing the projected shortage, and how industry can foster a positive working relationship with the Administration on these initiatives.
- Tuesday, October 13, 4:00 pm - 5:15 pm.

Reaching Retailers through Training
and Education

Creative Channel Services will be providing an overview of CE Smart, the consumer electronics industry's first nationwide retail training and educational program. Sponsored by industry leaders CEMA and TWICE, CE Smart brings together consumer electronics manufacturers and thousands of retail sales
associates for the interactive sharing of product information and techniques for increasing sales.
- Wednesday, October 14, 8:30 am- 10:00 am.
