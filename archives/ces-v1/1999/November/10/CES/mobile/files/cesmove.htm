CES® Mobile Electronics Moves to Atlanta for 1997

ORLANDO, FLORIDA, April 19, 1996. Next year's CES® Mobile Electronics will move to the Georgia World Congress Center in Atlanta, Georgia. The MES is the world's only trade show dedicated exclusively to the 12-volt industry and provides a true educational forum for manufacturers of mobile electronics products and accessories to conduct business with retailers and installers of these products. The 1997 Show will be held April 4-6.

The 1997 will features the next generation of products in car audio, vehicle navigation, vehicle security, radar detection, safety warning systems, CBs, scanners, mobile office, cellular, personal communications systems (PCS), paging and mobile electronics accessories.

The aftermarket mobile electronics industry is expected to continue its steady growth. The Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association forecasts dollar factory sales of aftermarket vehicle security to rise seven percent from 1996 to 1997. Unit factory sales of in-dash CD players are expected to grow 16 percent from 1996 to 1997 with sales of autosound CD changers to increase 13 percent during the same time period.

Exhibit space selection for the 1997 Show will begin at this year's show location in Orlando on April 20. Show management expects at least 200 exhibitors will be on board for the 1997 MES, covering approximately 98,000 net square feet. MES '96 exhibitors will have priority in selecting booth space for the 1997 Show.

CEMA owns, manages and sponsors several industry trade shows including the CES Mobile Electronics, International Winter Consumer Electronics Show (CES), CES Specialty Audio & Home Theater, CES Mexico, CES Habitech and CES Orlando...The Digital Destination (premiering in 1996).

In 1994 CEMA co-sponsored the MES with Bobit Publishing, the Show founder. CEMA assumed the role of Show manager of the MES beginning with the 1995 Show. All profits from the Show are reinvested into industry services and the programs and activities of the CEMA, including the Mobile Electronics Certification Program (MECP) and other services for the mobile electronics industry which are offered by CEMA's Mobile Electronics Division.
