Added Promotions Drive Sands Traffic
for Mobile Electronics Exhibitors

ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA, November 1, 1996. In an effort to build booth traffic for mobile electronics exhibitors at the Sands Expo & Convention Center, CES show management is adding a number of special promotions for the International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®) in Las Vegas, January 9-12, 1997. As of late October, requests for mobile electronics exhibit space were up 10 percent and requests for wireless communications exhibit space were up 34 percent compared to the same time frame for the 1996 show; much of the allocated mobile and wireless exhibit space within the Sands has been sold. The Winter CES is the world's largest trade-only exposition for the mobile electronics industry.

CES will be giving a 1997 Ford F150 Supercab that will be outfitted with some of the latest mobile electronics gear to a lucky attendee at the Sands. A duplicate model of the truck will also be on display at the Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC). Attendees should look for details on this promotional sweepstakes in December.

To further promote mobile electronics exhibitors at the Sands, CES is also teaming up with Sound Solutions to create a Mobile Electronics Exhibitor Information Kiosk System. Two kiosks will be placed in designated areas of the LVCC and one at the Sands. These kiosks will display a graphics display and booth number of each mobile electronics exhibitor, and exhibitors can purchase a 15 second to 30 second video presentation about their company or products for a minimal cost of $200. The kiosks will also contain details about bus schedules, conferences and a display of the Sands floor plan.

Outside of the show floor, attendees should look for additional mobile electronics exhibitors displaying their latest sound systems in the Sands parking lot. This space has recently opened up for exhibits. Interested exhibitors should contact the CES Sales Department at 703-907-7657.

Also in the Sands, the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA), the Winter CES sponsor and manager, will host an association booth featuring the Mobile Electronics Certification Program (MECP), mobile electronics market research, mobile electronics membership services and other products and publications for the mobile industry.

With vehicle navigation awareness on the rise, a special Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) Pavilion to help mobile electronics retailers take advantage of this growing market will be located within the Sands. The pavilion allows manufacturers - especially OEMs, smaller companies and young ITS product divisions at large corporations -- to get their feet wet in marketing their ITS products at Winter CES without the financial and logistical obligations of launching a full product line and booth. Participants in this year's pavilion include Etak, Navigation Technologies, POIS, RoadTrak and Rockwell. Interested companies should contact Kirk Smallwood of CES at 703-907-7606 to inquire about exhibit opportunities.

"With promotions and special events such as these -- along with our other promotional line-up that includes targeted advertising campaigns, on-site banners and signage, the location of Innovations at the Sands, service facilities, partnership mailings and express bus shuttles -- CES show management has pulled out all the stops to make Winter CES the best show ever for mobile electronics exhibitors," said Robbi Lycett, Winter CES Show Director. "Winter CES has always been the largest trade-show event for mobile electronics and we plan to continue that tradition."

All profits from the Winter CES and other CES-sponsored events are reinvested into industry services, including the MECP, media relations programs, engineering standards development, market research, and governmental and legal affairs support as directed by CEMA.
