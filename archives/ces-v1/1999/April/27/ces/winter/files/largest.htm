Sands Expo Will Hold
Mobile & Wireless' Largest
Trade Event Ever

ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA, August 26, 1996. Mobile electronics and wireless communications companies will have one of their best showings ever at the International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®) in Las Vegas, January 9-12, 1997. More than 80 percent of the product area for mobile electronics and wireless communications exhibitors within the Sands Expo & Convention Center has been sold. As of mid-August, 203 mobile electronics companies and another 111 cellular and paging companies had been assigned space within the Sands, totaling 227,263 net square feet -- ahead of 1996's sales pace in regards to cellular and about even in the cases of paging and mobile electronics. The Winter CES is the world's largest trade-only exposition for the mobile electronics industry.

"Overall exhibitor sales are going well with 1,331 companies signed up to exhibit," said Robbi Lycett, Winter CES Show Director. "Some of the biggest players in cellular, paging and mobile electronics will be showcasing their newest and most advanced products at the Sands, including AAMP, Air Touch, Alpine, Audiovox, BEL-Tronics, Blaupunkt, Clarion, Cobra, Directed Electronics, Eclipse by Fujitsu Ten, Ericsson, Kenwood, MobileComm, Motorola, MTX, NEC, Nokia, Pacific Bell Mobile Services, PageMart, PageNet, Pioneer Electronics, Rockford Fosgate, Stillwater Designs and Whistler."

"By using the entire Sands Expo and Convention Center for 1997, we're able to offer the mobile and wireless industries the best in facilities with special services for those exhibitors and attendees, plus room to expand as those product categories have grown both on the show floor and at retail," Lycett added.

Show management is finalizing promotional plans for the 1997 Winter CES. With the Sands as a primary facility for 1997, CES has added many services to balance and enhance traffic flow among all trade show facilities. Since this is the first time mobile electronics exhibitors are at the Sands, show management is pulling out all the stops to highlight this area for CES-attending retailers. These plans include:

    Express shuttle among all CES facilities, including back-door delivery;
    Complete line of service facilities, including an international business center, press room, registration area, CES store, and CEMA member business center and lounge;

    Innovations located at the Sands -- More than 30,000 attendees saw Innovations, the Winter CES awards program and display, during the 1996 show;

    Meeting/closing rooms;
    On-site housing assistance; and
    Direct non-stop bus service between official CES hotels and the Sands.
Other promotional activities include give-away incentives for buyers and press; a promotional partnership mailing; a video wall at the McCarren Airport promoting the Sands; banners at CES facilities, show hotels and bus shuttle sites promoting the Sands; and a special Sands advertisement to run in key trade publications.

More than 40 educational sessions are included in the 1997 Winter CES Conference Program. All wireless and mobile electronics sessions will be at the Sands, including:

    Paging's Role in the New Wireless World
    PCS: What is the Status
    Car Navigation -- What are the Options
    Computers in Mobile Electronics
    How to Troubleshoot During an Installation
    What's New in Auto Security
    How to Sell Beyond the Basic System
    Promoting Installation as a Profit Center
Moderators and panelists for these sessions will be announced within the next few weeks. A special Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) Pavilion to help mobile electronics retailers take advantage of this growing market will also be located within the Sands. This third annual showcase will house several manufacturers within 2,000 net square feet. The Pavilion allows manufacturers - especially OEMs, smaller companies, and young ITS product divisions at large corporations -- to get their feet wet in marketing their ITS products at Winter CES without the financial and logistical obligations of launching a full product line and booth. Last year's participants included Delco Electronics, Etak Inc., ITS America, Magneti Marelli, Navigation Technologies, POIS, Rockwell Automotive and Siemens Automotive.

All profits from the Winter CES and other CES-sponsored events are reinvested into industry services, including the Mobile Electronics Certification Program (MECP), media relations programs, engineering standards development, market research, and governmental and legal affairs support as directed by the Winter CES sponsor and manager, the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA).
