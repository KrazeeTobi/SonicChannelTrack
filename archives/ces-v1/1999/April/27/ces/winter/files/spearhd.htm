CES Internet Products Showcase Spearheading
Internet Activities; Sponsored by IBM

E-town Covering CES Live

ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA, December 3, 1996. Bolstering an already high-presence of internet related activities, the organizers of the 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®) announced today one of the internet anchors of the show -- the CES Internet Products Showcase sponsored by IBM. In the showcase will be E-Town broadcasting live from the CES over the World Wide Web.

The Internet Showcase, the result of a CES and IBM partnership, is a 40 x 50 foot exhibit booth located in the North Hall of the Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC).

Gary Smith, manager of trade show operations and marketing support for IBM, said, "the 1997 CES provides us with the best internet showcasing opportunity that exists in the industry. We are delighted to be spearheading this project with CES especially as part of one of the largest converging electronics trade shows in the world."

Smith added, "Both the high-interest and high-activity level for internet and converging categories at CES already demonstrate that this is indeed the ideal, exciting and vibrant venue for showcasing the converging marketplace."

Jonathan Thompson, vice president of CES, said "the industry and CES is fortunate to have an industry leader such as IBM demonstrating the latest in internet technology. For years, IBM has been a solid long-term contributor to the show as well as a proven industry pioneer. The company's sponsorship of the Internet Showcase only adds fuel to the internet fire at the 1997 show."

Overall, more than one million square feet of exhibit space at the Winter CES has been assigned to more than 1500 companies and manufacturers from all over the world. Featuring nearly 400 new exhibitors, the CES will be located at four major locations: the LVCC, the adjoining Las Vegas Hilton, the Sands Expo and Convention Center, and the Alexis Park Hotel.

Traditional audio and video products will converge with internet and satellite products in the LVCC. All major Direct Broadcasting System (DBS) manufacturers will exhibit their latest products in this facility. Companies such as Philips, Sony, Thomson, and Zenith will be demonstrating their new TV/PC and internet products.

The Sands Expo and Convention Center will be playing an ever-greater role at the `97 CES with about 650 exhibitors in over 500,000 square feet of exhibit space including the Emerging Technologies Pavilion (lower level), mobile electronics, cellular, paging, wireless, telephone, personal electronics, and more.

The Sands also is host to the Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) Pavilion. The ITS, represented by such companies as Etak, Navigation Technologies, Rockwell, Siemens, and ITS America, will feature products designed to improve passenger and freight efficiency and safety along surface transportation modes. Products will include in-vehicle navigation systems, traveler information systems, safety devices, Mayday and collision notification systems, and vehicle radar systems. As part of its larger presence at the CES, the Sands is also serving as an additional arena for all product categories as more and more companies catch the fever and sign on for the show.

The Alexis Park Hotel will be the main home for specialty audio for companies like AudioQuest, Krell Industries, Martin-Logan, Mobile Fidelity, Thiel, and Vandersteen displaying state-of-the-art audio advancements.

A highly-focused comprehensive conference program of over 50 sessions to be held at all four main locations consists of eight tracks: new technology, Internet, multimedia, SoHo, specialty audio, mobile electronics, home theater and general sessions. Two six-session accredited tracks on home systems and retail management also are available for $125 or $30 a session.

The keynote speaker for 1997 International Winter CES is Michael Bloomberg, president and founder of Bloomberg Financial Markets. Bloomberg will present the prestigious keynote address during the opening session on Thursday, January 9 from 8:30 to 10:00 a.m. Bloomberg Financial Markets monitors and analyzes the activities of the consumer electronics industry and provides multimedia, analytical and news services to financial and news organizations throughout the world. In addition, Bob Borchardt, president of Recoton, and Gary Shapiro, president of the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA) will also discuss the state of the consumer electronics industry during the keynote. year.
