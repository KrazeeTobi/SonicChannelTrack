Winter CES® First to Use IAEM Endorsed Independent Audit

NEW YORK, NEW YORK, November 21, 1996. The 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Show (CES®) will be the first major trade show to be independently audited since a recent agreement between the Audit Bureau of Verification Services, Inc. (ABVS) and IAEM Services, Inc.

Created specifically to verify trade show attendance, ABVS is a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Audit Bureau of Circulations (ABC), the world's largest auditor of newspapers and periodicals. In August, ABVS and IAEM Services, a subsidiary of the International Association for Exposition Management (IAEM), announced a joint program to educate trade show organizers and exhibitors on the necessity of auditing expositions.

Winter CES, to be held in Las Vegas, NV, January 9-12, is the world's largest computer and consumer electronics trade show aimed at the retail distribution channel. More than 80,000 retailers, distributors, buyers, press and industry personnel are expected to visit the 1500+ exhibitors, covering more than one million net square feet of exhibit space.

"CES has long held an honest policy of self-auditing of our attendee figures. In 1989, we were one of the first trade shows to verify our attendance figures through badge holder pick-up auditing. Now we're taking our policy of honesty one step further by using an independent auditor designed for trade shows," said Gary Shapiro, president of CEMA, the Winter CES manager and sponsor. "We are pleased that ABC and ABVS took the lead in offering this type of program. We are proud to be the first show to take advantage of it. We challenge other trade show organizers to do the same so that exhibitors can truly compare their trade show options."

"Industry acceptance of this program will allow the exposition industry to prosper as better comparisons can be made with alternative forms of marketing," said Steven Hacker, CAE, president of IAEM. "The audit will verify the absolute attendance of a show. It will also ensure that the demographic profiles exhibition management provides to potential exhibitors are accurate. If the trade show industry shifts to attendee verification and independent audit, the entire industry's credibility will soar."

"As an association, our members expect honesty. Now we have an independent measurement system in place to further back-up our statistics," added Shapiro.

"We congratulate CES for being the first trade show to make this commitment since the IAEM agreement. Their decision puts them at the forefront of a new effort to respond to the exhibitor's need for reliable, credible information to provide accountability for their trade show investment," said Michael J. Lavery, ABC and ABVS president and managing director.

ABVS auditing services verifies attendance and attendee information claims, records and recordkeeping systems. On-site and post-show analyses are conducted with every ABVS audit.

During the trade show, ABVS auditors will validate attendance with an in-depth examination of CES' recordkeeping systems and procedures, including direct, on-site observation of its registration system.

After CES closes, attendees will be contacted independently to validate information collected during the show. With the source documentation provided by CES, auditors will then reconcile the attendance list, eliminate duplicate names and verify demographic data.

Following the audit, ABVS will produce an audit report, identifying key audiences, demographics and geographic make-up of trade show participants. CES will receive the report on the winter show within three to four weeks after providing final documentation to the ABVS auditing team.

ABVS was established in 1995 to serve the verification requirements of new and non-traditional, census-based advertiser-supported media. In addition to its trade show auditing program, ABVS Interactive provides third-party audits for Web sites and other online advertising activity claims. ABC is the world's first and largest circulation-auditing association with more than 4,100 advertiser, advertising agency and publisher members.

IAEM is a not-for-profit association representing approximately 3,800 individuals within organizations that manage more than 75 percent of all North American trade shows.
