ITS Pavilion Planned for 1997 Winter CES®

ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA, May 31, 1996. With 83 percent of Americans making at least one "road trip" of 100 miles or more each year, Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) products have a ready-made market. A special ITS Pavilion to help mobile electronics retailers take advantage of this growing market is set for the 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®) in Las Vegas, Nevada, January 9-12. Located within the Sands Expo & Convention Center, the third annual showcase will house several manufacturers within 2,000 net square feet. The ITS Pavilion was first introduced at the 1995 Winter CES.

The ITS Pavilion educates retailers about new potential mobile growth areas within the ITS field. The Pavilion also allows manufacturers -- especially OEMs, smaller companies, and young ITS product divisions at large corporations -- to get their feet wet in marketing their ITS products at Winter CES without the financial and logistical obligations of launching a full product line and booth. Last year's participants included Delco Electronics, Etak Inc., ITS America, Magneti Marelli, Navigation Technologies, POIS, Rockwell Automotive and Siemens Automotive.

Out of 27,000 buyers surveyed during last year's Winter CES, 13 percent indicated that they were interested in ITS-type products. Manufacturers who wish to participate in the 1997 Pavilion should contact Kirk Smallwood of CES, tel: 703-907-7606, fax: 703-907-7691. The exhibit space cost of $4,100 per 100 net square feet includes a pre-fabricated booth set-up that requires minimal planning and effort on the part of the exhibitor.

"We were extremely pleased with the 1996 Winter CES in general and with the ITS Pavilion in particular. We plan on being part of the 1997 Pavilion as well," said Thomas P. Theisen, product line manager, navigation systems for Rockwell Automotive's Driver Information Systems business. "Participating in the ITS Pavilion provided us with increased visibility and booth traffic, which was critical to Rockwell. We used the show to meet with potential distributors and retailers of The PathMasterTM System, our premium turn-by-turn route guidance and information system. The ITS Pavilion attracted the kind of visitors to our booth that we wanted to meet."

ITS products are designed to improve passenger and freight efficiency and safety along surface transportation modes. ITS America hopes to make ITS technologies available to all consumers of passenger and freight transportation within five years.

More than half of Americans are aware that ITS car navigation systems are now available for purchase, according to a recent survey by the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA). And, more importantly, almost 20 percent of those surveyed would like to purchase some type of car navigation system.

State-of-the-art ITS navigation systems use Global Positioning System (GPS) satellites to identify vehicle locations, plus sophisticated mapping software and databases to show a driver where his/her car is in relation to its destination. Depending on the system, the software can identify such landmarks as surrounding highways and local roads, airports, parks, restaurants, specific addresses and more. Drivers can use the software to plan their routes, tour familiar sites, locate new addresses and plan their trips as they go.
