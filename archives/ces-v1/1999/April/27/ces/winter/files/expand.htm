1997 Winter CES® To Expand Use Of Entire Sands Facility

ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA, May 23, 1996 - To consolidate the International Winter Consumer Electronics Showr into fewer exhibit facilities and avoid temporary structures, the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA) is expanding to use the entire Sands Expo and Convention Center (Sands) for the 1997 Winter CES® and beyond. The 1996 Show used the entire Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC), and about 40 percent of the Sands, along with exhibit space at the Las Vegas Hilton, and the Sahara Hotel.

"We are excited about expanding throughout the Sands facility, and moving away from temporary and satellite locations," said Winter CES Show Manager Wayne Crawford. "We will continue to use the entire Hilton and LVCC but will no longer use the Sahara Hotel or temporary structures."

For the 1997 Winter CES, to be held January 9-12, several product categories have been moved to accommodate exhibitors. The Sands categories include: mobile electronics, cellular/paging/wireless, telephones, the Innovations pavilion, personal electronics and adult software. Computer and multimedia products will now be exhibited at the Las Vegas Convention Center along with audio and video, and home office/automation. Specialty/high-end audio exhibits will be housed at the Alexis Park Hotel. Another change for the 1997 Show is the move of home theater and satellite products from the Las Vegas Convention Center to the Las Vegas Hilton.

"This new home theater location will provide additional space for this popular category to expand," said Crawford. "We feel that the various product categories will benefit from being situated within each of these locations."
