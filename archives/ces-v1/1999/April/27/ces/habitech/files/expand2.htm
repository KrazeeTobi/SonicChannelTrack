CES® Habitech Expands to Include Small Building Systems

Dallas to Host 1997 Event


ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA, September 4, 1996. The premier trade and training event, CES® Habitech, is expanding its scope to include small building systems and service providers. The annual event, which is managed, sponsored and produced by the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA), will now showcase those products that are installed and integrated in the residential and light commercial market. CES Habitech '97, to be held June 24-26 in Dallas, will feature the infrastructure, controls, communications, entertainment, security and energy management products that enable systems integration in today's home as well as in small commercial applications.

Some of the systems and products to be exhibited at the Wyndham Anatole's Trinity Hall include network infrastructure, home theater/entertainment, HVAC, lighting, security, telecom/intercom, whole house/building, conference/board room, and related technologies and systems.

"We recognize that many of the products in this area are appropriate for both residential and light commercial applications and most of the integrators we attract do business in both markets," said CES Habitech Show Manager, John Galante. "In addition, Texas' construction and high technology markets are in the midst of a steady, healthy boom. Combine that with the easy, affordable air travel access in the Dallas area, and CES Habitech should experience an even stronger nationwide draw for the event."

In addition to the exciting and innovative products on display, CES Habitech will offer the most comprehensive conference program of its kind in North America, with dozens of hands-on trade and training sessions for the professional interested in advice from the experts in home and building networks. For 1997 a special conference track has been added to focus on small building networks.

"The CES Habitech conference program provides attendees with the technical, marketing and management information needed to tap the potential of the home systems market," said conference organizer, Tricia Parks, president of Parks Associates. "We've worked hard to provide the best training possible for system integrators."
