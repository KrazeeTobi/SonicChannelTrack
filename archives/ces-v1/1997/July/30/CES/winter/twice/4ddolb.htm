Dolby Digital Pricing Moves Begin At CES

DVD isn't even here, but the Dolby Digital price competition is already heating up with Kenwood's announcement that it has reduced the expected everyday retail price for one of its new Dolby Digital receivers to $700 from $800.

The industry's next-lowest priced Dolby Digital receiver is a model introduced by Pioneer at an expected everyday retail of $799.

The Kenwood model, the 1080VR, is due in April, features 3x120+2x60 watts of amplification, and comes with a FutureSet remote whose codes can be updated by end users through a telephone download from Kenwood.

Pioneer's $799 model, the VSX-D606S, features a 5x120-watt amp and, unlike the Kenwood model, features onboard RVF demodulator for use with Dolby Digital laserdisc players. Pioneer's unit is due in February.
