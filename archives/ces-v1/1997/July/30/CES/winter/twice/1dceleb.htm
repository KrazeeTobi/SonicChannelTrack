Various Celebs Add Sparkle To WCES Events

As usual, Winter CES draws celebrities. As of press time, here is a brief rundown of which luminaries are scheduled and where they will be appearing. Football greats Dick Butkus and Joe Theismann will be huddling with retailers at the Fuji booth 1440 at WCES to showcase the company's status as the official audio and video tape of the National Football League. Today Butkus will appear 1:00 to 3:00 p.m., while Theismann will be at the booth on Friday from 1:00 to 3:00 p.m.

Singer/guitarist George Benson will headline Monster Cable's annual dealer awards during WCES. Monster Cable will present its Most Monestrous Dealer Awards in the event set for Friday, January 10, at 8:30 p.m. in the Mirage Hotel's Grand Ballroom.

Mick Fleetwood of Fleetwood Mac is making two appearances at Winter CES today. His first stop is Lanzar in booth 15845 in the Sands Convention Center, between 10:00 a.m. and 12 noon. Fleetwood, who has recently been named a spokesman for the Lanzar's car audio products, will be signing autographs and meeting dealers. Between 2:00 and 4:00 p.m., he will be in the Cidco booth 20827, also in the Sands, promoting the company's iPhone, a smart phone that surfs the web.

Primestar has three celebrities visiting booth 3516 during WCES. Former heavyweight champion George Foreman is there today between 12:00 noon and 1:30 p.m., while country music star Clay Walker takes over from 1:30 to 3:30 p.m. Leading NASCAR driver Ted Musgrave will appear tommorrow, between 10:00 a.m. and 12:00 noon.
