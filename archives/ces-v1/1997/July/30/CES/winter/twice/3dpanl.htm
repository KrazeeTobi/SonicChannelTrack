Panel Provides Expansion Tips For Independents

Store expansion can be a challenging venture, whether a retailer has two stores and is planning a third, or has 100 locations and expects to double that.

At a WCES seminar, NARDA executive director Elly Valas brought together two retailing pros from outside the consumer electronics industry to offer their perspectives on "How To Successfully Expand."

"It's difficult today to be cavalier about expanding a business," Valas said in kicking off the seminar. "Expansion has become much more of a science."

Dik Glass, a VP of the FAO Schwartz toy chain, related how his company grew from four stores in 1972 to its present 42-store count (with a few changes in ownership along the way). Glass said the decision to expand should be made strictly on the basis of sales. "You have to get to a specific level of sales before adding more stores makes sense," he told his audience, primarily other retailers.

Glass also said it was important to make sure you can afford to fully stock the expanded number of stores: "If you don't have the merchandise, people will walk away."

Tony Grappo, who helped launch the Outback Steakhouse restaurant chain, said it's important to research potential store locations carefully, looking for desirable demographics and the presence of other high-traffic businesses in the area. "And talk to potential customers to see what they need," Grappo added.

"You also need to look at how much business the new store will take away from your existing store," he said.
