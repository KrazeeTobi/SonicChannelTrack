25-Channel, 900MHz C'dless
Phones To Debut At Winter CES

Manufacturers of cordless phones continue to add features to their 25-channel and 900MHz products. Although some companies, such as Sony and Lucent Technologies typically have private showings for introductions, here are just some of the products being seen, or talked about on the floor, during Winter CES. (For a complete report on market trends, see feature story on page 97.)

Cobra is introducing the AN8640 25-channel cordless phone with answerer, dual keypad and duplex speakerphone at $149. The built-in digital answerer has 15 minutes of recording time.

Also coming from Cobra are new Intenna 25-channel cordless phones with Type II Caller ID, the model 2525 at $129 and the 2530 at $139.

Panasonic is spotlighting a 32-channel, digital 900MHz Spread Spectrum unit (Model KX TCS970M-B) at $349.95 SRP, and a 900MHz instrument (KX TCM938-B) with digital answering machine, SRP $279.95.

Northern Telecom (Nortel) is showing 25-channel and 900MHz phones with Caller ID. The new Maestro 6200 CW and 6300 CW both offer Caller ID with Call Waiting with call disposition (Type III Caller ID). They are also preprogrammed to interface with network services such as call forwarding and call return. Suggested retail prices are $219.95 and $249.95, respectively.

Also new are the 25-channel Maestro 4525 and 4625, with speakerphone, paging and intercom as well as Caller ID. The suggested retail prices are $189.95 and $219.95, respectively.

The company is also announcing its new Maestro 900 DSS, a digital spread spectrum 900MHz cordless phone with Caller ID with Call Waiting. All of the Maestro phones offer features such as a three-line backlit display, personal directory, message waiting voice mail indicator, a million digital codes for security, and preferred name match.

Sanyo is adding two 25-channel cordless phones with answerers built in, the CAS-837 at $169.99 SRP and the CAS-887 at $199.99. Both offer digital answering machines with 20 minutes of recording time and personal mail boxes, and SpeakStation speakerphones in the handset. The CAS-887 adds a speakerphone on the base.

The company also has three new 25-channel cordless phones with Caller ID to hold the names/numbers of the last 40 callers. These include the CLT-657 at $149.99 SRP, the CLT-667 at $179.99 with speakerphone, and the CLT-677 at $199.99 SRP with speakerphone in both the base and handset.

Sharp is introducing four new 25-channel models and two 900MHz cordless phones. The 25-channel units start with the CL-160 at $99.99; the CL-260 with speakerphone and dual keypads at $149.99; the CL-265ID with Caller ID, a three-line display and electronic phone book at $179.99; and the CL-510 with an all digital answerer and two voice mailboxes at $229.99.

The company's two new 900MHz phones are the CL-905 with digital voice scrambling at $219.99 and the CL980ID with Caller ID at $269.99.

In addition to its existing SPP-ER101, that was listed in the November issue of Consumer Reports as the top-rated 900 MHz phone, Sony will have some additional products to its cordless line for early second quarter. And it will be looking at traditional multi-line products this year. Several models have Caller ID Type II capability.

Toshiba: Toshiba is featuring a 900MHz digital cordless phone with Type II Caller (Caller ID with call waiting). The FTH986BK displays the caller information and also stores up to 45 separate records and then can dial them automatically at the touch of a button. The suggested retail price is $249.
