Merchandising Ideas Provided At CES Seminar

Key elements for retail success were explored in a WCES management conference, "Merchandising 101," yesterday.

During the conference, consultant Al Meyers of Management Horizons urged retailers to create "a unique, compelling competitive advantage too difficult for competitors to replicate." The "secret weapons" in creating that advantage are price, product, shopping experience and service, he said.

Meyers encouraged retailers to look outside their own industry for examples of merchandising innovation and to monitor customers regularly to keep on top of their likes and dislikes.

Lee Carpenter, chairman/CEO of DesignForum, noted recent changes in the landscape of retailing, including store closing and negative sales performances.

Effective retail concepts, Carpenter said, give customers additional and new reasons to shop; sell solutions, not just product; offer something special; provide convenient, non-traditional locations; and make the shopping experience fun.

Describing his own unique retail concept was Ken Fletcher, president/CEO of the Roberds chain. Fletcher offered a look at the innovative Roberds Grand store that opened in Cincinnati on July 1, 1996.

The 250,000-square-foot Roberds Grand location focuses on huge product assortment. "Our pricing is very, very competitive in our market, and we're finding that customers drive up to 50 miles to buy from us," Fletcher said.
