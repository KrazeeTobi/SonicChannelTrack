Retail Management Program to Debut at 1997 Winter CES

NEW YORK, NEW YORK, November 21, 1996. For the first time in its 30 year history, the 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®) will offer an extensive Retail Management program as part of its comprehensive seminar series. Designed to offer creative solutions and suggestions to doing business in the consumer electronics industry, the Retail Management program is one of two paid seminar series being introduced at CES. The comprehensive seminar line up also includes over 40 free workshops addressing a host of issues impacting the consumer electronics industry. The Winter CES will take place January 9-12, 1997, in Las Vegas.

One of the featured Retail Management programs is How To Successfully Expand - Lessons From Other Industries. Moderated by Elly Valas of NARDA, this special session welcomes experts from other industries who will share their tips for success and answer questions ranging from how to finance an expansion to managing a multi-store chain. Other Retail Management sessions will include The Internet and Retail - What Every Dealer Should Know, moderator: Ron Goldberg of E-Town; Merchandising 101: Store Design/Display, moderator: Lee Carpenter of Design Forum; Merchandising 102:Promotions/Planning/ Marketing, moderator: Steve Bryant, The Bryant Group; Customer Returns: A "New" Look at an "Old" Problem, presenter: William Stuart, Stuart & Associates and Eliminating Costly Habits: Zero Percent Financing and Price Competition, presenter: Jules Steinberg, Jules Steinberg Associates.

The six Retail Management Sessions will be held at the Las Vegas Hilton. The cost to attend all six sessions is $125 or $30 for each.

A second paid track, Home Systems, will have the same cost structure as the retail management program. Seminars will include Home Systems Standards - A Reality Check, moderated by Tricia Parks of Parks Associates. From X-10 to the coming of LonWorks and CEBus, this session will offer advice and solutions for communications standards and discuss the actions and products that home systems installers can expect to see in the coming year. Additional Home Systems workshops will include: Consumer Trends in Home Systems, moderator: Susan Whitehurst of SDM Home Systems Installer; Sources of Recurring Revenue, moderator: Ken Moyes, Electronic House; An Incremental Approach: Selling "Islands" of Automation and Integration to Consumers, moderator: Rich Holtz; Working with Home Builders to Pre-Sell Systems, Dan McLeister, Professional Builder; The Ultimate Integrator- A Winning Company Profile for the 21st Century, moderator: Myra Moore, Digital Technology Consulting. All six Home Systems workshops will be held at the Las Vegas Hilton.

"To address the new technologies that continue to impact both business and lifestyle trends, we are providing targeted, in-depth programs to help retailers understand and capitalize on these industry upgrades," commented Robbi Lycett, Winter CES group show director. In addition to Retail Management and Home Systems, workshop categories include the Internet, technology, home theater, multimedia, mobile electronics, specialty audio, SOHO and a variety of general interest subjects.

Internet sessions will include Are Consumers Really Ready For A PC In The Living Room?, moderated by Ed Baig of Business Week. A panel of representatives from Acer America Corporation, Compaq, Gateway, IBM and Thomson Consumer Electronics will discuss the PC/TV and it's impact at retail. Additional Internet sessions will include: The Internet Box - What Is The Potential?, moderator: Mike Antonucci, San Jose Mercury News; The Broadcast-Internet Connection, moderator: Marge Costello of CE Online News and Broadcast Engineering Advances In Audio And Video On The Internet, moderator: Eric Lundquist, PC Week and Beyond The Modem - New Ways to Bring The Internet Home, moderator: Roger Lanctot, Computer Retail Week. All five Internet sessions will be held at the Las Vegas Convention Center.

Five Technology seminars will also be a part of the workshop program. Moderated by Bruce Schwartz of USA Today, New Products, New Technologies: What Do Consumers Really Want? will examine consumer attitudes and buying trends and offer concepts that retailers can use to formulate marketing strategies. Panelists will include representatives from Thomson Consumer Electronics, Hachette Filipacchi Magazines and Fairfield Research. Other Technology seminars will include DVD Basics - A Primer, moderator: Marc Horowitz, Video Magazine; DBS: So Many Systems, So Little Space, moderator: Steve Blum, The Carmel Group; DVD - Is It The Digital Messiah?, moderator: Paul Gluckman, Audio Week and New Video Displays, moderator: Mike Heiss, Video Systems. All five Technology seminars will be held at the Las Vegas Convention Center.

A series of four Home Theater seminars will not only advise retailers on how they can communicate the features of home theater, but also how they can benefit from working with interior designers to ensure that the latest technology fits in with the latest decors. Home Theater Upgrades: Selling 5.1 Channel Audio, moderated by Bob Ankosko of Stereo Review, will feature representatives from Bjorn's Audio.Video.Car Stereo, Yamaha and Dolby Laboratories Licensing Corporation offering retail strategies for communicating the benefits of 5.1-channel audio to the public and give tips on in-store demonstration techniques. The Home Theater track will also offer How to Sell Home Theater, moderator: Brent Butterworth, Home Theater Technology; So, You Want To Be A Custom Installer, moderator: Maureen Jenson, Audio Video Interiors and A New Customer: Working With Interior Designers, moderator: Linda Lentz, Home Magazine. All Home Theater seminars will be held at the Las Vegas Hilton.

Five Multimedia seminars are designed to inform retailers about what's currently available in the multimedia world and what's on tap for the future. The Future of Gaming -- The Next Wave, moderated by David English of Computer Entertainment News, will explore what kind of games will be sold in the next few years and what effect that the growing popularity of online gaming will have on retail. Panelists include representatives from America Online, Mpath, Kesmai Corporation, and Engage. Other seminars in the multimedia track include: The Game Buzz -- How to Pick the Winners, moderator: David Elrich, E-Town; Interactive TV: What Will it Take to Make it a Reality, moderator: Martin Levine, Digital Technology Report; Multimedia: New Profit Opportunities, moderator: Russell Glitman, PC World and Multimedia at Retail -- What Does the Future Hold?; moderator, Jim McCullaugh, Multimedia Merchandising. All Multimedia seminars will be held at the Las Vegas Convention Center.

With topics ranging from aftermarket OEM installations to PCS and cellular, nine Mobile Electronics sessions are scheduled to take place at the Sands Expo and Convention Center. Find out if cellular and PCS can co-exist in the same marketplace at Cellular and PCS: Friend or Foe?. Moderated by Joe Palenchar of TWICE Magazine, select PCS carriers will outline how they're putting cellular on the defensive, cellular carriers will outline how they've alternately responded to the competition and embraced it and an independent analyst will separate the hype from the reality. Panelists will include Pacific Bell Mobile Services, Western Wireless and MTA/EMCI.

Other Mobile workshops will include Paging's Role in the New Wireless World?, moderator: Cindy Stevens, Wireless Business and Technology; Car Navigation-What are the Options?, moderator: Mike Mettler, Car Stereo Review; Computerizing Your Mobile Electronics Business, presenter: Robert Fields, Sound Solutions; How to Troubleshoot During an Installation, moderator: Ed Kuehner, Luzerne Community College; How the Aftermarket Can Complement OEM Installation, presenters: Ron Freeman, AAMP of America and Rob Putman, SoundGate Interface; What's New and Profitable in Auto Security, moderator: Barnet Fagel, Car Sound Magazine; How to Sell Beyond the Basic System, moderator: Terry Miller, Car Audio and Electronics; Promoting Installation as a Profit Center, moderator: Michelle Guido, Mobile Electronics Retailer.

By attending the five Specialty Audio seminars that will accompany the numerous exhibitors featured at the Alexis Park Hotel, retailers can not only find out about the industry upgrades in specialty audio, but also how to attract new customers to the different products that are already available. Understanding the Music Applications of DVD, moderated by John Eargle of JME Consulting Corporation, will explain the technology behind multi-channel audio and discuss whether or not there is a future for it without a picture to accompany it. Panelists include representatives from Meridian, TMH Corporation and Sony. Other seminars in the specialty audio track include: The Art of Listening -- How to Conduct Listening Tests and Demos, moderator: Robert Harley, Stereophile Magazine; High End Accessories -- Do They Make a Difference?, moderator: Art Dudley, Listener Magazine; How To Introduce New People to Specialty Audio, moderator: Kerry Moyer, CEMA and The Changing World of Tube Electronics, moderator: Edward Foster, Diversified Science Labs.

Three seminars make up the small office-home office track which will inform retailers of the new products that allow executives to efficiently and effectively perform their jobs while they work from home or travel. Mobile Communications -- Keeping the Corporate Road Warrior in Touch, moderated by Jack Killion of Wireless for the Corporate User, will feature representatives from Radiomail, Siemens Wireless and Apple discussing the software, hardware and service options available to keep executives in the information loop even when they are away from their offices. Other seminars in the SOHO track are: Peripheral Vision - New Consumer Products, New Profit Opportunities, moderator: Bill Howard, PC Magazine and Executives Who Work at Home and On the Road: A New Retail Customer, moderator: Chris Sandlund, Home Office Computing. All SOHO panels will take place in the Las Vegas Convention Center.

Other hot topics in the consumer electronics industry ranging from marketing to special audiences to the latest in digital photography will also be covered as part of the conference program. Retailing the Year 2000, moderated by David Bolotsky of Goldman Sachs, features representatives from JVC, RC Wiley and Best Buy, who will examine what the retail landscape will look like in the year 2000, what role the Internet will play and what retailers will have to do to be competitive. Other seminars include: Digital Photography - CE or Photo Store, moderator: Jonathan Takiff, Philadelphia Daily News; Reaching Out to the Baby Boomers, moderator: Gary Arlen, Arlen Communications; Reaching Out to Generation X , moderator: Steve DeLuca, Details Magazine and Can You Afford to Only Sell Hardware?, moderator: Mike McGann, Dealerscope.

In addition, two conference programs will feature government and industry representatives, the first is HDTV: What is next? Gary Shapiro, CEMA president will moderate this panel. New York Times political writer and author of the recently released book, Defining Vision, the Battle for the Future of Television, Joel Brinkley will participate in this panel along with an FCC representative, a broadcaster and a Capitol Hill representative.

New Technologies: The Government's role is the second panel which will fetaure key Congressional and Administrative officials discussing this topic.

The Show will open with the keynote address by Michael Bloomberg, President and Founder of Bloomberg Financial Markets. This renowned company monitors and analyzes the activities of the consumer electronics industry and provides multimedia, analytical and news services to financial and news organizations throughout the world.
