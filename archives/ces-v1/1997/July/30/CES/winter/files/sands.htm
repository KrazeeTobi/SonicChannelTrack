Sands Expo Showcases the Hottest New Products in Mobile, Wireless, Cellular, Paging at 1997 Winter CES®

Features 650 exhibitors, Special Technology Pavilions,
and Innovations '97

NEW YORK, NEW YORK, November 21, 1996. Representing more than 500,000 square feet of exhibit space, conferences, publications, associations, Innovations `97 and specially-dedicated technology pavilions at the 1997 Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®), the Sands Expo and Convention Center is a major trade show by itself.

Instead, the use of the Sands comprehensive complex has grown over 150 percent from the 1996 show into one of the largest parts of the Winter CES with more than 650 exhibitors. It is a full service facility complete with all exhibitors and attendee services that are expected at CES. The Las Vegas Convention Center, the traditional CES anchor facility with audio, video, computer, multimedia and more, while the Las Vegas Hilton is host to home theater and home automation, and the Alexis Park is home to specialty and high-end audio. Meanwhile, the Sands also is host to the prestigious Innovations '97, the annual showcase of design, engineering and software excellence in consumer electronics in addition to special technology pavilions.

Housing the mammoth 160,000 square feet of mobile electronics (the world's largest trade-only exposition for the mobile electronics industry) the Sands upper level also is home to wireless communications as well as cellular, paging, telephone products and Innovations `97. In addition to publications, trade associations, the Sands' main level contains personal electronics, watches, health care products, adult software, retail resource, and serves as the all-important Winter CES overflow area for audio and video hardware and accessories as companies continue to join up.

Jonathan Thompson, Vice President of the Consumer Electronic Shows, said "the outstanding growth of the mobile electronics category told us that we needed to move it into a dedicated area surrounded by other logical categories like cellular, telephony, paging and wireless products. This new grouping of categories is an incredible draw by itself."

Key exhibitors in the cellular, paging, wireless and telephony areas at the Sands include such industry giants as Motorola, Ericsson, Samsung Cellular, Nokia, PageMart, PageNet, and Pacific Bell Mobile Services as well as the hot caller ID manufacturers CIDCO and Colonial Data. This portion of the show represents some of the largest growth areas in terms of exhibitors and exhibit space for the Show. In addition, this is the largest tradeshow for wireless, telephony and cellular retail products in the U.S.

The Sands Expo and Convention Center also is an even greater drawing card by hosting such show stoppers as the first-time Winter CES Emerging Technologies Pavilion, and the Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) Pavilion, in addition to Innovations '97.

Located on the lower level, the Emerging Technologies Pavilion will focus on new and exciting technologies from small manufacturers. Eligible companies such as Centerpin and Heartronics that produce less than $2 million, have never had a CES booth larger than the basic 10' x 10', and have fewer than 15 employees. As part of the pavilion, these companies will gain exposure from the nearly 80,000 retailers investors, financiers, venture capitalists, and media.

The ITS Pavilion, represented by such companies as Etak, Navigation Technologies, Rockwell, Siemens, and ITS America will feature products designed to improve passenger and freight efficiency and safety along surface transportation modes. Products will include in-vehicle navigation systems, traveler information systems, safety devices, Mayday and collision notification systems, and vehicle radar systems. The internet area featuring Audiotek is a showcase of the latest state-of-the-art Internet and world wide web related and support products.

Innovations `97 includes those "best-in-class" products that are winners in the Innovations `97 Design & Engineering Honors Program. These select product winners are honored as the most prestigious product and design advancements at the 1997 Winter CES.
