First-Time Exhibitors Choose Winter CES
for Positive and Upbeat Reasons

Over 22 Percent New Companies Exhibit

ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA., December 10, 1996. With more than 1,800 companies exhibiting at the 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®), some 400 companies representing over 22 percent are first-time exhibitors at the world's largest annual trade event showcasing consumer electronic products. The 1997 Winter CES is being held in Las Vegas from January 9th through the 12th.

All exhibitors including the new exhibiting companies are utilizing the nearly one million square feet of exhibit space at the Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC), the Las Vegas Hilton, the Sands Expo and Convention Center, and the Alexis Park Hotel.

New companies join up and exhibit at the Winter CES every year with 1997 experiencing a very positive show of force from the newest WCES rookies. As convergence becomes the focal point of a variety of technologies and as over 3,000 journalists descend on the show to cover the latest and hottest technologies improving consumers' lives, the new exhibitors will both contribute and benefit in the success of the 1997 WCES.

One of the new companies exhibiting is using the WCES to launch its new business unit. Heather Tippett, trade show coordinator for Samsung Telecommunications America, Inc. (a part of Samsung Electronics), Dallas, Texas, said, "It's an extremely good show for us to launch our brand new business unit focusing on digital wireless telecommunications products. We're the new kid on the block and the WCES is ideal for us to gain all the exposure we can with the right distributors, the best end-users as well as the media. We want the industry to know that Samsung is a major player in manufacturing and marketing quality cellular and paging products, and offers telecom capabilities in addition to its traditional consumer electronics expertise."

Another first-time company is Kensington Microwave, Ltd., of San Mateo, Calif. Kathy Kane, vice president of marketing, said "Since Kensington is a leader in global computer accessories, Winter CES is a major international showcase which allows us to have `one-stop-shopping' with our consumer electronics channel of distribution. In addition, it is the ideal stage for introducing our new line of power protection products with our customers as well as the tremendous number of media covering the show. Also, we wish to reinforce our global leadership with the trade and the business press since all the major media outlets cover the show in-depth."

Bob Marsocci, senior manager of communications for DirecTV of Los Angeles, said, "The Winter CES is the foremost international show for consumer electronics in the U.S. and the world and it's the perfect venue for the fastest growing consumer electronics category. The stature of the attending audience of WCES is critical for us. It includes many of our manufacturing partners such as Hitachi, Magnavox, Panasonic, RCA, Sony, Toshiba, Uniden, and others. Furthermore, we want to continue getting the retailers excited about DSS so that we can continue selling and marketing our direct broadcast satellite television service into and throughout 1997."

"CES offers QUALCOM the proper venue to showcase our progressive family of CDMA digital phones to dealers from around the country and the world," said Jeffrey K. Belk, director of marketing, QUALCOMM Subscriber Products Business Unit.
