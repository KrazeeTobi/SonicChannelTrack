Convergence Plays Key Reality Role at 1997 Winter CES

DVD, TV/PC, Emerging Technologies, Internet and ITS Pavilions
are Supporting Cast Players In Over 1 Million Net Sq. Ft.

NEW YORK, NEW YORK, November 21, 1996. Convergence plays the key "reality" role for the 1997 International Consumer Electronics Show® (CES®) surrounded by a supporting cast including red hot internet technologies, dramatic digital products, and pulsating special-interest pavilions.

Convergence, once a mundane technical term meaning "to come together" today best describes the coming together of the full spectrum of technologies with television companies offering interactive, digital Internet capabilities and computer companies offering broadcast and entertainment venues.

Robbi Lycett, Winter CES Show Director, said "This long-anticipated, exciting convergence phenomenon is here for buyers and retailers to be a part of when the doors open at 10:00 a.m. on January 9, 1997 officially kicking off the 1997 CES. In addition to celebrating 30 years of CES shows, the '97 winter show which runs through January 12, 1997, promises to offer the most interesting slate of technological, marketing and corporate alliances, partnerships, and other coming together progressive relationships which are nothing short of terrific for the consumer electronics industry."

Converging technologies include DVD, DSS, TV/PC, PCS, multi-media, interactive cable, smart phones, pagers, wireless products and more -- all with related ancillary products, accessories and support.

The cast for the other surrounding characters for the 1997 Winter CES include the debut of the Emerging Technologies Pavilion, the Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) Pavilion, Innovations '97 and a supporting cast of hundreds led by top names such Casio, Denon, Thomson, Philips, Pioneer, Sharp, Sony, and Zenith. "These pavilions represent the hottest emerging technologies coming into the marketplace today. For buyers and retailers to see all of this together in one place at one time and have the occasion of being part of this first-hand, is an opportunity that simply should not be missed," said Lycett.

Nearly one million square feet of exhibit space has been assigned to more than 1500 companies and manufacturers from all over the world. Featuring over 400 new exhibitors, the CES will be located at four major locations: the Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC), the adjoining Las Vegas Hilton, the Sands Expo and Convention Center, and the Alexis Park Hotel.

Traditional audio and video products will converge with internet and satellite products in the Las Vegas Convention Center (LVCC). All major DBS manufacturers will exhibit their latest products in this facility. Companies such as IBM, Microsoft, Philips, Sony, Thomson, and Zenith will be demonstrating their latest hot TV/PC and internet products. Companies like JVC and Casio will be showcasing their advanced digital camcorders. The latest technologies and products for the small office/home office (SoHo) will be showcased by such companies as Brother International, Franklin Electronic Publishing, Hewlett-Packard USA, Royal Consumer Business Products, and Smith Corona.

In the nearby Las Vegas Hilton will be the headquarters for all home theater and home systems manufacturers and marketers. MB Quart, as part of its Domain loudspeaker line, is unveiling a new exciting high-powered subwoofer with an interchangeable flip-up top for ease of operation by the consumer in home theater scenarios. Also, Lutron Electronics is using the show to introduce its super new Spacer lighting remote control and dimmer, making home entertainment retreats or office environments as custom and automatic as desired. Other companies unveiling equally exciting new products at the CES include THX, MTX, Creston, Phast, and X-10 (USA). "As products for home entertainment and other home functions become popular these companies' integrated home system products are becoming a necessity for the home builder and installer," said Lycett.

The Sands Expo and Convention Center will be playing an ever-greater role at the '97 CES with about 650 exhibitors in over 500,000 square feet of exhibit space including the Emerging Technologies Pavilion (lower level), mobile electronics, cellular, paging, wireless, telephone, personal electronics, and more. In addition, Twentieth Century Fox is exhibiting in the Sands capitalizing on and showing growing support for the DVD introductions expected in 1997. CIDCO, with its red hot caller ID products; AudioVox and Motorola with small, digital telephones, and Tandy and others with "Family Radio" are all part of the powerful assembly of exciting products being exhibited at the Sands. Innovations '97, the prestigious honors showcase of the best-of-the-best products exhibited, is judged by a panel as the ultimate in design, engineering and software excellence.

The Sands also is host to the Intelligent Transportation System (ITS) Pavilion. The ITS, represented by such companies as Etak, Navigation Technologies, Rockwell, Siemens, and ITS America, will feature products designed to improve passenger and freight efficiency and safety along surface transportation modes. Products will include in-vehicle navigation systems, traveler information systems, safety devices, Mayday and collision notification systems, and vehicle radar systems. The internet area featuring such companies as Audiotek is a showcase of the latest state-of-the-art Internet and world wide web related and support products. As part of its larger presence at the CES, the Sands also is serving as the overall overflow arena for all product categories as more and more companies catch the fever and sign on.

The Alexis Park Hotel will be the main home for specialty audio for companies like AudioQuest, Krell Industries, Martin-Logan, Mobile Fidelity, Thiel, and Vandersteen displaying state-of-the-art audio advancements.

A highly-focused comprehensive conference program of over 50 sessions to be held at all four main locations consist of eight tracks: new technology, Internet, multimedia, SoHo, specialty audio, mobile electronics, home theater and general sessions. Two six-session accredited tracks on home systems and retail management also are available for $125 or $30 a session.

The keynote speaker for the 1997 International Winter CES is Michael Bloomberg, President and founder of Bloomberg Financial Markets. Bloomberg will present the prestigious keynote address during the opening session on Thursday, January 9 from 8:30 to 10:00 a.m. along with Bob Borchardt, president of Recoton and CEMA Chairman and Gary Shapiro, president of the Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association (CEMA). Bloomberg Financial Markets monitors and analyzes the activities of the consumer electronics industry, and provides multimedia, analytical and news services to financial and news organizations throughout the world.
