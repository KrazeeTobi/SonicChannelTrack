Retail Management Seminars Among Highlights
of 1997 Winter CES Conference Program


ARLINGTON, VIRGINIA, October 1, 1996. Retailers attending the 1997 International Winter Consumer Electronics Showr in Las Vegas, January 9-12, 1997 will have the opportunity to participate in an extensive conference program designed to offer solutions and suggestions for increasing sales and profitability. The program, which consists of tracks on retail management and home systems, is part of a new paid concept being instituted at Winter CESr for the first time. Each track is comprised of six sessions.

In addition, a comprehensive schedule of topics covering new technology, the Internet, convergence products, multimedia, SOHO, mobile electronics, specialty audio, home theater and general interest subjects will also be presented. These sessions will be open to all badged attendees at no charge.

The paid Retail Management Track will offer Merchandising 101: Store Design/Display, moderator: Lee Carpenter of Design Forum; Merchandising 102: Promotions/Planning/ Marketing, moderator: Steve Bryant, The Bryant Group; How To Successfully Expand - Lessons From Other Industries, moderator: Elly Valas, NARDA; Customer Returns: A "New" Look at an "Old" Problem, presenter: William Stuart, Stuart & Associates; Eliminating Costly Habits: Zero Percent Financing and Price Competition, presenter: Jules Steinberg, Jules Steinberg Associates; and The Internet and Retail - What Every Dealer Should Know, Moderator: Ron Goldberg, E-Town. The cost to attend all six sessions is $125 or $30 for individual sessions.

The Home Systems Track, which has the same cost structure as the retail management program, includes Sources of Recurring Revenue, moderator: Ken Moyes, Electronic House; Consumer Trends In Home Systems, moderator: Susan Whitehurst, SDM Home Systems Installer; Home Systems Standards - Reality Check, moderator: Tricia Parks, Parks Associates; An Incremental Approach: Selling "Islands" of Automation and Integration to Consumers, moderator: Richard Holtz, RRH Associates; Working with Home Builders to Pre-Sell Systems, Dan McLeister, Professional Builder Magazine; The Ultimate Integrator- A Winning Company Profile for the 21st Century, moderator: Mike'l Dornhecker, Custom Home Electronics.

The Show will open with the keynote address by Michael Bloomberg, president and founder of Bloomberg Financial Markets. This renowned company monitors and analyzes the activities of the consumer electronics industry and provides multimedia, analytical and news services to financial and news organizations throughout the world.

"Training is an important role for a trade association," said Gary Shapiro, president of Consumer Electronics Manufacturers Association, sponsor of CES. "Smarter, trained retailers lead to greater customer satisfaction, increased profitability and improved industry sales. Formal training at the CES gives retailers another reason to be in Las Vegas in January."
