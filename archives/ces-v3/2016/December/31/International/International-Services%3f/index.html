    Maximize Your CES Experience

    We provide an array of pre-event and on-site services to help maximize your CES experience — and get your business done.
     

    International Business Center (IBC)

    LVCC, South Hall Connector, Room S224
    Sands, Level 2, Lobby

    The International Business Center (IBC) offers services exclusively for our international attendees and exhibitors. Services include

    • Private meeting rooms, reserved on a first-come, first-served basis
    • Laptop hookups and computers with internet access so that you can stay connected to your office back home
    • A lounge area where you can meet casually with colleagues or rest after a busy day on the show floor

    Multilingual interpreters are available in the LVCC, South Hall Connector, Room S224 to assist you as needed.

    International Matchmaking Reception

    Thursday, Jan. 5, 2017
    6-7:30 PM
    LVCC, South Hall Connector, Room S222

    All international attendees, exhibitors and media are invited to the International Matchmaking Reception. Don’t miss this opportunity to network and enjoy light refreshments after an exciting day on the CES show floor. This event is open to all international registrants and their invited guests.

    Interpreters

    Interpreters are available to assist you in several languages, including Spanish, French, Chinese, Japanese and Korean. They are available in the IBC located in the LVCC, South Hall Connector, Room S224 and to assist with registration in the following areas:

    • LVCC, CES Central Plaza, Main Registration Area
    • Westgate Ballroom, Attendee/Exhibitor Registration Area  

    In the registration areas, look for the counter titled, “International Language Interpretation” if you need assistance.

    CES has contracted with two interpretation vendors to assist you at the show. Learn more:

    • TransPerfect

    • Judy Venn and Associates, Inc.

    International Buyer Program

    CES is a proud participant in the International Buyer Program, sponsored by the U.S. Department of Commerce, International Trade Administration.

    Through the U.S. Commercial Service’s network of offices in the U.S. Embassies and Consulates worldwide, the IBP recruits pre-screened foreign buyer delegations and brings them to selected trade shows in the U.S. to connect and facilitate business between U.S. companies and international buyers.

    International Trade Specialists from the U.S. Department of Commerce are available on-site at CES 2017. Visit the International Business Center, (LVCC, South Hall Connector, Room S224) to meet with experts who can provide matchmaking assistance and export trade counseling to international attendees and exhibitors.
