CTA’s Research Summit gives executives from diverse industries timely intelligence on trends and technologies at CES 2018. The summit takes a data-centric approach, offering insights on inflection points through quantitative and qualitative data. Get what you need to understand what’s happening in growing and adjacent technology segments within the industry.

Top Three Topics:

  • Trends, retails, and driverless vehicles
  • Shifting business models and cyber-security  
  • Cognitive technology and virtual Reality (VR)

9-10 AM January 8

LVCC, North Hall, N257

CES 2018 Trends to Watch

Before CES opens its doors, you can preview impactful trends, next big things and disruptive innovations that will redefine our industry in 2018. See how new technologies will occupy the landscape of consumer technology before you hit the show floor.


Steve Koenig
Sr. Director of Industry Analysis,
Consumer Technology Association

10:15-11:15 AM January 8

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Now Generation | New Attitudes Driving Success

The Now Generation (age 15-25) are immersed in a world of social media and new technologies. Often new technologies emerge “just because we could build it,” but success depends on understanding this group’s evolving expectations.

Speaker
Karen Ramspacher
SVP Innovation and Insights,
GfK MRI

Speaker
Kathy Sheehan
Executive Vice President ,
GfK

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Self-Driving Exploration- Past, Present and Future

NASA’s first launch didn’t reach the moon. In the quest for self-driving cars automakers are aiming for the autonomous moonshot.  Competition is accelerating between major car brands, tech companies and Tesla. What advanced tech is coming to our roads and when?

Moderator
Karl Brauer
Executive Publisher,
Autotrader and Kelley Blue Book

Speaker
Michael Ableson
VP Global Strategy,
General Motors

Speaker

2:15-3:15 PM January 8

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Consumer Preferences for Personalized Content Viewing

With endless ways to consume content, consumers are developing preferences for live, streamed, online and ad-supported content. Understand watching behaviors and consumers’ tolerance for different ad characteristics. Identify the tradeoffs consumers are willing to make for the most personalized content.

Moderator
John Penney
EVP, Strategic Partnerships,
Twentieth Century Fox

Speaker
Eric Berger
CDO at Sony Pictures Television Networks and GM of Crackle,

Speaker

3:30-4:30 PM January 8

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Who's Responsible for Driverless Car Liabilities and Risks?

Self-driving vehicles and autonomous driving features are transforming the way we travel. It’s also shifting the landscape of risk, liability, data privacy and cybersecurity. If a collision happens, who is responsible?

Moderator
Salen Churi
Professor and Director of the Innovation Clinic,
University of Chicago Law School

Speaker
Robert W. Peterson
Professor Emeritus,

LVCC, North Hall, N262

Understanding AR and VR Content Challenges

The augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) content market stands to impact many industries. Understand consumer sentiment towards virtual reality headsets and awareness of AR/VR technology as well as the demand and challenges for content delivery.

Moderator
Meenakshi Ramasubramanian
Senior Research Analyst,
Consumer Technology Association

Speaker
Kam Diba
President,
Reverge VR

Speaker

LVCC, North Hall, N262

The State of Cognitive in the Enterprise

Executives have highly diverse views when it comes to artificial intelligence. This session will look at how leaders are investing in and applying cognitive technologies along with their successes, challenges, expectations and aspirations in the next three years and beyond.

Paul Sallomi
Global Technology, Media and Entertainment and Technology Industry Leader,

LVCC, North Hall, N262

2017 Consumer Tech Holiday Season and Beyond

The NPD Group reveals the highlights, lowlights and everything in between from the 2017 holiday season based on industry retail sales reports from this critical period.

Speaker
Stephen Baker

The NPD Group

1:30-2 PM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N262

The Evolving Retail Landscape: Consolidation and Curation

As retail adapts to product changes and vice versa, relevancy is all about getting the right products out there. Checkout Tracking℠ will spotlight the proliferation of online brands - particularly in growth segments and emerging categories with challenging in-store placement.

Speaker
Liz Cutting
Executive Director, Checkout Tracking,
The NPD Group

LVCC, North Hall, N262

How Technology-based Business Models Drive Market Valuations

Traditional industry boundaries have blurred and industry convergence drives Wall Street to measure value differently. Value has shifted to businesses that use tech-based business models. Tomorrow’s successful companies will blend physical business models with virtual models to win the Great Race.
