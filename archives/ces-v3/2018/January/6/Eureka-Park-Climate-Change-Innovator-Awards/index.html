  • CES Eureka Park Climate Change Innovators

Apply to be recognized for your product’s or technology’s potential to reduce greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions.

Application deadline: Nov. 16, 2017. 

Applications for the Climate Change Innovator Awards Contest are now CLOSED.

As the next generation of entrepreneurs and startups, Eureka Park exhibitors are uniquely positioned to combat climate change by using cutting-edge innovations to reduce the environmental impact of traditional business practices.

The CES Eureka Park Climate Change Innovators program highlights the leadership and innovation of consumer technology startups in combating climate change.

CES 2018 Innovators

  • Conservation Labs: Sands, Hall G, Booth #52335
  • K&A Wireless, LLC: Sands, Hall G, Booth #50322
  • Lightyear: Sands, Hall G, Booth #51522
  • Qarnot Computing: Sands, Hall G, Booth #50722
  • Sharpell Technologies: Sands, Hall G, Booth #51083
  • Wavelite: Sands, Hall G, Booth #5187

Recognition for Innovators

Winners will

  • Receive a plaque, identifying their company as a CES Eureka Park Climate Change Innovator, to display in their Eureka Park exhibit space at CES 2018.
  • Be recognized during CES 2018 Sustainability Day on Jan. 11, 2018.

The Awards Process

Successful applications with products or technologies that demonstrate the potential for significant reductions in GHG emissions will be notified by CTA no later than Dec. 20, 2017.

A committee of climate change and industry experts will review all award applications and select winners based on submissions that show the most GHG reduction potential. 

See the official contest rules. 

CES is owned and produced by the Consumer Technology Association, which provides the ultimate platform for technology leaders to connect, collaborate, and propel consumer technology forward.
