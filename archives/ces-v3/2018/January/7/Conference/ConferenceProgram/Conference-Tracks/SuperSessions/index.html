CES SuperSessions feature emerging industry trends and senior executives sharing experiences and predictions for the year ahead.

11:30 AM-12:30 PM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Insights from the FTC

FTC Acting Chairman Maureen Ohlhausen sits down with CTA President and CEO Gary Shapiro for a candid conversation about the challenges facing the agency and navigating the constantly evolving technological landscape.

Moderator
Gary Shapiro
President and CEO,
CTA

Speaker
Maureen K. Ohlhausen
Acting Chairman,
Federal Trade Commission

1-2 PM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N257

The Future of News

The news business is a vibrant (and often controversial) part of our global community. From politics and culture, to entertainment and sports, technology has dramatically impacted how people receive and consume news. Innovators and industry leaders discuss how news will evolve as technology continues to disrupt the Fourth Estate.

Moderator
Brent Weinstein
Head of Digital Media,
United Talent Agency

Speaker
Yashar Ali
Journalist,
Independent

Speaker

3-4 PM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Next Big Thing: The Invisible Doctor

Join CNET and a panel of luminaries rethinking the way we manage our health. Futurists discuss the fantastical future while realists are using today’s technology to improve lives. We’ll explore how the digital health industry will take signals from all parts of our lives and integrate them into personalized care.

Moderator
Brian Cooley
Editor-at-Large,
CNET

Moderator

Speaker

10-11 AM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Self-Driving Cars: The Future of Personal Transportation

As self-driving cars become reality, the entire automotive ecosystem will need to evolve. This session, featuring leaders from government and automotive, technology and insurance companies, will focus on how driverless cars and other disruptions in personal transportation will impact the consumer.

Moderator
Lindsey Turrentine
Vice President and Editor-in-Chief,
CNET

Speaker

Speaker

11:30 AM-12:30 PM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Top Trends Shaping Global Innovation

Economic growth and prosperity are most dynamic where policies and political climates foster innovation and the entrepreneurial spirit. Learn where companies find innovation-friendly environments and whether governments compete to attract innovators or protect those they have. Global business leaders and policy operators weigh in on the digital economy.

Moderator
Alan Murray
Chief Content Officer,
Time Inc.

Speaker
Pascal Cagni
Chairman of the Board and French Ambassador for International Investment,
Business France

Speaker
Bridget Karlin

IBM Global Technology Services

Speaker

Startup Envoy of the Netherlands,
StartupDelta

2-3 PM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N257

Turning AI into New Ways of Doing Business

Professionals are putting AI to work turning our most valuable resource – data - into new ways of doing business. With AI we are no longer wrestling with data but putting it to work to decide with confidence, accelerate research and discovery and engage with customers - on their terms. Discover how companies in various industries around the world are putting IBM Watson - an AI platform that embeds in workflows, learns from small data sets and allows you to own your insights – to work. Uncover the outcomes others are seeing and learn the many ways you can put AI to work today – and in the future.

Speaker
Donna N. Dillenberger
IBM Fellow, Enterprise Solutions ,
IBM

Speaker
Bridget Karlin
CTO & VP, GTS Technology, Innovation & Automation,
IBM Global Technology Services

Speaker
Beth Smith

IBM
