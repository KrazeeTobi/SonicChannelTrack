Augmented reality is coming to the masses. How will it be integrated into everyday life? Tech is driving innovation and streamlining delivery of new devices. Find out what TV will look like in 2020. Get the update on tech, travel and hospitality, startups to watch in 2018 and retail technology.

Top Three Topics:

  • Augmented reality coming to consumers’ everyday lives. Virtual reality, its potential, and why companies are investing
  • Customer experience and innovation
  • Startup spotlight. Who to watch in 2018

9-10 AM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N261

All Eyes on Augmented Reality

Companies are making ambitious efforts to bring augmented reality to the masses. Hear from an AR visionary on what is on the horizon for this game-changing technology and how AR will soon become part of the consumer’s everyday life.

10:15-11:15 AM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N261

Tapping Virtual Reality’s Real Potential

Entertainment, education, healthcare, tourism, and automotive and even space travel are just a few of the industries tapping into VR’s potential. Discover how this technology is being used, what’s in the near future and why companies are investing in VR.

11:30 AM-12:30 PM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N261

What Will TV Look Like in 2020?

4K Ultra High Definition TV sales are expected to exceed $15.6 million in 2017. How will manufacturers and the content community continue to innovate and drive sales? One new development is Next Gen TV (ATSC 3.0) that enables both broadcast and broadband.

Speaker
Kevin Cahill
Head of Product Marketing,
Hisense

1-2 PM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N261

Travel and Hospitality Transforming with Technology

Travelers are tech-savvy and they’re demanding more technology on the road. The travel industry has stepped up its game with mobile check-in and keyless entry to full personalized experiences and collaborative robots. The travel/hospitality tech revolution is here. 

2:15-3:15 PM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N261

Every Company is a Tech Company

Retailers continue to embrace technology and innovations to meet customer needs. New industries are using tech to drive innovation. This session showcases tech transformations and  valuable insights on creating seamless and engaging customer experiences while leveraging new devices and platforms.

Speaker
Joe Corr
Executive Creative Technology Director,
CP+B

Speaker
Brooks Tobey
Head of Technology Strategy & Business Operations,
Turner

3:30-4:30 PM January 10

LVCC, North Hall, N261

Startup Spotlight: Companies to Watch in 2018

Who are the most intriguing tech newcomers in 2018? Tech journalists and VCs highlight the most innovative startups at CES 2018’s Eureka Park. Learn how these unique products and technologies were conceived and why they may be trendsetters in 2018.

Presenter
Gregory Borchardt
Co-Founder & Managing Partner,
Caerus Ventures

11:30 AM-12:30 PM January 11

LVCC, North Hall, N261

A Preview of the IEEE ICCE's Most Interesting Technologies

The Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers leading scientists and engineers are hosting the IEEE International Conference on Consumer Electronics, a CES partner event. These experts will review disruptive and emerging technologies in video, audio, computing, gaming, communications and more.

1-2 PM January 11

LVCC, North Hall, N261

Gaming: 3 Rising Trends

The gaming industry has seen explosive growth in recent years and shows no signs of slowing down.  Experts will examine how the intersection of gaming and technology has given rise to some of today’s hottest trends, including wearables, mobile and cloud gaming.  The impact of online influencers will also be addressed.
