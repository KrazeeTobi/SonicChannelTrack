The future is here. Connect2Car aftermarket and investors have shown confidence in funding millions in innovative connected car technology companies.  Although some have OEM relationships, these innovative companies are providing the next generation of aftermarket mobile electronics and playing a large role in the success of V2V, V2I and V2X.

Top Three Topics:

  • Connected vehicle data and the next big thing for smart cities
  • The future of mobility and autonomy
  • Cybersecurity in a connected world

9-10 AM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N256

Future of Connected Smart Cities

Cities continue to grow and face more challenges with traffic congestion, citizen safety and infrastructure planning. How can cities and automotive organizations find the middle ground of leveraging this data for their citizens and for their driver?

10:15-11:15 AM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N256

Connected Vehicles the Next Leap

Over the past several years, many automakers and their suppliers have made a major leap forward effectively becoming software companies. This panel provides insights from automakers, suppliers, and aftermarket companies who are all teaming to make this second leap successful.

11:30 AM-12:30 PM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N256

The Future of Mobility and Autonomy

Autonomous vehicles release the driver and passengers to engage in activities that previously were not possible due to driver distraction and other safety concerns. This mobility panel explores views from forward-looking organizations in automotive industry to answer these interesting questions.

1:30-3:15 PM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N256

Connect2Car: Next-Gen Automobility

The Connect2Car aftermarket and investors have funded millions in connected car technology companies. These innovative companies are providing the next generation of aftermarket mobile electronics and playing a large role in the success of V2V, V2I and V2X.

3:30-4:30 PM January 9

LVCC, North Hall, N256

Cybersecurity in a Connected World

Where will cybersecurity be applied to autonomous vehicles?  Vehicles, smart cities, mobility services, telecommunications/telematics devices and public transportation are all threat targets. How will innovative technologies and strategies protect wireless transmissions, software, firmware, semiconductors, storage devices, the cloud and you?
