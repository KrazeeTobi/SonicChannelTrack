    12.20.2016

    Media Access to the Exhibit Floor

    Media are not permitted on the CES exhibit floor during exhibitor set-up, prior to show opening at 10 AM on Jan. 5, unless accompanied by a CTA staff person with CES Communications team approval. If you are approached by a member of the media, you must get approval from someone on the CES Communications team before you escort anyone onto the exhibit floor, and you must remain with them for the duration of their time on the exhibit floor.

    Once the show opens, media must be escorted by a CES exhibitor for the duration of any media event or briefing outside of official exhibit hours (before or after the exhibit floor opens on Thursday, Jan. 5, through Sunday, Jan. 8) or have a special broadcast wristband given out by the CES broadcast team for media doing early morning news segments. Please note that broadcast media have access with their wristbands 24/7.

    Exhibitors holding media events before or after hours must complete an “After Hours Event” Form to obtain approval from the CES Communications and CES Operations teams. Once approved, CES Security will have an approved list at exhibit entrances, and exhibitors must escort media on and off the exhibit floor. Contact Megan Sanko for the “After Hours Event” Form.

    CTA's Digital Answer Man, Jim Barry, and other CTA spokespeople will be highlighting new products introduced at CES 2017 in broadcast media interviews on-site at CES.

    CNET at CES

    CNET returns to CES 2017 as the leading source for real-time show coverage.     Learn More About CTA’s Communications Committee

    The Consumer Technology Association (CTA)™ Communications Committee provides guidance to CTA’s communications team on the design and implementation of the association’s public relations, media outreach, consumer education, public affairs communications, industry promotion and related activities.
